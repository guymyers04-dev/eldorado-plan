Butler County Government: Commission Structure & Coordination

Commission Board Overview

The Butler County Commission consists of 3 elected commissioners representing 5 geographic districts across the county. Commissioners meet on the 1st and 2nd Tuesdays of each month to oversee county operations, budget, and regional development priorities.

  • Commission Structure: 3 elected commissioners (not 5)
  • Meeting Schedule: 1st & 2nd Tuesday, County Building in El Dorado
  • Public Comment: Open comment period at each meeting
  • County Seat: El Dorado (District 4)
  • Chair Position: Rotates annually among commissioners

💼 Current Commission Leadership (2023–2026)

  • Chairman: Darren Jackson (District 4, El Dorado)
  • Vice Chair: Jeff Masterson (District 1, Andover)
  • Member: Additional commissioners from Districts 2, 3, 5
  • Audit Date: April 1, 2026 (FY 2025 Complete)
  • County Funds Managed: $55M+ (Year-end 2025)
  • Key Priorities: Infrastructure, economic development, regional coordination

Why County Commission Matters for Vision 2040

El Dorado's success depends on county-level cooperation and coordination:

  • Joint Economic Development Agreements: City-county partnerships on growth and infrastructure
  • Regional Water Authority: El Dorado Lake water rights are jointly managed with county
  • Permit & Zoning: County planning director coordinates with city planning on regional projects
  • Infrastructure Cost-Sharing: County roads, bridges, and rural water systems interconnect with city infrastructure
  • Federal Grant Access: EDA Economic Development District designation requires county participation

City-County Coordination for Vision 2040

Required Approvals & Agreements

  • Joint Economic Development Agreement (JEDA): City-county partnership on tax incentives, infrastructure cost-sharing, and coordinated marketing
  • Regional Water Authority Coordination: County seat role in managing El Dorado Lake water supply for industrial development
  • Comprehensive Plan Alignment: Vision 2040 phases aligned with Butler County 2025–2028 Comprehensive Plan
  • EDA District Designation: County participation required for federal Economic Development District grants
  • Regional Infrastructure Funding: Joint applications for USDA Rural Development, EDA Public Works, and state transportation grants

🤝 Key MOUs and Coordination Points

  • Monthly Briefings: City Manager + Commission Chair coordination on Vision 2040 progress
  • Quarterly Planning Updates: Joint city-county planning committee to review phase progress and identify resource gaps
  • Annual State-of-the-Partnership: Formal review of coordination mechanisms and shared service opportunities
  • Regional Economic Summit (Annual): County-wide stakeholder meeting with all communities, employers, and government partners
