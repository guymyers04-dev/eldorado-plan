## TALKING POINTS FOR OBJECTIONS

**"$60–110K is a lot. Can we start smaller?"**

*Response:* Yes, we could defer the consultant ($25–50K) or delay certain workstreams. However, the $60K minimum covers the coordinator role (mandatory for managing 5 parallel teams) + materials. Without the coordinator, city staff burnout risk is HIGH. Recommend: Approve full $60–110K; phase funding if needed ($60K Jul, $20K Aug if needed).

---

**"How do we know the roadmaps will actually solve our problems?"**

*Response:* The roadmaps are diagnostic tools. We're not committing to solutions yet — we're gathering facts:
- City Engineer's permitting roadmap shows us *where* decisions happen and *when*
- Workforce pipeline shows us *if* USD 490 + BCC can scale capacity
- Housing roadmap shows us *which* financing mechanisms are viable

Once we have these facts, city council can make informed decisions about resource allocation + incentive authority. This is exactly what we need *before* developers or investors arrive.

---

**"Why do we need a dedicated coordinator? Can ED Director handle it?"**

*Response:* ED Director is already managing economic development day-to-day + leading supply chain research. Adding 5 parallel research projects + managing 8+ stakeholder interviews would require a second person. The coordinator does:
- Schedule & track 30+ research tasks
- Consolidate findings into 1–2 page summaries (weekly)
- Manage stakeholder interview prep + follow-up
- Update master calendar + risk register
- Communicate progress to city leadership + public

*This is a full-time role.* If we want to avoid delays, coordinator is essential.

---

**"What if we don't get a developer inquiry? Is Tranche 2 wasted?"**

*Response:* No. Developer inquiry is one success indicator, but not the only one:
- **Housing Finance:** LIHTC + CDBG funding is available *independent* of specific developers. Securing these sources de-risks our housing strategy.
- **Workforce:** Training programs benefit community *regardless* of FDI investment. USD 490 capacity = improved local career options.
- **Permitting:** Clear permitting timeline benefits *any* developer or business. This is foundational infrastructure.
- **Master Calendar:** Creates accountability + transparency for city council + community. Builds trust.

Tranche 2 is about making Vision 2040 *executable* — not dependent on any single deal.
