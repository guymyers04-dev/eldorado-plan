## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Vision 2040 targets 300 mixed-income housing units by 2032 (150 affordable, 150 market-rate). This roadmap secures financing through a mix of:

- **LIHTC** (Low-Income Housing Tax Credits): 40% of affordable units
- **CDBG** (Community Development Block Grants): 20% of gaps
- **KHRB** (Kansas Housing Resources Board): 15% of gaps
- **City Gap Funding**: 10% (local capital + bonds)
- **Private Financing**: 40% market-rate units (conventional mortgages)
- **Foundations**: 5% (place-based development grants)

**Cost Assumptions:**
- Affordable unit: $150–180K (land + hard + soft costs)
- Market-rate unit: $200–250K
- Average blended cost: $170–200K per unit
- Total project cost: $50–60M

**Financing Mix:**
- Equity (LIHTC credits + grants): $20–25M
- Debt (mortgages + bonds): $25–30M
- Private investor equity: $8–10M
- **Total: $53–65M**

**Result:** 300 units, $600–800/month affordability (30% of income for HUD income targets), zero displacement, strong community benefit.

## SECTION 3: FUNDING SOURCES & ANNUAL ALLOCATION

### Federal Funding (40–50% of total project cost)

**CDBG: Community Development Block Grants**
- **Annual Allocation:** $300–400K/year to El Dorado (HUD formula grant)
- **Use:** Gap funding for affordable housing (fill difference between development cost + resident affordable rent)
- **Application:** Annual application to HUD (competitive ranking within state)
- **Timeline:** Application Jan, awards announced Sep
- **Typical Award:** 3-year rollover (~$900K–1.2M over 3 years)

**LIHTC: Low-Income Housing Tax Credits (Federal)**
- **Kansas Annual Allocation:** ~$20M statewide
- **El Dorado Expected:** $2–3M/year (based on project competitiveness + local match)
- **Structure:** Syndicated to national investors (who buy credits for cash to equity the development)
- **Price per Credit:** $0.80–1.00 (market rate varies with demand)
- **Example:** $2.5M credits × $0.95 = $2.375M equity into project

**HOME: Home Investment Partnerships**
- **Annual Allocation:** $150–200K/year to El Dorado (HUD formula)
- **Use:** Acquisition, construction, or rehabilitation of affordable homes
- **Flexibility:** Can be combined with LIHTC or used standalone for rental or homeownership

**Total Federal Funding (Annual):** $450–600K + LIHTC syndication equity

### State Funding (20–30% of total project cost)

**KHRB: Kansas Housing Resources Board Mortgages**
- **Program:** Permanent affordability mortgages (50-year term, 0% interest)
- **Amount:** $1–2M per project (typically 20–30% of project cost)
- **Qualification:** LIHTC project or other state-approved development
- **Permanence:** 50-year affordability covenant required; KHRB holds mortgage in perpetuity

**Workforce Housing Initiative (State Program)**
- **Target:** Families 60–80% AMI (wage earners in Vision 2040 jobs)
- **Funding:** $500K/year from Kansas housing trust fund
- **Format:** Down payment assistance + favorable mortgage terms
- **Integration:** Pairs with conventional financing for market-rate units

**Community Development Fund (Kansas Dept of Commerce)**
- **Amount:** $200–400K one-time for rural housing projects
- **Qualification:** Contributes to economic development (job creation, population growth)
- **Application:** Included in Vision 2040 package to Commerce Dept

**Total State Funding (Annual):** $300–500K + KHRB mortgages

### Local Funding (15–25% of total project cost)

**Tax Increment Financing (TIF) District**
- **Structure:** City designates development site as TIF district
- **Mechanism:** Property tax revenue growth diverted to affordable housing fund for 20 years
- **Estimated Capacity:** $200–300K/year (assuming 300-unit buildout)
- **Timeline:** TIF district designation by city council (simple approval)
- **Risk:** Low (city commits no upfront capital; incremental tax revenue only)

**General Fund Appropriation (Gap Funding)**
- **Amount:** $100–200K/year (optional, if TIF insufficient)
- **Source:** City general fund or bonding authority
- **Decision:** City council votes on affordability commitment annually
- **Permanence:** Multi-year commitment (3–5 year pledge recommended)

**Community Land Trust (CLT) Model**
- **Structure:** Nonprofit holds land in perpetuity, retains control of affordability
- **Local Contribution:** City donates or sells land at below-market ($100K one-time per project)
- **Benefit:** Reduces development cost, ensures affordability permanence, strengthens community control
- **Partner:** EarthWorks (Kansas CLT nonprofit) or create local CLT

**Total Local Funding (Annual):** $300–500K

### Philanthropic Funding (5–10% of total project cost)

**Foundation Grants (Place-Based Development)**
- **Target Foundations:**
  - **Community Development Trust** — $100–250K per project
  - **Local Initiatives Support Corporation (LISC)** — $50–150K
  - **MacArthur Foundation** — $200–500K (competitive, national program)
  - **Kate B. Reynolds Charitable Trust** — $100–300K (rural development focus)

- **Application Process:** 6–12 month cycle (LOI → full proposal → review → award)
- **Total Potential:** $500K–1.5M over 3 years (across 3–4 projects)

**Local Charitable Contributions:**
- **El Dorado Community Foundation** (if established) — $50–100K endowment goal
- **AMAZONE corporate foundation** — $100K pledge (corporate citizenship)
- **Bank community reinvestment** — $200K+ (CRA credits for participation)

**Total Philanthropic Funding (Annual):** $150–300K

### Private Financing (35–50% of total project cost)

**Conventional Mortgages (Market-Rate Units)**
- **Lender:** Local banks, regional mortgage companies, Fannie Mae
- **Amount:** $5–8M per 100-unit project (40% of development cost)
- **Terms:** 25–30 year amortization, market interest rates (5–6%)
- **Qualification:** Project must demonstrate lease-up feasibility + strong sponsor

**Private Equity / Developer Equity**
- **Amount:** 8–15% of project cost
- **Source:** Developer + nonprofit partner + community investors
- **Syndication:** LIHTC credits syndicated to national investors (generates ~$2.5M per $2.5M credits awarded)

**Total Private Financing (Annual):** $2–4M (depends on project pipeline)

## SECTION 5: AFFORDABILITY PRESERVATION MECHANISMS

### Deed Restrictions & Covenants

**30-Year Affordability Covenant (Affordable Units)**
- Property deed restricted to affordable rents for 30 years
- If building sells, affordability transfers to new owner (runs with land)
- Violation = city has right to force sale at affordable rent
- Recorded at county; visible to any future buyer

**Community Land Trust (Long-Term Permanence)**
- Nonprofit holds land in perpetuity (typically)
- Residents lease land year-to-year; own building only
- Gives affordability protection beyond 30 years
- Builds community wealth (residents build equity; land appreciation captured in CLT endowment)

### Homeownership Preservation (if applicable)

**Down Payment Assistance Programs**
- First-time homebuyers in Vision 2040 jobs: $10K–25K down payment help
- Funded by state + KHRB + city
- Affordable mortgage products (fixed-rate, 5–6% interest)
- Homeownership counseling mandatory

## SECTION 8: RISK MITIGATION & CONTINGENCY PLANS

### Risk 1: LIHTC Application Rejected
**Trigger:** Application scores low, doesn't receive award  
**Impact:** 1–2 year delay in affordable housing development  
**Mitigation:**
- Strengthen application (hire expert consultant, $10–15K fee)
- Build broader funding coalition (multiple foundation + nonprofit partners reduce reliance on single LIHTC award)
- Fallback: Use 100% CDBG + KHRB mortgage model (slower, but works without LIHTC)

---

### Risk 2: Market-Rate Mortgage Rates Spike
**Trigger:** Rates rise to 7–8% (making mortgages unaffordable)  
**Impact:** Market-rate projects become unviable (occupancy < 95% = negative cash flow)  
**Mitigation:**
- Mix market-rate + affordable (crosssubsidy: affordable units strengthen project underwriting)
- Ground-lease model (city retains ownership, reduces developer equity requirement)
- Extended lease-up timeline (30 days → 60 days, reduces pressure on pricing)

---

### Risk 3: Insufficient Local Matching Funds
**Trigger:** City budget shortfalls, TIF underperforms  
**Impact:** $200–400K gap in project funding  
**Mitigation:**
- Pre-secure foundation grants (lock in $300K from MacArthur, LISC before TIF becomes uncertain)
- Community land trust model (CLT accepts land donation in-kind = matches city equity requirement)
- Employer housing fund (AMAZONE + manufacturers contribute $100K/project, seen as workforce benefit)

---

### Risk 4: Residents Unable to Sustain Affordability (too poor)
**Trigger:** Project rents set at 40% AMI ($525/month), but residents' incomes drop  
**Impact:** Collections issues, vacancy, service demands increase  
**Mitigation:**
- Build social services into project (job coaching, financial counseling, health services)
- Supportive services coordinator (onsite staff, funded by grants)
- Partnership with USD 490 + Butler CC (residents get job training, wage increases)
