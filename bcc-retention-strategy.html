Butler Community College Retention Strategy

Anchoring the Region's Largest Educational Institution

Retention and expansion strategy for BCC: workforce partnerships, housing for faculty/staff/students, enrollment growth, economic integration

Why BCC Matters: El Dorado's Anchor Institution

The Anchor

Butler Community College is El Dorado's largest employer and most significant regional economic institution:

  • Enrollment: 5,000+ students (regional draw)
  • Employees: 400+ faculty/staff
  • Annual budget: $60–80M
  • Economic impact: $150M+ direct/indirect annually
  • Competitive threat: Losing enrollment to KU Edwards, K-State Salina, Wichita State

📊 Market Position

  • Regional competition: 5–6 community colleges + state universities within 2-hour drive
  • Enrollment trend: Flat to declining over past 5 years
  • Threat: Loss of 200–300 students annually = $8–12M budget impact
  • Opportunity: Strategic partnership with El Dorado can differentiate BCC as "place-based learning"

The Retention Strategy: Three Pillars

  • Workforce partnerships: Align BCC training with El Dorado growth industries (precision ag, advanced manufacturing, health services)
  • Campus integration: Faculty/staff housing near campus; student housing districts in walkable neighborhoods
  • Enrollment growth: Market BCC as gateway to El Dorado's economic opportunities

15-Year Retention Plan: Workforce + Housing + Integration

Phase 1: Partnership Foundation (Years 1–3)

  • Workforce alignment: Develop certificates/degree programs aligned with precision ag, batteries, biotech, aerospace industries
  • Housing initiative: Develop 50–75 faculty/staff housing units (townhomes, condos)
  • Campus amenities: Student commons, gathering spaces, walkable retail/dining
  • Enrollment target: +300–400 students (growth initiatives, targeted recruitment)
  • Investment: BCC $5–8M (academic); City $8–12M (housing + infrastructure)

Phase 2: Expansion (Years 4–7)

  • Sector-specific training: Precision ag tech center, advanced manufacturing lab, health services training expansion
  • Student housing: Develop 100–150 student apartment units (near campus)
  • Campus expansion: New academic buildings, expanded library, student center
  • Enrollment target: 5,500–6,000 students (stabilized, +500 from baseline)
  • Investment: BCC $15–20M (facilities); City $15–20M (housing + utilities)

Phase 3: Integration (Years 8–15)

  • Full integration: BCC seen as central to El Dorado's economic identity
  • Applied learning: Students interning/working with precision ag companies, manufacturers, health systems
  • Community partnerships: BCC partnering on city projects, events, economic development
  • Enrollment stabilized: 5,500+ students; market share growth vs. competing institutions
  • Investment: $45–60M (cumulative)

Economic Impact: Multiplier Effects & Retention Value

Direct Value (of staying in El Dorado)

  • Employment retention: Keeping BCC = 400 jobs; facility investments = $150M+ regional economic impact
  • Housing demand: Faculty/staff + student housing = 250–300 new residential units
  • Student spending: 5,500+ students spending locally on food, retail, entertainment = $8–12M annually
  • Regional attraction: BCC growth = talent pipeline for local employers

Cost of Losing BCC

  • Job loss: 400 direct + 600 indirect = 1,000 jobs lost
  • Economic impact: $200–250M annual economic impact loss
  • Population decline: 2,000–3,000 residents relocated
  • Tax base erosion: $8–12M annual tax revenue loss

Implementation: BCC, City, Workforce Partners

🎓 Butler Community College

  • Adopt "Place-Based Learning" strategic plan
  • Develop workforce partnerships with precision ag, manufacturing, health sectors
  • Plan faculty housing near campus; student housing partnerships
  • Targeted enrollment growth initiatives

🏛️ City of El Dorado & Butler County

  • Develop faculty/staff housing (public-private partnership)
  • Utilities: water/sewer for student housing, campus expansion
  • Street improvements around campus; pedestrian/bike infrastructure
  • Support workforce training programs with city employers

💼 Workforce Partners (Employers, Industry Associations)

  • Work with BCC to develop certificates/degrees in high-demand fields
  • Provide internship/job placement opportunities for BCC students
  • Support campus development (donations, sponsorships)
