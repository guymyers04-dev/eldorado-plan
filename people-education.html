Education 2040 Target

Vision 2040 Education Goals

By 2040, USD 490 will be a recognized regional leader in career-pathway education, STEM integration, and workforce alignment with regional economic development goals.

Academic Excellence

Increase proficiency in math and reading to state average by 2028 through professional development, instructional coaching, and technology integration.

Target: +15 percentage points proficiency

STEM & Tech Integration

All students (K–12) engage with STEM curriculum. HS students have access to coding, robotics, and maker education aligned with technology jobs.

Target: 80% student enrollment in STEM electives

Career Pathways

Every HS student completes a career pathway aligned with regional industries (manufacturing, healthcare, technology, agriculture).

Target: 100% pathway completion
Education Strategy

Key Initiatives

1. Advanced Placement & Dual Enrollment

Partner with Butler CC to offer dual enrollment courses, allowing HS juniors/seniors to earn college credit. Support AP exam fees for low-income students.

2. Manufacturing Pathway Program

Expanded CNC, welding, and industrial maintenance courses. Partner with local manufacturers for internships and apprenticeships (junior/senior year).

3. Technology & Coding Lab

New STEM lab with laptops, robotics kits, 3D printers, and coding curriculum. Accessible to all students K–12, with after-school STEM clubs.

4. Teacher Professional Development

Annual summer PD on STEM instruction, project-based learning, and technology integration. Partner with regional universities for training grants.

5. College & Career Counseling

Dedicated college/career counselor. All students complete career assessment (grade 8) to guide pathway selection and course planning.

6. Employer Partnerships

Formalized partnerships with regional employers for guest speakers, site visits, job shadowing, and internship placement. Advisory board guides curriculum.
