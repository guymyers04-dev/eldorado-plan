## KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY

### El Dorado's Competitive Position
El Dorado is the **smallest city by population (12,500)** but has the **strongest unique structural advantages** among regional competitors. Despite population disadvantage, El Dorado can win advanced manufacturing, data center, aerospace supply, and lake tourism sectors.

### Top Three Differentiators
1. **WATER RIGHTS (Unique):** 23M gallons/day owned capacity; only Kansas city with USACE water contract
   - Strategic Value: Data center recruitment; manufacturing water security
   - Competitive Advantage: UNREPLICABLE; 50+ year security

2. **I-35 DIRECT ACCESS (Unique):** Exits 71/76 on primary corridor; direct logistics to Wichita/Kansas City/Dallas
   - Strategic Value: Supply chain efficiency; BNSF Intermodal Park connection (30 mi)
   - Competitive Advantage: Non-bypass location; only peer with direct I-35 node

3. **STATE PARK ASSET (Unique):** #1 most-visited Kansas state park (1M+ annual visitors)
   - Strategic Value: Tourism revenue; quality-of-life draw; young professional attraction
   - Competitive Advantage: UNDERDEVELOPED; huge upside with lake activation

### Secondary Advantages
- FTZ #28 enrollment (customs duty savings $200K–$500K annually for manufacturers)
- AMAZONE FDI proof-of-concept (German company, operational 2025, expanding)
- Business-friendly regulations (fastest permits among peers; no license fees)
- Safety (2nd lowest crime rate among competitors)
- Affordability (lowest cost of living; lowest home prices)

### Critical Gaps Requiring Action
1. **HOUSING AFFORDABILITY CRISIS:** Price-to-income ratio 3.46x (should be 2.5–3.0x)
   - Action: Accelerate North El Dorado Phase 1 (100 units by Q4 2026)
   
2. **WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT LAG:** Youth retention only 38% vs. Salina 55%
   - Action: Match McPherson's programs; expand Butler CC technical pathways
   
3. **SHOVEL-READY SITE INVENTORY:** 450 acres adequate but behind Dodge City (700+), Salina (1,200+)
   - Action: Expand industrial site inventory; prioritize North El Dorado mega-site development
   
4. **BROADBAND PARITY WINDOW:** BEAD funding will equalize broadband by 2027–2028
   - Action: Close data center LOI by Q2 2027 before competitive advantage erodes

## THE 24-MONTH CRITICAL WINDOW (2026–2028)

**Why This Window Matters:**
- BEAD broadband will reach parity by 2027–2028 (data center competitive advantage erodes)
- AMAZONE momentum can attract follow-on FDI, but only if leveraged aggressively
- Housing supply must increase to support FDI job creation (North El Dorado Phase 1 critical)
- Lake activation is first-mover advantage; must execute Phase 1 by end of 2026

**Year 1 (2026): Foundation**
- [ ] Data center task force + recruitment deck
- [ ] Formalize 72-hour permit guarantee
- [ ] North El Dorado housing Phase 1 (100 units, Q4 2026)
- [ ] Lake activation Phase 1 (1–2 anchor tenants)
- [ ] Butler CC technical programs expansion

**Year 2 (2027): Execution**
- [ ] Data center feasibility study + LOI
- [ ] AMAZONE Phase 2 expansion (50+ jobs)
- [ ] North El Dorado Phase 2 (100 units)
- [ ] Lake activation Phase 2 (3–4 tenants operational)
- [ ] 2nd major FDI closed

**Year 3 (2028): Consolidation**
- [ ] Data center under construction OR operational
- [ ] North El Dorado complete (300+ units)
- [ ] Regional Water Authority generating revenue
- [ ] Lake tourism mature ($10M+ annual impact)
- [ ] 3rd major FDI in pipeline

## QUICK FACT LOOKUPS

**What's El Dorado's biggest competitive advantage?**  
→ Owned water rights (23M gal/day) + I-35 direct access. Unique combination that no competitor can replicate.

**Which competitor is the biggest threat?**  
→ McPherson (local labor market competition) for workforce; Salina (for aerospace supply sector). Dodge City not a threat (different sector).

**What's the critical timeline?**  
→ 24 months (2026–2028). Must close data center LOI by Q2 2027 before BEAD broadband parity erodes advantage.

**What's El Dorado's biggest weakness?**  
→ Housing affordability crisis (3.46x price-to-income ratio). Must deliver 300+ units by 2028 to support FDI job creation.

**How much does a data center deal generate in economic impact?**  
→ ~$300M capital investment + 500+ jobs + $50M+ annual tax revenue over 10 years = 30–50x ROI on recruitment investment.

**What's the lake tourism opportunity?**  
→ #1 Kansas state park (1M+ annual visitors) currently underdeveloped. Potential $75M–$150M annual tourist spending; $5M–$10M annual city tax revenue by 2030.

**What's the water export opportunity?**  
→ Regional water authority (50-year revenue stream): $2M–$5M annually by 2030, depending on demand from Butler County municipalities.
