Industry Target Markets

Priority Industries for El Dorado

El Dorado's industrial strategy targets four sectors where the city has existing assets, regional positioning, and workforce alignment: advanced manufacturing, logistics, food processing, and energy-related services. These sectors generate high-wage jobs, support diverse skill levels, and leverage El Dorado's geographic advantages.

Advanced Manufacturing

Precision equipment, aerospace components, and custom fabrication. El Dorado's existing manufacturing base and skilled workforce position it for specialty manufacturing recruitment targeting companies seeking Kansas locations outside Wichita's congested metro.

Est. 200–300 jobs · Avg. $52,000/year

Regional Logistics Hub

Distribution, warehousing, and fleet services leveraging US-77 positioning between Wichita and Kansas City. El Dorado can capture overflow from I-35 congestion and serve as a secondary distribution point for regional e-commerce and manufacturing supply chains.

Est. 250–400 jobs · Avg. $44,000/year

Food Processing & Agriculture Tech

Value-added processing, specialty foods, and ag-tech services. Kansas is a top agricultural state; El Dorado can serve as a secondary processing and innovation hub for food safety, packaging, and supply-chain management.

Est. 150–200 jobs · Avg. $45,000/year

Energy Services & Infrastructure

Oil & gas services, renewable energy installations, and utility management. El Dorado's oil heritage and regional energy infrastructure position it for energy sector service companies and specialized equipment manufacturing.

Est. 100–150 jobs · Avg. $58,000/year

El Dorado's Competitive Advantages

📍

Strategic Location

Between Wichita (40 min) and Kansas City (90 min). US-77 corridor offers lower congestion, lower costs, and better regional connectivity than I-35.

💰

Lower Operating Costs

Real estate, labor, and utilities 20–30% below Wichita. Tax incentives through TIF district and state programs available for qualified projects.

👷

Available Workforce

500+ available workers in region. Butler CC offers CNC, welding, and maintenance training. USD 490 has vocational alignment with manufacturing.

🏭

Industrial Land

1,131-acre mega-site zoned for manufacturing. Infrastructure-ready parcels with water, sewer, and electric access. Shovel-ready sites available.

🤝

Community Support

City Commission actively supports economic development. El Dorado Inc. provides marketing, site selection, and incentive management.

🌟

Quality of Life

Affordable housing, excellent schools, Lake recreation, and vibrant downtown. Attracts managers and families seeking alternatives to big-city costs.

Industry Go-To-Market

Economic Development Recruitment Strategy

El Dorado's recruitment strategy focuses on mid-market companies (50–250 employees) seeking secondary locations outside major metros. El Dorado Inc. targets specific companies in target industries through direct outreach, trade shows, and brokerage networks.

Primary Recruitment Channels

  • Site Selection Consultants: White box, commercial real estate brokers recommending Kansas secondary markets to corporate clients
  • Industry Trade Shows: NTMEC (National Association of Manufacturers), logistics conventions, food processing summits
  • Direct Outreach: Companies identified through market research, contacted by El Dorado Inc. director with proposal
  • Chamber Networks: Butler County Chamber, Kansas Chamber of Commerce partnerships with Wichita firms seeking satellite locations

Workforce Development Alignment

El Dorado's recruitment strategy must be paired with workforce training and education. Vision 2040 aligns USD 490, Butler CC, and local employers to ensure the pipeline of skilled workers matches recruitment targets.

Butler Community College — Workforce Training

Offers CNC machining, welding, HVAC, and industrial maintenance certifications. Vision 2040 supports expansion of manufacturing-focused programs and equipment to match recruitment targets.

  • • CNC Operator Certification (6–12 weeks)
  • • Welding & Fabrication (12–24 weeks)
  • • Maintenance Technician Program (12 months)

USD 490 — Career Pathways

High school vocational program aligned with manufacturing careers. Vision 2040 supports equipment updates and partnerships with local manufacturers for apprenticeships and internships.

  • • Manufacturing technology pathway (grades 9–12)
  • • Employer internships (junior/senior year)
  • • Dual-enrollment with Butler CC
