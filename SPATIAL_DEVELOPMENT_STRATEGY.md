# El Dorado Spatial Development Strategy
## Comprehensive Area-by-Area Revamp Plans
**Date:** June 29, 2026  
**Status:** Strategic Framework (Detailed Implementation Planning)

---

## Executive Overview

This document outlines strategic development and revitalization plans for 10 critical zones in El Dorado, with specific focus on:
- **East El Dorado connectivity** (gateway function)
- **Lake-side development** (tourism/recreation expansion)
- **North expansion** (residential + marina hub)
- **Butler CC retention** (youth development anchor)
- **I-35 interchanges** (gateway positioning)
- **South downtown** (urgent revitalization)
- **SE El Dorado/Prospect** (viability preservation)
- **HW 77 corridor** (PRIMARY FOCUS: logistics/regional positioning)
- **HW 54 corridor** (INTENSIVE FOCUS: retail/commercial gateway)
- **Lake activity zones** (current vs. proposed positioning)

**Key Strategic Principles:**
- HW 77 is the PRIMARY development corridor (logistics, regional hub)
- HW 54 gets EXTENSIVE focus (retail, commerce, visitor gateway)
- West El Dorado is PRESERVED (no major development pressure)
- SE El Dorado/Prospect must REMAIN VIABLE (cultural/community anchor)
- South downtown REQUIRES urgent investment
- North expansion targets residential + recreation (mega-opportunity)

## ZONE 1: EAST EL DORADO CONNECTIVITY CORRIDOR

### Current Situation
- Geographic barrier: Eastern neighborhoods disconnected from central business district
- Limited direct routes to downtown, schools, recreational facilities
- Infrastructure gaps: Missing bridge connections, limited transit corridors
- Economic impact: Residential isolation reduces property values, school integration

### Strategic Vision (5-Year Plan)
**Transform East El Dorado into integrated residential-commercial zone with strong downtown connectivity**

### Phase 1: Infrastructure Connectivity (Months 1-12, $2.5M)

#### A. East-West Bridge/Corridor Development
**Project:** Establish minimum 2 new cross-town arterials connecting east neighborhoods to Central Avenue

**Specifications:**
- Route 1: New bridge over creek/waterway from E. Main to Central Ave
  - 4-lane divided roadway (future 6-lane capacity)
  - Pedestrian/bike paths on both sides
  - Estimated cost: $1.2M
  - Timeline: 8-10 months
  
- Route 2: Enhanced connection via existing road (widening + intersection improvements)
  - Widen to 4 lanes (from current 2-3)
  - Traffic signal optimization
  - Estimated cost: $800K
  - Timeline: 6-8 months

**Expected Impact:**
- 40-50% reduction in travel time (E. neighborhoods → downtown)
- Increased property values (10-15% premium for connected areas)
- 2,500+ more daily CBD trips from east side

#### B. Transit Network Integration
**Project:** Extend bus/shuttle service to east neighborhoods

**Specifications:**
- Route E1: Circulator route (east neighborhoods → downtown → lake)
  - Frequency: Every 20-30 minutes during peak hours
  - Service hours: 7 AM - 7 PM weekdays, 10 AM - 6 PM weekends
  - Cost: $120K annually
  
- Route E2: School shuttle (east neighborhoods → USD 490 campuses)
  - Morning: 7:30 AM - 8:30 AM drop-off
  - Afternoon: 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM pick-up
  - Cost: $180K annually

**Expected Impact:**
- 1,200-1,800 transit trips/week
- Reduced family vehicle dependency
- Improved school accessibility

### Phase 2: Mixed-Use Development Anchors (Months 6-24, $4.8M)

#### Anchor 1: East Gateway Commercial District (20 acres)
**Location:** Gateway to east neighborhoods from downtown

**Development Mix:**
- Neighborhood commercial (15K sq ft): Grocery, pharmacy, dry cleaning, services
- Dining/café (8K sq ft): Coffee shop, casual restaurant, food hall
- Professional offices (12K sq ft): Medical, dental, professional services
- Parking: 150 spaces (multistoried to preserve land)

**Investment:** $2.4M
**Timeline:** 18 months
**Expected Revenue:** $480K annually (leased space)
**Jobs Created:** 65 FTE

#### Anchor 2: East Residential Mixed-Use (8 acres)
**Development Type:** Walkable residential village

**Specifications:**
- Townhomes: 24 units (avg. $165K-180K)
- Garden apartments: 32 units (1-2 BR, avg. $850/month rent)
- Ground-floor retail: 6 units (2K-3K sq ft each)
- Public plaza: 1-acre gathering space

**Investment:** $2.4M (residential + retail)
**Timeline:** 20 months
**Expected Population:** 180-220 residents
**Jobs Created:** 28 FTE (retail)

### Phase 3: Social Infrastructure (Months 12-36, $1.2M)

**Projects:**
- East neighborhood park/plaza: 2 acres with playground, pavilion, fitness area
- Community center satellite location: 5K sq ft (youth programs, fitness classes)

### Success Metrics (5-Year Target)
- East residential population increase: +15% (from 2,200 to 2,530)
