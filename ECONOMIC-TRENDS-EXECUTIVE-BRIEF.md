# El Dorado Vision 2040: Economic Trends Executive Brief
## Key Findings & Strategic Implications (2024-2030)

**Prepared:** June 2026 | **Audience:** City Leadership, Investors, Development Partners

---

## HEADLINE: EL DORADO AT INFLECTION POINT

El Dorado, Kansas faces a **4-year window of exceptional opportunity (2026-2030)** driven by converging regional economic tailwinds. Conservative execution yields population +1,800 (1.8% CAGR); optimized Vision 2040 execution captures population +3,000 (3.2% CAGR), positioning the city for sustainable 3.0%+ annual growth through 2040.

**Critical Decision Point:** H2 2026. Investments in water infrastructure, broadband-ready industrial parks, and housing acceleration determine whether El Dorado captures front-end of Wichita MSA spillover growth (2027-2030) or cedes opportunity to competing jurisdictions.

---

## KEY INSIGHT #1: KANSAS MANUFACTURING RENAISSANCE CREATES WATER DEMAND

### The Data
- **Panasonic De Soto $4B EV battery plant** = largest private economic development project in Kansas history (2024-2025 construction)
- **2024 Reshoring announcements:** 244,000 jobs; 35% from semiconductor/electronics; 31% from electrical equipment (EV batteries, solar)
- **National investment:** $1.7 trillion by end 2024 (up from $933B in 2023)
- **Kansas water advantage:** Industrial-scale treatment capacity at El Dorado; strategic location for water-intensive manufacturing

### For El Dorado
**Water = Competitive Moat.** Reshoring companies explicitly prioritize locations with industrial-scale water access. Panasonic's choice of De Soto (30 miles from El Dorado) validates regional demand.

**Action:** Accelerate B.A.S.E. grant projects; secure 2-3 water-using industrial commitments by 2030. Market proposition: "Megawatt-scale water for high-growth industries."

**Upside:** Each major water-using tenant = 100-300 jobs, $10-50M annual payroll, $50-150M capital investment.

---

## KEY INSIGHT #2: HOUSING SHORTAGE = POPULATION MAGNETIC FORCE

### The Data
- **Rural Kansas housing need:** 4,000 new homes/year to meet growth + replacement
- **State deficit:** 55,000+ units short for low-income residents
- **Price growth:** Kansas median home +$21K in 2 years ($248K→$269K); starter homes scarce
- **Millennial driver:** #1 relocation reason = housing affordability (16.8% cite this)
- **El Dorado advantage:** $217K median (Butler Co.) vs. $269K state average = $52K cheaper

### For El Dorado
**El Dorado is a Goldilocks target:** Too expensive for extreme rural (Lincoln Co. $92K), too expensive for metro (Johnson Co. $560K), but perfect for affordability-seeking millennials and remote workers.

**Action:** Target 75 new housing units 2026-2030; partner Habitat for Humanity + community land trusts; aim for $180-200K entry-level home price point.

**Upside:** Each 20 new housing units = ~60 new residents; each new resident = ~$25-30K annual spending (schools, retail, services), generating 0.3-0.4 jobs per resident.

**Multiplier:** 75 new units → 225 new residents → $5.6-6.8M annual spending → 70-90 new jobs.

---

## KEY INSIGHT #3: BROADBAND DEPLOYMENT = REMOTE WORK UNLOCKED

### The Data
- **BEAD approval:** $166.6M federal allocation; Kansas construction begins H2 2026
- **Coverage:** 26,673 eligible households/businesses statewide
- **Technology:** Fiber (30%), fixed wireless (67%), LEO satellite (3%)
- **Lincoln County case study:** Remote worker program generated 330+ applications for 5-person target (66x oversubscription)

### For El Dorado
**Remote workers = High-income, low-impact residents.** Median remote worker income $80-120K (vs. $55.8K local median); require minimal municipal services; prefer small-town quality of life.

**Action:** Launch targeted remote worker recruitment program (H1 2027, post-BEAD deployment); highlight affordability + broadband + small-town amenities.

**Target:** 150-250 remote worker household relocations by 2030; each generating $20-30K annual local spending (30-40% above local average due to higher incomes).

**Upside:** 200 remote workers × $25K spending = $5M annual stimulus; minimal infrastructure burden; net positive fiscal impact.

---

## KEY INSIGHT #4: WICHITA MSA SPILLOVER = COMMUTE-SHED EXPANSION

### The Data
- **Wichita MSA projected growth:** 663K (2025) → 750K (2040) = 17.1% increase (~6K new residents/year)
- **Growth concentration:** Western edges of Butler County (proximity to Wichita)
- **K-96 improvements:** $260M investment; 9-mile corridor upgrade; handles 50K→100K daily vehicles by 2050
- **Commute tolerance:** Typical suburb dweller accepts 30-45 min commute; El Dorado = 30 min to downtown Wichita

### For El Dorado
**El Dorado is the "first ring" growth opportunity for Wichita MSA overflow.**  As suburban cores (Andover, Derby, Maize) saturate, second-ring commuter communities become attractive. El Dorado offers:
- 30-min commute (acceptable)
- $217K home price (vs. $330K+ suburban average)
- Small-town quality of life
- K-96 improvements reducing friction

**Action:** Position as "affordable Wichita suburb with character"; target commuters ages 30-45 with household income $80-150K.

**Upside:** Capture 1.5-2% of Wichita MSA annual net new residents (6K × 1.75% = 105 new residents/year); 4-year accumulation = 420 new residents (conservative); 600+ with optimized marketing.

---

## KEY INSIGHT #5: SKILLS GAP = EDUCATION PARTNERSHIP OPPORTUNITY

### The Data
- **Kansas skills gap:** Labor shortage in building trades; foundational literacy/math deficits limiting apprenticeship access
- **Entry-level hiring slowdown:** Young job seekers face "low-hire, low-fire" market
- **Butler Community College advantage:** 6,841 students; #2 largest CC in Kansas
- **FRAME grant program:** $2M available for CC residential training + construction

### For El Dorado
**Education partnerships = Competitive advantage in workforce recruitment.** Vision 2040 can position El Dorado as training hub for regional skilled trades.

**Action:** Develop Butler CC partnership for:
- Ag-tech training programs (precision ag, automation, drones)
- Building trades apprenticeships (for housing + infrastructure projects)
- Advanced manufacturing certifications
- Broadband/digital skills training

**Upside:** Attract training-seekers; create pipeline for local job recruitment; position El Dorado as destination for career changers and rural youth.

## MARKET WINDOW: THE 2026-2030 OPPORTUNITY

### Why This Window Is Critical

1. **Wichita MSA growth inflection (2026-2030):** Suburban saturation driving second-ring growth; El Dorado is perfectly positioned landing zone.

2. **Broadband deployment timing (H2 2026):** Remote work infrastructure enabling rural relocation; El Dorado can capture early wave of attracted workers.

3. **Housing affordability crisis peak (2025-2030):** Millennial family formation + boomer downsizing creating maximum demand for starter homes; supply severely constrained nationally.

4. **Manufacturing reshoring momentum (2024-2030):** Tariffs + supply chain localization driving industrial investment; water-intensive sectors seeking regional hubs.

5. **Leadership & capital availability (2026):** New city commission; Vision 2040 adoption momentum; $3M B.A.S.E. grant ready for deployment; state/federal funding windows open.

### Why Delay Is Costly

**2031-2035 becomes harder:** Competing jurisdictions will have locked in commitments, developer relationships, land assemblies. First-mover advantage is 2026-2028.

**Demographic window narrows:** Millennial family formation peaks 2025-2030; boomer retiree migration continues through 2035. El Dorado misses peak affordability-seekers if housing pipeline isn't active 2026-2028.

**Tariff uncertainty:** If tariffs ease 2027-2028, reshoring investment moderates. Current wave (244K jobs in 2024) must be captured NOW while compensation/location calculus still favors reshoring.

## CONCLUSION: SEIZE THE MOMENT

**El Dorado's opportunity in 2026-2030 is real, measurable, and time-sensitive.**

The convergence of:
- Manufacturing reshoring (water demand)
- Housing shortage (affordability advantage)
- Broadband deployment (remote work)
- Wichita MSA spillover (commute-shed growth)
- Skills gap (education partnership)

...creates a **"perfect storm" of positive demand pressures** on rural Kansas communities. El Dorado's water infrastructure, location, and affordability position it to capture disproportionate share of this growth.

**Conservative execution:** Population +1,800; tax base +12.5%; quality-of-life improvements modest.

**Optimistic execution (Vision 2040 full deployment):** Population +3,000; tax base +29%; transformational community investment; sustained 3%+ growth through 2040.

**Time is the scarcest resource.** Decisions made in H2 2026 determine which scenario unfolds. The window closes 2028-2029 as competing jurisdictions lock in land, capital, and developer commitments.

**Recommendation:** Accelerate execution on Priorities #1-3 (water, housing, industrial park) immediately. Use 2027 to validate market response and refine strategy for 2028-2030 expansion phase.
