Vision 2040 Risk Management & Response Playbooks

Identify, assess, and mitigate 8 major risks to Vision 2040 execution. Proactive response strategies for each scenario.

Risk Assessment Framework

Each risk is rated on two dimensions: Probability (likelihood of occurring) and Impact (consequence if it happens). Overall risk level combines both factors.

Risk 1: FDI Recruitment Failure

No major company commits to El Dorado despite 2-year recruitment effort

HIGH
Probability: Medium (30–40%)
Impact: High (derails entire 2040 plan)

Root Causes

  • Execution gaps (poor outreach, weak pitch)
  • Competitor aggressiveness (Dodge City, Salina match incentives)
  • Market headwinds (recession, sector slowdown)
  • Land/infrastructure constraints (unforeseen issues)

Response Playbook

PHASE 1: Detection (Months 1–6)
  • Track outreach metrics: emails sent, calls booked, site visits, LOI pipeline
  • Monthly executive dashboarding of KPIs
  • If site visits drop below 2/month → RED FLAG
PHASE 2: Mitigation (Months 6–12)
  • Boost outreach budget: hire dedicated FDI officer or recruitment consultant
  • Diversify targets: don't rely on 1–2 company LOIs; expand prospect list to 20+
  • Leverage AMAZONE proof-of-concept: "We just recruited a German manufacturer; we can recruit more"
  • Accelerate water/FTZ messaging: lead with unique advantages
PHASE 3: Fallback (Months 12+)
  • Scale down Vision 2040 ambitions: focus on smaller wins (50–100 job projects vs. 500+ job facilities)
  • Pivot to tourism/hospitality development: leverage Lake, downtown, arts infrastructure
  • Expand entrepreneurship programs: focus on startups + small business clusters instead of major FDI

Risk 2: Water Supply Uncertainty

Drought, climate change, or regulatory action threatens El Dorado Lake capacity

MEDIUM
Probability: Low (10–15%)
Impact: Catastrophic (invalidates entire FDI strategy)

Response Playbook

PREVENTION (Ongoing)
  • Annual water quality/capacity audits by USGS
  • Drought contingency planning with USACE
  • USACE contract renewal preparation (currently 50+ years, monitor changes)
  • Invest in water conservation tech (recycling, treatment)
IF THREAT EMERGES
  • Invest in water recycling/treatment infrastructure (add 5–10M gal/day capacity)
  • Negotiate expanded USACE contract terms (extend to 75+ years)
  • Pivot FDI strategy: emphasize recycled water capability to sustainability-minded companies

Risk 3: Youth Retention Failure

Young adults continue to leave for Wichita/larger cities (currently 38% retention)

HIGH

Response Playbook

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Start Year 1)
  • Lake activation: concerts, events, dining, recreational infrastructure to create "quality of place"
  • Downtown revitalization: coffee shops, breweries, arts venues to attract young professionals
  • Housing incentives: $15K–$30K down-payment help for ages 22–35 in North El Dorado district
  • Workforce development: partner with companies to create career pathways for USD 490 graduates
MEDIUM-TERM (Years 2–4)
  • Partner with regional companies: job placement agreements with precision ag, battery, aerospace employers
  • Salary acceleration: FDI companies bring $50K+ jobs for entry-level positions (3-year bump plan)
  • Alumni engagement: create mentorship program connecting El Dorado natives who left with current youth

Risk 4: Labor Force Capacity Constraint

Current labor force (5,200) insufficient for 1,000+ new jobs from FDI

HIGH

Response Playbook

PHASE 1: Recruitment (Years 1–2)
  • Workforce housing: North El Dorado development targets Wichita-area workers (30 mi commute)
  • Relocation incentives: $10K–$20K for out-of-area workers to move to El Dorado
  • Wage premium: FDI companies pay 10–15% above Wichita rates to attract top talent
PHASE 2: Training (Ongoing)
  • Partner with USD 490 + Butler CC: curriculum alignment with precision ag, manufacturing, biotech
  • Kansas TARC grants: $150K–$500K annually for specialized training (welding, CNC, composites)
  • Incumbent worker programs: retrain existing workers for higher-skill roles

Risk 5: Broadband Parity Erodes Data Center Advantage

By 2027–2028, BEAD funding gives all competitors gigabit fiber, eliminating El Dorado's broadband advantage

MEDIUM

Response Playbook

24-MONTH CRITICAL WINDOW
  • Goal: Close data center LOI by Q4 2027, before broadband parity
  • Accelerate outreach: identify 10–15 warm prospect companies by Month 6
  • Site visits: complete 5+ site visits per month during 2026
  • LOI target: Groundbreaking by Q1 2027
IF DATA CENTER DEAL FAILS
  • Pivot messaging: lead with water + FTZ (not broadband) for advanced manufacturing
  • Target biotech + ag tech instead: these prioritize water + ag ecosystem, not broadband
  • Diversify FDI: don't bet entire plan on 1 data center; pursue 5 simultaneous projects

Risk 6: Competing Cities Win Major Projects

Dodge City or Emporia land high-profile FDI before El Dorado closes deal

MEDIUM

Response Playbook

COMPETITIVE RESPONSE
  • Analyze competitor wins: identify why they won (incentives, land, water, workforce)
  • Boost El Dorado advantages: if Dodge won cold storage, El Dorado can win precision ag (differentiation)
  • Increase incentive budget: if competitors match incentives, allocate additional funds
  • Double down on AMAZONE: "We've already proven we can attract German manufacturers; we'll do it again"
