Waterfront Commercial Development Strategy

El Dorado Lake's 22-mile shoreline currently captures zero commercial activity within park boundaries. Vision 2040 activates waterfront commercial zones through coordinated development of marina infrastructure, dining, retail, and water sports — all clustered in strategic nodes to maximize economic impact and visitor experience.

Waterfront Commercial Zones Framework

Three interconnected commercial zones, each serving different visitor segments and market needs:

West Marina Zone

North Gateway waterfront. Full-service marina + boat rentals + nautical retail. Primary boater destination.

Dining District

Three-restaurant cluster (fine dining, casual cafe, marina bar). Anchors visitor spending + secondary retail.

Water Sports Hub

Kayak rentals, paddleboard school, sailing concession. Day-trip recreation infrastructure.

Primary Commercial Node

West Marina Commercial Zone

The West Marina Zone is the anchor of waterfront development, positioned on North Gateway waterfront within easy walking distance of the new neighborhood. This 40-50 acre node includes marina operations, boat rentals, waterfront retail, and hospitality support facilities.

Facility Components

  • Marina: 150 slips, fuel dock, pump-out, electric charging
  • Dry Storage: 80 boats seasonal
  • Ship's Store: 2,500 sf retail (navigation, safety, apparel)
  • Boat Rentals: Powerboats, sailboats, day-use slips
  • Water Sports: Kayak/SUP rental and instruction
  • Office/Admin: 1,500 sf marina operations
  • Restrooms/Showers: Public facilities
  • Waterfront Plaza: Event + gathering space

Recreation Infrastructure

Water Sports Hub

A dedicated water sports hub serves day-trippers and new learners. Kayak rentals, paddleboard instruction, sailing concession, and beach access infrastructure create low-barrier entry points to lake recreation.

Water Sports Program Components

Rental Fleet

  • Kayaks: 30 single + tandem
  • Paddleboards: 20 SUP boards
  • Sailboats: 5 small sailing craft (beginner-friendly)
  • Specialty Craft: 10 day-use power rentals
  • Seasonal Hours: May-September daily; weekends only Oct-April

Instruction Programs

  • Youth Swimming: Spring/summer clinics
  • Kayak Safety: Beginner + intermediate courses
  • Stand-Up Paddleboarding: Lessons (2-3 per week)
  • Sailing Clinics: Weekend seminars
  • Guided Tours: Naturalist-led paddling tours
