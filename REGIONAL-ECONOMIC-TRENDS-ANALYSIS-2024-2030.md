# Regional Economic Trends Analysis: El Dorado, KS & Butler County
## 2024-2030 Outlook & Impact on Vision 2040

**Date:** June 2026
**Focus Area:** El Dorado, KS; Butler County; Wichita MSA (context)
**Research Period:** 2024-2026 (with 5-year projections)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

El Dorado, Kansas is positioned at an inflection point for regional growth. Butler County is experiencing modest but consistent population growth (0.52% annually, +4.9% 2014-2024), driven by proximity to Wichita's expanding MSA. Critical tailwinds include:

1. **Kansas economic recovery** (2.4% GSP growth in 2024, improving to 2.2-2.0% 2025-2026)
2. **Wichita MSA expansion** ($46.47B GDP; 17.1% growth projected to 750K by 2040)
3. **National manufacturing reshoring** ($1.7 trillion investments, 244K jobs in 2024)
4. **Historic housing shortage** (4,000 new homes/year needed in rural Kansas)
5. **BEAD broadband approval** ($166.6M for Kansas; construction begins H2 2026)
6. **Regional data center boom** (Google, Meta, Cloverleaf projects in Kansas City area)

**For El Dorado Vision 2040:** These trends suggest strong demand for industrial water users, workforce-ready infrastructure, and affordable housing — all core pillars of El Dorado's strategic plan.

## 3. HOUSING & REAL ESTATE TRENDS

### Regional Housing Crisis

**Scale of Shortage:**
- **Rural Kansas need:** 4,000 new homes/year to meet population growth + replacement
- **State deficit:** 55,000+ housing units short for low-income residents
- **Construction gap:** Labor shortage in building trades constraining supply

### Price Escalation

| Location | 2023 | 2024 | 2025 | Change |
|----------|------|------|------|--------|
| **Kansas Median** | $248,000 | $255,000 | $269,000 | +8.6% in 2 years |
| **Johnson County** | — | $560,000 | $575,000 | Extreme outlier |
| **Butler County** | — | $207,000 | $217,600 | +5.1% |
| **Wichita** | — | $194,372 | $205,000 | +5.5% |

**Affordability Crisis:**
- 27% of all Kansas residents spending >30% income on housing costs
- Majority of low-income Kansans severely cost-burdened
- Starter home shortage: Fewer affordable entry-level properties

### New Construction Pipeline

**Recent Developments (2024-2026):**
- Habitat for Humanity: 14-home community land trust in Olathe (affordability preservation)
- FRAME program: Training + construction of workforce housing
- Projected new construction growth: +10% in 2025, +5% in 2026

**Market Dynamics Favoring El Dorado:**
1. **Price differential:** El Dorado home prices ($217K median, Butler Co.) significantly below Wichita metro average ($330K Kansas average)
2. **Millennial attraction:** Affordability is #1 reason millennials relocate (16.8% cite housing as primary driver)
3. **Rural-to-urban migration:** Wichita attracting ag workers, smaller town residents seeking healthcare/education — but El Dorado can capture cost-sensitive segment
4. **Remote work cohort:** Rural Kansas positioning itself to capture remote workers (Lincoln County program generated 330+ applications for 5-person target)

### Demographic Housing Demand

**Driving Demand:**
- Baby boomer aging (469K seniors in Kansas; 15.5% of population; growing care needs)
- Millennial family formation (peak home-buying age 30-40, concentrated 2025-2030)
- Immigration-driven growth in Wichita (Vietnamese, Somali, Burmese communities)
- Rural-to-urban migration within Kansas (smaller towns → Wichita → possible backlash to El Dorado?)

**Key Insight:** Aging population + young family demand = bimodal housing market opportunity (seniors seeking maintenance-free housing; young families seeking affordable starter homes).

## 6. DEMOGRAPHIC SHIFTS & MIGRATION PATTERNS

### Wichita Metro Population Growth Drivers

**Immigration-Driven Growth:**
- Vietnamese, Somali, Burmese communities sustaining metro population growth
- Rural-to-urban in-migration from smaller Kansas towns
- Agricultural workers relocating for healthcare/education access

**Millennial Relocation Patterns (2024-2025):**
- 1 in 10 millennials moved in 2024
- #1 motivation: Better/new housing (16.8%)
- Wichita attractive due to home values ($194K average, below national $400K+ median)
- Entry-level affordability driving metro attraction

**Baby Boomer Retirement Migration:**
- Wichita/Kansas not primary destination (warm states—FL, AZ, TX dominant)
- However, Kansas affordability ($924/month Topeka housing) attractive to cost-sensitive seniors
- Growing senior population (15.5% of Kansas) creating healthcare demand
- Opportunity: El Dorado positioned for "third-place" retirement (affluent Kansans seeking small-town amenities + urban proximity)

### Remote Worker Opportunity Segment

**National Trend:** Remote workers disproportionately choosing rural communities with:
- Broadband access (critical)
- Cost of living <$250K home value (El Dorado at $217K median ✓)
- Quality of life amenities (recreation, culture, small-town feel)
- Proximity to regional hub (within 1 hour of metro services)

**Lincoln County, KS Case Study:**
- Program offered: $5,000 relocation grant + free lot for home building
- Response: 330+ applications for 5-person target; 35+ qualified applicants
- Median home value: $92K (extreme rural); demonstrates strong demand at even lower price points
- Spinoff effects: Property value appreciation, new local business formation, population stabilization

**El Dorado Opportunity:** Positioned between Lincoln County (too remote) and Wichita (too expensive); ideal landing zone for remote worker attraction.

## 10. SPECIFIC RECOMMENDATIONS FOR VISION 2040 ALIGNMENT

### Priority 1: Water Infrastructure = Competitive Moat
- **Action:** Accelerate B.A.S.E. grant projects (water treatment expansion, transmission main, sewer interceptor)
- **Rationale:** Industrial water is scarcest resource in reshoring location calculus; Panasonic precedent signals demand
- **2026-2030 Target:** Secure 2-3 water-intensive industrial commitments (food processing, chemical, beverage manufacturing)

### Priority 2: Housing Acceleration = Population Growth Engine
- **Action:** Implement affordable housing development program; partner with Habitat, community land trusts; target 50-100 units 2026-2030
- **Rationale:** Housing shortage acute; millennial demand peaking; can capture 2-3x market share with targeted approach
- **2026-2030 Target:** 75 new housing units; achieve $20K price advantage vs Wichita metro average

### Priority 3: Broadband-Ready Industrial Park = Attraction Asset
- **Action:** Plan broadband infrastructure into industrial park master plan; leverage BEAD program for municipal connectivity
- **Rationale:** Data centers + reshoring = fiber-dependent; El Dorado can differentiate on connectivity
- **2026-2030 Target:** 100-150 acres broadband-ready; land leases averaging $12-15K/acre/year for SME + light industrial

### Priority 4: Regional Transportation Corridor Marketing = Positioning
- **Action:** Capitalize on K-96 improvements (2026-2030); position El Dorado as "30-min to Wichita, small-town life" destination
- **Rationale:** Commute-shed expanding; El Dorado at optimal distance-to-amenity ratio
- **2026-2030 Target:** Attract 25-40% of Wichita MSA commuter overflow; capture 1.5-2% of metro new residents annually

### Priority 5: Agricultural Technology Ecosystem = Niche Economic Cluster
- **Action:** Create ag-tech incubator; partner Butler CC on training programs; position as regional service hub
- **Rationale:** Precision ag, automation, conservation practices = sustained rural demand; El Dorado's agricultural proximity = natural advantage
- **2026-2030 Target:** 5-10 ag-tech startups; 50+ new specialized service jobs

### Priority 6: Remote Worker Recruitment = Demographic Targeting
- **Action:** Launch remote worker relocation program (smaller than Lincoln County model, but complementary); highlight affordability + broadband
- **Rationale:** High-ROI demographic targeting; proven demand signal; low-cost marketing (digital + word-of-mouth)
- **2026-2030 Target:** Recruit 150-250 remote workers; each averaging $80-120K income = $12-30M annual spending stimulus

**Optimistic Case (VIS 2040 execution):** El Dorado reaches ~18,000 population, +3,000 residents over 4 years (3.2% CAGR), with $180-250M in industrial/mixed-use investment, 2-3 major water-using industrial tenants recruited, and 150-250 remote worker household relocations.

**Key Success Factor:** Accelerated execution on water infrastructure, broadband-ready industrial park development, and targeted housing programs in 2026-2027 to capture front-end of regional growth wave (2027-2030 most favorable demographic/economic window).
