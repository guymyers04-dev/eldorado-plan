# El Dorado Vision 2040: Permitting Roadmap
## Clear Path from Letter of Intent to Occupancy

**Prepared by:** City Engineer + Planning Director  
**Date:** August 15, 2026  
**Status:** APPROVED FOR EXECUTION  
**Timeline:** LOI → Occupancy: 90–180 days (expedited pathway)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Vision 2040 targets 1,500–2,400 new jobs + 300 housing units by 2032. Current permitting timeline (12–18 months) is a competitive disadvantage. This roadmap compresses approval to **90–180 days** through:

1. **Pre-approved industrial/residential templates** (eliminates design phase bottleneck)
2. **Concurrent permitting** (planning + engineering review in parallel, not sequential)
3. **Designated decision-makers** (single point of contact for each project stage)
4. **Expedited review SLAs** (24–48h turnaround on staff feedback)

**Result:** Developers see clear 9-month runway from decision to construction. AMAZONE expansion, housing pilot, and supply chain partners can move quickly.

## SECTION 2: DECISION GATES & APPROVAL FLOWCHART

### Tier 1: "Fast Track" (LOI → Construction in 90 days)
**Projects that qualify:** 
- Single industrial building < 50,000 sq ft
- Single residential building < 25 units
- Uses pre-approved site plan template
- No environmental constraints

**Approval Path:**
```
LOI → (24h) Engineer pre-review → (7 days) Concurrent site plan review 
→ (7 days) Zoning staff approval → (14 days) City council vote 
→ (7 days) Final permits → GO
```

**Owner:** City Engineer + Planning Director  
**Turnaround SLA:** 7 days between each gate

---

### Tier 2: "Standard Track" (LOI → Construction in 135–165 days)
**Projects that qualify:**
- Industrial 50,000–150,000 sq ft OR
- Residential 25–75 units OR
- Mixed-use with retail component OR
- Requires Phase II ESA or utility upgrades

**Approval Path:**
```
LOI → (48h) Engineer + Planning + Finance review → (30 days) Concurrent site plan 
+ environmental → (30 days) City council → (30 days) Final permitting → GO
```

**Owner:** City Engineer + Planning Director + Finance Director  
**Turnaround SLA:** 48h for initial feedback, 7 days between gates

---

### Tier 3: "Complex Track" (LOI → Construction in 180+ days)
**Projects that qualify:**
- Industrial > 150,000 sq ft OR
- Residential > 75 units OR
- Major infrastructure investment (new water line, major utility upgrade) OR
- Environmental constraints requiring mitigation

**Approval Path:**
```
LOI → (48h) Full team review → (45 days) Feasibility + site planning 
→ (45 days) Environmental assessment + design → (30 days) City council work session + vote 
→ (30 days) Final permitting → GO
```

**Owner:** City Manager + City Engineer + Planning + Finance  
**Turnaround SLA:** 48h initial feedback, 10-day gates between phases

## SECTION 3: ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW FRAMEWORK

### Phase II ESA (Environmental Site Assessment)

**When Required:**
- Industrial mega-sites (prior commercial/industrial use suspected)
- Near Walnut River (wetlands/riparian area coordination)
- Groundwater considerations (monitoring required)

**Timeline:** 30 days (parallel with site planning)

**Process:**
1. **Day 1–5:** Scope determination (Phase I records review completed; Phase II scope defined)
2. **Day 6–20:** Phase II field sampling (soil borings, groundwater samples)
3. **Day 21–30:** Lab analysis + report + remediation recommendations (if any)

**Key Contact:**
- City Engineer: Coordinates ESA contractor
- Kansas Department of Health & Environment (KDHE): Regulatory oversight (if contamination found)

**Contingency:** If contamination found, remediation timeline extends 60–120 days (managed separately, doesn't delay site plan approval if cleanup plan agreed)

---

### Walnut River Coordination

**When Required:**
- Projects within 500 ft of Walnut River
- Stormwater discharge to river
- Floodplain development

**Timeline:** 30 days (parallel with site planning)

**Agencies:**
- US Army Corps of Engineers (USACE): Wetlands/waters of the US jurisdiction
- Kansas Department of Wildlife & Parks (KDWP): Stream health + restoration
- City of El Dorado: Floodplain management

**Fast-Track Option:** If project includes riparian restoration + stormwater bioswales, USACE review accelerated (positive environmental outcome = expedited permit)

---

### Utility Capacity Assessment

**When Required:** All industrial + residential projects > 10 units

**Timeline:** 45 days (parallel with site planning)

**Components:**
1. **Water Capacity:** Current demand vs. supply. Can [Project] be served? If upgrade needed, timeline?
2. **Sewer Capacity:** Treatment plant + collection system capacity. Major lift station needed?
3. **Electric:** Substation capacity? Step-down transformer needed?
4. **Gas:** Pressure/capacity available?
5. **Stormwater:** Detention pond capacity? Can project use on-site infiltration?

**Outcome:** Utility interconnection agreement signed (cost-share framework) + upgrade timeline (if needed)

## SECTION 4: DESIGN STANDARDS & PRE-APPROVED TEMPLATES

### Industrial Site Plan Standard
✅ Pre-approved for developments meeting this criteria:
- Lot size: 5+ acres
- Building setback: 50 ft (front), 25 ft (side), 50 ft (rear)
- Parking: On-site, 3 spaces per 1,000 sq ft gross building area
- Stormwater: On-site detention, 100-year storm design
- Traffic: US-77 access signalized, left-turn lane if needed
- Loading: Screened from public view, truck turning radius verified
- Utilities: Underground electrical, clear utility easements

**Design Package Approval:** 7 days turnaround (vs. 30–45 days for custom review)

---

### Residential Site Plan Standard
✅ Pre-approved for developments meeting this criteria:
- Unit count: 15–75 mixed-income units
- Parking: 1.0 space/unit minimum, EV charging 20% of spaces
- Open space: 20% of site (playground, pocket park, or trail access)
- Pedestrian access: Connected to adjacent streets, downtown if within 1 mile
- Stormwater: On-site management (bioswales, rain gardens, infiltration)
- Architectural: Single-family-scale massing (no monolithic facades), pitched roofs preferred, earth-tone palette
- Street frontage: Retail/active use on ground floor (if downtown site)

**Design Package Approval:** 10 days turnaround (custom designs reviewed in 20–30 days)

---

### Utility Interconnection Standard
✅ Fast-track for projects meeting this criteria:
- Water demand: < 500 gallons/day (typical for commercial) or < 100 GPD per residential unit
- Sewer demand: < 500 GPD or proportional residential
- Electric: < 500 kW (no major substation upgrade)
- Gas: Standard pressure/volume available

**Utility Approval:** 30 days (vs. 45–60 days for complex upgrades)

## SECTION 8: RISK MITIGATION & CONTINGENCY PLANS

### Risk 1: Utility Upgrade Delays (Water/Sewer)
**Trigger:** Project requires water/sewer line extension > 1 mile  
**Impact:** 45-day delay (design + construction of utility line)  
**Mitigation:** 
- Pre-fund utility master plan ($500K–1M) to design extensions ahead of projects
- Utility interconnection agreements lock in cost-share (developer reimburses % of cost)
- Fast-track utility design (coordinate with project site plan concurrently)

---

### Risk 2: Environmental Contamination Found (Phase II ESA)
**Trigger:** Phase II ESA finds soil/groundwater contamination  
**Impact:** 60–120 day delay (remediation plan + KDHE oversight)  
**Mitigation:**
- Include Phase I ESA in pre-LOI review (identify likely risk before major investment)
- Pre-identify sites with known/suspected contamination (oil industry legacy)
- Develop generic remediation frameworks for common contaminants (light petroleum = standard remediation)
- KDHE pre-approval of remediation approaches (no surprises late in process)

---

### Risk 3: Walnut River Wetlands Issues
**Trigger:** USACE determines project impacts jurisdictional wetlands  
**Impact:** 45–90 day delay (permitting + mitigation requirements)  
**Mitigation:**
- Early USACE pre-consultation (before LOI → confirm jurisdiction before site design)
- Integrate riparian restoration into all riverfront projects (positive environmental outcome = expedited USACE review)
- Purchase mitigation credits from pre-approved mitigation bank (fast-track compliance)

---

### Risk 4: Community Opposition to Project
**Trigger:** Neighbors/groups object to zoning change or density  
**Impact:** Delayed city council vote, revised design, 30–60 day slip  
**Mitigation:**
- Early community engagement (developer presents at city council work session before official vote)
- Community benefit agreements (CBA) that address concerns (affordable unit commitment, green space, local hiring)
- Clear design standards (pre-approved templates reduce design surprises)
