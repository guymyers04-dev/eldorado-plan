Initiative Overview

What Is It?

Develop 150-200 units of modern, affordable student housing targeted at USD 490 CTE students, Butler CC commuters, and WSU regional students. Mix of dorms, townhomes, and studios at $400-700/month.

Why It Matters

CTE programs attract students from across region. On-campus housing increases attendance, retention, and completion. Also serves Butler CC students seeking affordable housing closer to El Dorado than Wichita.

Housing Program

Housing Mix

  • CTE Dorms: 80-100 beds (residence halls)
  • Townhomes: 30-40 units (3-bed, 2-bath)
  • Efficiency/Studio: 40-60 units (1-bed)
  • Total Capacity: 150-200 residents
  • On-Campus Common Spaces: Dining, study, recreation

Pricing Model

  • CTE Dorms: $425/month (include utilities, meals)
  • Townhomes: $600-750/month (utilities included)
  • Studio/Efficiency: $400-500/month
  • Financial Aid: Tie-in with USD 490 CTE scholarships
  • Accessibility: Subsidies for low-income students

Financial Model

Capital Investment

  • Dorm Construction: $6-8M (100 beds @ $60-80K/bed)
  • Townhome Development: $2-3M (40 units)
  • Efficiency/Studio: $800K-1.2M (50 units)
  • Total Capital: $8.8-12.2M

Annual Operating Revenue

  • Dorm Revenue: $510K (100 × $425 × 12)
  • Townhomes: $360K (40 × $675 × 12)
  • Studios/Efficiency: $210K (50 × $450 × 12)
  • Total Annual Revenue: $1.08M

Funding Strategy

Financing Sources

  • HUD/USDA Housing Grants: $2-3M
  • State Education Bonds: $3-4M
  • New Market Tax Credits: $2-2.5M
  • Low-Income Housing Tax Credits: $1.5-2M
  • Private Developer Investment: $1-1.5M

Operating Model

  • Public-Private Partnership: USD 490 + developer
  • Non-Profit Management: USD 490 foundation or regional housing authority
  • Self-Supporting: Revenue covers operations + debt service by Year 3-4
