Foreign Trade Zone #28

El Dorado's FTZ status is an economic advantage. Tariff-free imports, duty deferral, and logistics benefits make it attractive for manufacturing and distribution companies.

What Is an FTZ?

A Foreign Trade Zone (FTZ) is a U.S. Customs-designated area where foreign goods can enter duty-free and be stored, manufactured, or assembled without U.S. tariffs until they enter the broader U.S. market. Think of it as a "tariff-free zone" for international commerce.

El Dorado is part of FTZ #28 (Kansas), one of the most active foreign trade zones in the Midwest. This enrollment gives companies importing goods or components significant cost savings.

El Dorado's FTZ Benefits

1. Duty Elimination on Foreign Imports

Companies importing raw materials, components, or finished goods can store them in an FTZ without paying U.S. tariffs until goods enter domestic commerce. This can save 5–20% on import costs.

Example: Precision ag tech company imports sensors from Germany → stores in El Dorado FTZ duty-free → assembles into product → enters U.S. market → pays tariffs only on value-added (not raw goods).

2. Duty Deferral on Manufacturing

If a company manufactures or assembles goods in an FTZ using imported components, tariffs are deferred until the finished product enters the U.S. market. This reduces cash flow burden.

Example: Battery thermal systems: import raw aluminum/copper → assemble in El Dorado FTZ → export to Tesla or domestic OEM → tariffs deferred during manufacturing cycle.

3. Tariff Avoidance on Re-Exports

If goods are imported, assembled, and then re-exported (not sold domestically), tariffs are avoided entirely. This is valuable for companies with global supply chains.

Example: Ag tech company assembles guidance systems in El Dorado, ships to Canada/Mexico → zero U.S. tariffs (USMCA benefit).

4. Logistics & Distribution Hub Status

Companies can establish distribution centers in FTZ areas to serve multiple markets with tariff advantages. El Dorado's I-35 location + FTZ status = ideal regional logistics node.

Example: Cold chain logistics company imports pharmaceutical products → stores in El Dorado FTZ warehouse → distributes across Midwest without tariff penalties.

Financial Impact Example

Scenario: Precision Ag Tech Company

Imports annually: $5M in sensors/components from Germany
U.S. tariff rate (standard): 2.5%
Tariff cost (without FTZ): $125,000/year
With FTZ status: Tariff deferred until product enters U.S. market
Annual savings (conservative): $50,000–$75,000 (40–60% reduction due to manufacturing value-add)

For a 30-person facility → $50K–$75K annual FTZ tariff savings = extra $1,600–$2,500 per employee in profit that can fund higher wages, R&D, or equipment investment.
