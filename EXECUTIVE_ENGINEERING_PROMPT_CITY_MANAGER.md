## 📊 DATA-BACKED BUSINESS CASE

### Regional Context (Why Now?)

**I-35 Corridor Saturation (2026 Kansas DOT Data):**
- Primary Wichita-Kansas City corridor nearing capacity
- Commercial traffic seeking alternative routes
- Distribution/logistics companies need alternatives to I-35 congestion

**E-Commerce Driving Logistics Growth:**
- Warehouse/distribution jobs growing +3-5% annually across Kansas
- Regional market (7-county south-central area) needs infrastructure
- Wichita-area distribution centers at 90%+ occupancy (no capacity for growth)

**El Dorado's Competitive Position:**
- **US-77 highway:** North-south corridor with 50% spare capacity
- **KS-54 highway:** East-west connection to Wichita Metro (29 miles)
- **Industrial land:** 1,131-acre development-ready mega-site
- **Labor/costs:** Significantly lower than Wichita area
- **Experience:** Existing manufacturing base (AMAZONE, oil refineries)
- **Utilities:** Capacity for major industrial expansion
- **Timing:** 18-24 month first-mover advantage window

### Market Opportunity (15-Year Horizon)

**Current US-77 Traffic:** 3,500-5,000 vehicles/day  
**2040 Projection:** 5,500-8,000 vehicles/day (+50-100% growth)  
**Capacity Status:** Adequate for entire Vision 2040 horizon

**Regional Demand:**
- Distribution/logistics companies seeking Wichita alternatives
- 3PL providers (third-party logistics) needing satellite locations
- Cold storage facilities (agricultural products, food distribution)
- E-commerce fulfillment centers (last-mile delivery hubs)
- Trucking and transportation services

### Economic Targets (2040)

**Jobs:** 50-100 distribution/logistics (core) + 50-100 supporting services = **100-200 total**

**Economic Activity:** $50-80M cumulative (facilities, operations, supply chain)

**Tax Base:** $20-40M permanent annual increase

**Population Growth:** 150-300 residents (workers + families relocating for jobs)

**Regional Identity:** Recognized "US-77 Regional Logistics Hub" by:
- Kansas DOT (highway planning)
- Business community (recruitment consideration)
- Regional chambers (regional economic strategy)
- Logistics/distribution industry

## ⚠️ CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

### 1. First-Mover Advantage (18-24 Month Window)
**Risk:** Other Kansas towns (Oxford, Burns, Beaumont) recognize opportunity and position first  
**Mitigation:** Execute positioning + recruitment aggressively in 2026-2027  
**Consequence:** Miss window → lose competitive advantage for years

### 2. Executive Alignment
**Risk:** City Commission votes for strategy, then doesn't fund it or support recruitment  
**Mitigation:** Secure written Commission approval + budget commitment + Mayor backing  
**Consequence:** Strategy dies in budget cycle

### 3. Sustained Recruitment (5-10 Year Commitment)
**Risk:** First tenant fails to close, team gives up  
**Mitigation:** Understand logistics hub building takes years; commit to at least 5-year recruitment effort  
**Consequence:** Hub vision dies; become generic small town again

### 4. Regional Marketing Consistency
**Risk:** Messaging becomes inconsistent across channels; hub positioning diluted  
**Mitigation:** Create brand guidelines, messaging platform, consistent collateral  
**Consequence:** Regional market doesn't recognize El Dorado as "the" US-77 hub

### 5. Infrastructure Execution
**Risk:** Site isn't ready when tenant is interested (utilities, zoning, permits)  
**Mitigation:** Complete infrastructure readiness assessment in Phase 1; be ready to move fast  
**Consequence:** First tenant goes to competitor town; hub opportunity lost
