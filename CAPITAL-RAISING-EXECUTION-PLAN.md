## SECTION 1: FEDERAL GRANT STRATEGY

### Priority 1: EDA Good Jobs Challenge ($25M–$50M Potential)

**Program:** Economic Development Administration Good Jobs Challenge  
**Timeline:** Application typically due April–June annually; awards Sept–Oct  
**El Dorado Fit:** Workforce development + job creation (underserved workers)  

#### Preparation Phase (Now – January 2027)
- [ ] **Research Step 1:** Visit eda.gov/programs/good-jobs-challenge; download latest RFP
- [ ] **Research Step 2:** Analyze past winners (200+ projects awarded 2022–2024; review 5–10 Kansas/regional winners)
- [ ] **Internal Planning:** Identify 3–5 El Dorado projects aligned with "good jobs" (health care training, apprenticeships, ag-tech, manufacturing)
- [ ] **Workforce Partner Identification:** Engage Butler CC (primary partner), Kansas TARC, local employers

#### Application Phase (February – May 2027)
- [ ] **Hire Grant Writer** (if not already) — $60K–$80K annual cost; CRITICAL for $25M+ awards
- [ ] **Develop Project Narrative:**
  - What jobs will be created? (1,600+ Vision 2040 jobs, target 300–500 in underserved populations)
  - What training will be provided? (Apprenticeships, certificate programs, on-the-job training)
  - What's the public investment? (Commit $500K–$1M city + Butler CC + employer match)
  - Why El Dorado? (I-35 access, workforce, training infrastructure, AMAZONE proof-of-concept)
- [ ] **Financial Projections:**
  - Cost per job created (typical EDA target: $15K–$25K per job)
  - El Dorado project: $20M Good Jobs → 800–1,000 jobs → $20K–$25K cost/job ✓ Fits
- [ ] **Letters of Support:**
  - Butler CC (training commitment)
  - Chamber of Commerce (employer buy-in)
  - Kansas Department of Labor (state agency support)
  - 2–3 major employers (HF Sinclair, AMAZONE, SBA Hospital commitment to hire)

#### Submission Phase (June 2027)
- [ ] **Final Narrative:** 15–20 pages (not more; EDA reviewers get 100s of applications)
- [ ] **Budget Detail:** Complete project budget, timeline, staffing plan
- [ ] **Evaluation Criteria Met:**
  - ✓ Jobs created (target 500–1,000 in Phase 1)
  - ✓ Underserved workers (include women, minorities, long-term unemployed, displaced workers)
  - ✓ Wage quality ($20/hour minimum; El Dorado target: $22–28/hour average)
  - ✓ Regional economic impact (Kansas economy benefit)
  - ✓ Partnership strength (multiple public/private partners)
- [ ] **Submit via Grants.gov** (online portal)

#### Post-Submission (September–December 2027)
- [ ] **Award Notification:** Expect Oct–Nov 2027
- [ ] **If Awarded:** ($25M–$50M opportunity)
  - Hire Program Director ($100K+ salary)
  - Launch training program with Butler CC
  - Begin employer outreach and job placement
  - **Revenue to El Dorado:** $25M–$50M over 2–3 years (workforce training + infrastructure)

#### Win Probability & Strategy
- **Historical Win Rate:** 15–20% (highly competitive; 200+ applications, 30–40 awards)
- **Success Factors:**
  - Strong partnership network (Butler CC critical)
  - Quantifiable job targets
  - Demonstrated employer commitment (signed letters)
  - Regional economic significance
- **If Not Awarded:** Apply again next year (2028); refine based on feedback

**Timeline:** 12–18 months from now to potential award

### Priority 2: EDA Public Works Grants ($1M–$5M)

**Program:** EDA Public Works & Economic Adjustment Assistance  
**Amount:** $1M–$5M per project (50% cost share required)  
**El Dorado Fit:** Infrastructure benefiting industrial/economic development  

#### Best Candidates (In Priority Order)
1. **Mega-Site Infrastructure** ($2–3M EDA request + $2–3M city match = $4–6M project)
2. **Water System Expansion** ($1.5M EDA request + $1.5M bonds = $3M project)
3. **Wastewater Plant Upgrade** ($2M EDA request + $2M bonds = $4M project)

#### Application Approach (Flexible Timeline)
- **Rolling Submission:** EDA accepts applications year-round (review cycle 12–18 months)
- **Strategy:** Submit annually (Jan or Sept); plan for 2-year funding cycle

#### Mega-Site Infrastructure Project ($4–6M Total)

**Project Scope:**
- Road improvements (rail spur entrance, truck routes, intersection upgrades): $1–1.5M
- Water/sewer capacity (lines to mega-site, treatment upgrades): $1–2M
- Utilities (electrical, broadband backbone): $800K–$1.5M
- Site prep (clearing, grading, environmental remediation if needed): $500K–$1M
- **Total:** $3.5–6M (request $2–3M EDA; city/partners match remainder)

**EDA Application Components:**
1. **Project Narrative (8–10 pages):**
   - Why this infrastructure? (Enable industrial development, mega-site absorption)
   - Expected outcomes? (500–1,000 jobs, $100M+ investment)
   - Regional impact? (Kansas employment, tax revenue)
   - Sustainability? (Infrastructure benefits last 30+ years)

2. **Budget Detail:**
   - Line-item costs (with quotes from contractors)
   - Project timeline (months to completion)
   - Staffing/management plan

3. **Economic Impact Analysis:**
   - Current baseline (El Dorado employment, tax base)
   - Projected impact (with mega-site development)
   - Multiplier effects (induced employment, supplier jobs)
   - ROI to federal government (tax payback over 20 years)

4. **Letters of Support:**
   - Butler County Commission (regional priority)
   - El Dorado Inc. (economic development entity)
   - Potential mega-site tenants (if identified; shows readiness)
   - SCKEDD (certified CDC, regional economic development organization)

5. **Environmental Review:**
   - Phase 1 Environmental Site Assessment (if any contamination risk)
   - NEPA compliance (environmental impact statement, if required)
   - Wetlands/water quality review

#### Application Timeline
- **Preparation:** 3–6 months (engineering, budget, letter coordination)
- **Submission:** 2–4 weeks (final edits, Grants.gov upload)
- **EDA Review:** 6–9 months (multiple rounds of questions)
- **Award Decision:** 12–18 months total from submission
- **Funding Received:** 18–24 months after award (closeout processes)

#### Win Probability & Strategy
- **Historical Win Rate:** 40–50% (more favorable than Good Jobs Challenge)
- **Success Factors:**
  - Clear, well-engineered infrastructure plan
  - Strong regional support (county, multiple organizations)
  - Job creation quantified
  - Applicant (city) has capacity to manage funds
- **Multiple Year Strategy:** Apply every year (Jan submission); expect 1–2 awards over 3–4 years

**Timeline to First Funding:** 18–24 months

- **Success Factors:**

- **Key:** Build relationship with USDA RD loan officer early (Q3 2026)

**Timeline to First Funding:** 6–12 months

### Priority 2: EB-5 Visa Program for Foreign Investment

#### Target Projects: Hotel & Senior Care

**EB-5 Program Basics:**
- Foreign investors receive US green card for $500K–$1M investment (in TEA = Targeted Employment Area)
- El Dorado qualifies (rural + high unemployment)
- Project creates 10+ jobs per $500K investment
- Regional Centers facilitate (regulatory compliance, investor recruitment)

#### Phase 1: Hire EB-5 Specialist (Q3–Q4 2026)
- [ ] **Consultant Search:** Find EB-5 regional center operator or immigration attorney
  - Ask for references; vet track record
  - Cost: $100K–$150K per project (placement + legal)
  - Services: Regulatory compliance, investor recruitment, documentation, USCIS filings
- [ ] **Select Specialist:** Commit to partnership

#### Phase 2: Project Structure & Documentation (Q4 2026 – Q1 2027)

**Hotel Project ($15M):**
- [ ] **Project Business Plan:** 50–100 page document
  - Executive summary
  - Market analysis (local hotel demand, competition)
  - Financial projections (10-year P&L, balance sheet)
  - Use of funds (land, design, construction, soft costs)
  - Job creation detail (positions, wages, full-time equivalents)
  - Exit strategy (refinance, sale, or hold long-term)
- [ ] **I-924 Petition:** Regional center submits to USCIS
  - Job creation economic analysis (by third party)
  - Investor pool sizing (how many $500K investors needed; e.g., $15M ÷ $500K = 30 investors)
  - Timeframe (construction 18 months; occupancy 6 months; job creation begins month 12)
- [ ] **Job Creation Verification:** Detailed plan
  - Direct jobs: 80–100 (hotel employees, contractors)
  - Induced jobs: 50+ (supplier, service economy jobs)
  - Total: 130–150 jobs (achieves 10 jobs per $500K × 30 investors = 300 job threshold)

#### Phase 3: Investor Recruitment (Q1–Q2 2027)
- [ ] **Investor Outreach (Geographic Focus):**
  - China (historically 40–50% of EB-5 investors; now restrictions; cautiously approach)
  - Vietnam, India (emerging sources; 20–30% market share)
  - Korea, Philippines (15–20% market)
  - Canada (small %); Europe (minimal)
- [ ] **Investor Events:** Regional center hosts webinars/events
  - Pitch: "Invest $500K, get US green card, earn 5–6% return"
  - Target: Attract 30 investors × $500K = $15M capital
  - Recruitment timeline: 3–6 months
- [ ] **Due Diligence (by USCIS):**
  - Source of investor funds verified
  - Criminal/sanctions background checks
  - Job creation economic model reviewed
  - Regional center oversight verified

#### Phase 4: USCIS Approval & Investor Closing (Q2–Q4 2027)
- [ ] **I-526 Petitions Filed:** Each investor submits (30 petitions for hotel project)
  - USCIS processes: 2–3 years typically (expedited processing available; costs $1,500 per case)
  - Approval: Conditional green card issued (valid 2 years)
- [ ] **Investor Capital Deployment:** Funds flow to project (once I-526 approved or conditional approval)
  - Timeline: 6–12 months after I-526 filing (staggered as investors approved)
  - Capital placement: $15M available for hotel construction
- [ ] **Job Creation Documentation:** Track and verify
  - After investor receives green card (2-year mark), conditional status removed
  - Project must maintain documented jobs for 2-year period
  - Annual reporting to USCIS

#### Expected Timeline & Capital
- **Hotel EB-5 Capital:** $15M; capital flows Q2 2027–Q1 2028 (staggered)
- **Senior Care EB-5 Capital:** $15–20M; capital flows Q3 2027–Q2 2028 (if pursued)
- **Total EB-5 Capital Potential:** $30M–$35M over 12 months

#### Success Factors & Risks
- **Factors:**
  - Strong project fundamentals (job creation verifiable; financial projections realistic)
  - Experienced regional center/law firm (regulatory compliance critical)
  - Investor pool identification (market conditions + geography matter)
  - Job creation documented rigorously (USCIS scrutinizes)
- **Risks:**
  - Political/regulatory risk (EB-5 program changes; visa allocation uncertainty)
  - Investor immigration risk (visa cap annually; priority dates; wait times)
  - Project execution risk (construction delays affect job creation timeline)
  - Return risk (if project underperforms, investor exit difficult; green card still granted, but capital recovery uncertain)

## SECTION 4: FEDERAL TAX CREDIT STRATEGY

### Priority 1: Historic Tax Credit (HTC) Syndication

**Program:** Federal (20%) + Kansas State (25%) = 45% total tax credit on qualified rehab costs

**Target: Central Avenue Historic District (8 buildings)**

#### Phase 1: NRHP Nomination (Q4 2026 – Q2 2027)

**Process:**
1. **Preliminary Evaluation:** Contact Kansas State Historic Preservation Office (SHPO)
   - Confirm buildings eligible for NRHP
   - Estimated nomination timeline: 6–9 months
2. **Nomination Preparation:** Hire historian/architect
   - Research building history
   - Document architectural significance
   - Photograph and measure buildings
3. **NRHP Listing:** Nominate to National Park Service
   - State review: 3 months
   - Federal review: 3–4 months
   - Expected listing: Q2–Q3 2027

#### Phase 2: Tax Credit Syndication (Q3 2027 – Q1 2028)

**Process:**
1. **Syndicator Recruitment:** Contact HTC syndicators
   - Merrill Lynch Community Development Finance
   - Boston Capital
   - WNC Housing Tax Credits
   - Meet with 2–3 syndicators; gauge interest
2. **Project Financial Modeling:**
   - Estimated rehab costs per building: $800K–$1.5M (total $8M–$12M for 8 buildings)
   - Tax credit value: 45% × $8–12M = $3.6–5.4M
   - Syndication value: $3.6–5.4M sold to investor pool
   - Proceeds fund rehab (covers 60–70% of cost; rest from developer/city grants)
3. **Syndicator Selection:** Choose best terms/partner
4. **Developer Recruitment:** Find restoration developers
   - Architect with historic preservation certification
   - Contractor with historic rehab experience
   - End-use operator (retail, office, residential conversion)

#### Phase 3: Project Execution (2028–2030)

**Timeline per Building:**
- Year 1: Design, permitting, financing (6 months)
- Year 2: Construction (9–12 months)
- Year 3: Lease-up/occupancy (3–6 months)
- Each building sequential (avoid credit timing issues)

**Total Capital Deployed:**
- Year 1 (2028): 2 buildings start; $2–3M HTC proceeds + private capital
- Year 2 (2028–2029): 3 buildings active; $3–4M HTC proceeds
- Year 3 (2029–2030): 3 buildings active; $2–3M HTC proceeds
- **Total HTC Proceeds:** $3.6–5.4M (all buildings by 2030)

#### Expected Outcomes
- 8 historic buildings rehabilitated
- 50–80 jobs created (construction + permanent operations)
- 120–150 housing units (if residential conversion)
- Downtown tax base increased $8–12M (improved property values)
