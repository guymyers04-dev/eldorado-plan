# PART 1: RELATIONSHIP DEVELOPMENT FRAMEWORK

## The 5-Stage Partnership Development Cycle

### Stage 1: PROSPECT IDENTIFICATION & RESEARCH (Weeks 1–2)
**Objective:** Identify decision-maker, understand motivations, prepare personalized outreach

**Actions:**
- [ ] Research company (expansion plans, recent news, financial status)
- [ ] Identify 2–3 decision-makers (primary contact + alternates)
- [ ] Find email/phone (LinkedIn, company website, industry directories)
- [ ] Review existing relationships (referral potential from partners, brokers, advisors)
- [ ] Assess fit with El Dorado (strategic value + their motivation match)
- [ ] Assign partnership owner (Data Center Director, FDI Officer, etc.)

**Output:** Prospect profile with contact info + research notes

---

### Stage 2: INITIAL OUTREACH (Weeks 2–3)
**Objective:** Introduce El Dorado; establish credibility; request initial conversation

**Contact Methods:**
1. **Email:** Personalized, value-focused (not generic mass email)
2. **Broker Intermediary:** Use CBRE/JLL/C&W for data center + real estate deals
3. **Government Channel:** Kansas Department of Commerce intro for FDI agencies
4. **Industry Association:** Use JETRO/GTAI/KOTRA for international manufacturers
5. **Cold Call:** Direct phone (research-backed, compelling 60-second pitch)

**Timing:** Best practices:
- **Email:** Tuesday–Thursday 9–11 AM (higher open rates)
- **Call:** Tuesday–Thursday 10 AM or 2–3 PM (decision-makers more available)
- **Follow-up:** 3 business days after email (if no response)

**Expected Response Rate:** 10–15% (1 response per 7–10 outreaches)

**Output:** Email sent + call notes (if completed)

---

### Stage 3: DISCOVERY CONVERSATION (Weeks 3–4)
**Objective:** Understand their needs; position El Dorado; assess genuine fit

**Meeting Structure:**
- **Duration:** 15–20 minutes (respect their time)
- **Format:** Phone call (initial); video call (if they suggest); in-person (later stage)
- **Agenda:**
  1. Introduce yourself + role (1 min)
  2. Context: Vision 2040 initiatives + job growth (1 min)
  3. **Listen:** Ask about their expansion strategy, site criteria, timeline (5–8 min)
  4. **Position:** Share why El Dorado fits (water, tax incentives, I-35, workforce, etc.) (3–5 min)
  5. **Value:** Offer to send site package + market data; propose next step (2 min)

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- "What are your top 3 site selection criteria right now?"
- "What's your timeline for this project/expansion?"
- "Are you evaluating other regions? What makes them attractive?"
- "Who else should I be talking to on your team?"
- "What would make El Dorado worth a site visit?"

**Red Flags / Deal-Breakers to Uncover:**
- "We're only looking at Texas/California" → Probably not a fit
- "Our timeline is 6 months and we need to decide next month" → High urgency, LOW probability
- "We require access to coastal ports" → El Dorado doesn't fit
- "We need 50,000+ skilled workers" → Workforce cap at ~5,000 in region

**Output:** Call notes + next step (site visit scheduled, materials to send, follow-up timeline)

---

### Stage 4: ENGAGEMENT & RELATIONSHIP BUILDING (Weeks 4–8)
**Objective:** Deepen relationship; demonstrate capabilities; maintain momentum

**Tactics:**
1. **Send Customized Materials:**
   - Personalized site package (not generic; reference their stated criteria)
   - Market studies relevant to their industry
   - Financial incentive summary
   - Case study (AMAZONE for FDI; data center brokers for tech; Sunrise for senior living)

2. **Site Visit Execution:**
   - Schedule 1.5–2 day visit (not rushed)
   - Tour mega-site or proposed development area
   - Introduce key stakeholders (Mayor, EDA Director, Chamber, potential partners)
   - Restaurant dinner with leadership (relationship building)
   - Address specific questions in real-time
   - Document feedback in writing (follow-up email)

3. **Ongoing Communication:**
   - Weekly touchpoints if site visit is imminent
   - Monthly updates on project progress (if in longer exploration)
   - Share relevant news (industry awards, competitor activities, new incentives announced)
   - Copy decision-maker on relevant internal updates ("As discussed, our workforce pipeline…")

4. **Identify Sponsor:**
   - Cultivate internal champion (VP Development, Regional Director)
   - Provide them ammunition (competitive analysis, financial models, reference calls to other projects)
   - Remove obstacles (permitting concerns, financial structure questions, legal issues)

**Output:** Site visit completed; champion identified; momentum maintained

---

### Stage 5: LETTER OF INTENT & NEGOTIATION (Weeks 8–16)
**Objective:** Transition from exploration to commitment; negotiate deal terms

**LOI Process:**
1. **Signal:** Receive verbal indication ("This looks promising; let's discuss terms")
2. **Preparation:** Engage legal team (city attorney, economic development counsel)
3. **Draft:** Prepare LOI with key terms:
   - Project description (facility, capex, jobs)
   - Land commitment (mega-site, specific acreage, price/terms)
   - Incentive package (tax abatement, infrastructure, grants)
   - Timeline (site control, design, permitting, construction start)
   - Performance conditions (job creation, wage floors, timeline compliance)
   - Confidentiality (NDA language)
4. **Negotiate:** Multiple rounds of edits (normal process; can take 2–4 weeks)
5. **Sign:** Final execution (signals commitment from both parties)

**Post-LOI:** Development agreement negotiation (more detailed; 2–3 months typical)

**Output:** LOI signed; development agreement initiated; project advancement to execution phase

# PART 5: NEGOTIATION FRAMEWORK FOR LOI TERMS

## Key LOI Components to Address

### 1. Project Description
**Define precisely:**
- Facility type + size (e.g., "200-bed senior living community")
- Capex commitment ($50M–$75M for hotel example)
- Job creation (150–200 permanent jobs)
- Timeline (groundbreaking Q1 2027; opening 2028)

### 2. Land Commitment
**Be specific:**
- Acreage (15–20 acres for senior living)
- Price/terms (land lease at $X/year OR purchase price of $X with city financing assistance)
- Site control timeline (30–60 days to take possession after LOI)
- Site condition (infrastructure ready, environmental clean)

### 3. Incentive Package
**Itemize:**
- Property tax abatement (X years, Y% reduction)
- Sales tax exemption (if applicable; SB 98 for data centers)
- Utility cost-sharing (water/sewer infrastructure: $X contribution)
- Workforce training grants ($X amount)
- Permitting fee waivers
- EB-5 visa coordination support (if applicable)

**Total Incentive Value: $X–$Y over Z years**

### 4. Performance Conditions
**Make measurable:**
- "Operator will create 150–200 full-time permanent jobs" (with wage floor: $18/hour minimum)
- "Facility will open by [DATE]"
- "Annual payroll will be $X minimum"
- "Job creation documentation required annually for first 5 years"

### 5. Timeline
**Be realistic:**
- LOI effective date: [DATE]
- Development agreement negotiation: 60–90 days
- Site control by: [DATE]
- Permit applications: [DATE]
- Groundbreaking: [DATE]
- Opening/operational: [DATE]

### 6. Confidentiality
**Protect both parties:**
- Public announcement requires joint agreement
- Financial terms remain confidential
- Project details not disclosed without authorization
- 1–2 year confidentiality window (typical)
