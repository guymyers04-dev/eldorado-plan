Regional Market Overview

El Dorado sits at a strategic crossroads: 36 miles south of Wichita (645K MSA), 130 miles from Oklahoma City (1.4M), 160 miles from Tulsa (980K), and 210 miles from Kansas City (2.2M). This positions the city as a regional hub for logistics, industrial operations, and workforce recruitment from a combined 5.2M person region.

  • Workforce advantage: 18,500 available workers within 30-mile radius (vs. 50K+ in competitor cities)
  • Cost advantage: Land ($24–30K/acre) 20–30% cheaper than peer communities
  • Logistics advantage: US-77 corridor offers 75% faster truck transit vs. I-35 (see US-77 Strategic Map)
  • Positioning: Underserved region between major metros; opportunity to capture logistics/distribution growth

El Dorado's Competitive Advantages

  • Lowest land costs: $24–30K/acre (vs. $32–50K in competitor cities)
  • Lowest industrial rent: $4.50/sf (tied with Joplin; 10–20% cheaper than Fayetteville, Springfield)
  • Strategic corridor access: US-77 provides faster truck transit to multi-state region than I-35 competitors
  • Regional workforce pool: 18,500 available workers within 30 miles; lower wage expectations
  • Quality of life: Outdoor recreation (lake, golf), affordable housing ($120K median), strong schools (USD 490)
