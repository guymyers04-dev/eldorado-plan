## HOW TO USE THESE DOCUMENTS TOGETHER

### For Different Situations

**SITUATION 1: "I need to prepare for the City Commission presentation in 2 days"**
1. Open **PRESENTATION-DECK-TEMPLATES-BY-AUDIENCE.md**, Deck 1 (City Commission)
2. Copy the slide structure and content into PowerPoint/Keynote
3. Reference **STAKEHOLDER-ENGAGEMENT-STRATEGY-COMPREHENSIVE.md**, Section 4 (City Commission Presentation Guide) for detailed speaker notes
4. Print handouts from the materials checklist (one-pager, financial summary, competitive analysis)
5. Review Q&A section for anticipated objections

**SITUATION 2: "I'm getting objections from the EDA Board about the strategy"**
1. Reference **STAKEHOLDER-ENGAGEMENT-STRATEGY-COMPREHENSIVE.md**, Section 2.2 (EDA Board Messaging) for core message and responses
2. Check **STAKEHOLDER-ENGAGEMENT-QUICK-REFERENCE.md** Contingency Plans section (If EDA Board is Unsupportive)
3. Consider scheduling individual meetings with swing voters
4. Prepare business case study from peer city to address credibility concerns

**SITUATION 3: "We need to present to the Chamber, but messaging isn't landing"**
1. Reference **STAKEHOLDER-ENGAGEMENT-STRATEGY-COMPREHENSIVE.md**, Section 2.3 (Chamber Messaging) to ensure value proposition is clear
2. Review **PRESENTATION-DECK-TEMPLATES-BY-AUDIENCE.md**, Deck 2 for Chamber-specific slides
3. Adjust focus from Vision 2040 strategy to member business benefits
4. Emphasize construction jobs (immediate opportunity) and supply chain (near-term)

**SITUATION 4: "Community has concerns about growth disrupting character"**
1. Reference **STAKEHOLDER-ENGAGEMENT-STRATEGY-COMPREHENSIVE.md**, Section 2.4 (Community Concerns & Responses) for talking points
2. Open **PRESENTATION-DECK-TEMPLATES-BY-AUDIENCE.md**, Slide 12 (Address Your Concerns)
3. Prepare detailed examples of how Vision 2040 preserves character (downtown heritage focus, lake recreation vs. commercialization, parks investment)
4. Consider scheduling follow-up neighborhood conversation

**SITUATION 5: "We're in Week 2 and need to know what meetings are happening this week"**
1. Open **STAKEHOLDER-IMPLEMENTATION-QUICK-REFERENCE.md**, Week 2 section
2. Confirm all 5 government partner meetings are scheduled
3. Prepare materials for each meeting (government partner briefing documents)
4. Schedule preliminary calls with brokers and FDI agencies

**SITUATION 6: "We want to measure success of the Chamber presentation"**
1. Reference **STAKEHOLDER-ENGAGEMENT-QUICK-REFERENCE.md**, Success Metrics section (Chamber of Commerce)
2. Metrics: 70%+ attendance, 75%+ positive sentiment, 0 negative comments, Chamber CEO commitment, 10+ member inquiries
3. Create feedback survey from template in document
4. Collect responses during/after presentation
5. Report results to City Commission and EDA Board

**SITUATION 7: "A broker says they're not interested; what do we do?"**
1. Reference **STAKEHOLDER-IMPLEMENTATION-QUICK-REFERENCE.md**, Contingency Plans section
2. Try alternative brokers (backup contacts identified)
3. Hire independent consultant if necessary
4. Consider direct outreach to operators without broker

**SITUATION 8: "What materials still need to be created?"**
1. Reference **STAKEHOLDER-IMPLEMENTATION-QUICK-REFERENCE.md**, Materials Checklist
2. See which items are marked READY vs. CREATE
3. Prioritize based on which presentations are happening soonest
4. Assign creation tasks with 40-50 hour total effort estimate
