## PART 5: BUTLER COUNTY ECOSYSTEM — DEVELOPMENT TIERS & NETWORK EFFECTS

### TIER 1: El Dorado (County Seat, 12,700 people)
**Vision 2040 Role:** Primary job creation, amenities, regional attraction  
**Cumulative Impact:** 1,000+ jobs, $80–120M payroll, $8–15M annual tax revenue

**Key Projects:**
- Data center (500MW) — 200+ direct jobs
- Niche industries (3–5 facilities) — 500+ direct jobs
- Downtown revitalization (60–80 lofts, 15 retail spaces)
- Lake activation (hotel, RV park, water sports) — 150+ jobs
- Health economy (surgical center, dental, senior care) — 200+ jobs
- El Dorado Lake State Park supports 1M+ annual visitors

---

### TIER 2: Regional Supplier Ecosystem
**Cities Benefiting:** Augusta (7.5K), Andover (12K), Rose Hill (3K), Latham (0.5K)  
**Radius:** 10–25 miles from El Dorado  
**Economic Role:** Manufacturing suppliers, logistics, staffing, professional services

**Opportunities:**
- Manufacturing supply chain (machining, welding, fabrication shops)
- Waste/recycling infrastructure (battery materials recovery)
- Logistics distribution centers (3-county coverage)
- Staffing agencies + labor management
- Equipment rental + maintenance facilities
- Professional services overflow (accounting, HR, legal)

**Butler County Comprehensive Plan Integration:**
- Support suppliers locating within reasonable distance (under 15 miles)
- Coordinate workforce training across county via Butler CC
- Develop regional transportation corridors (US-54, US-77, K-177)

---

### TIER 3: Workforce Expansion & Regional Draw
**Population Growth:** +500 people from surrounding counties (housing demand spillover)  
**Butler CC:** Enrollment growth +20% (1,200+ students from expanded service area)  
**Apprenticeship Network:** 200+ participants across 5-county region  
**In-Migration:** Young professionals (25–40 years old) seeking small-city quality of life + good jobs

**County Action Items:**
- Coordinate Butler CC satellite campuses (North Gateway, K-177 corridor)
- Support housing development in adjacent communities (Augusta, Andover, Rose Hill)
- Market region as "Flint Hills Small-City Opportunity Zone"

---

### TIER 4: County Agricultural Base
**Land Use:** 70% agriculture; 18% urban/developed  
**Economic Base:** Commodity crops, livestock, ag equipment dealers  
**Vision 2040 Integration:** AgriTech supply chain, food processing hub, conservation programs

**Key Synergies:**
- Precision Ag Tech manufacturers service 9M-acre region
- Food processing creates demand for local farm products
- Equipment dealers supply region's 50K+ farmers
- Conservation programs protect water resources (El Dorado Lake)
- Regional fairgrounds (August fair, winter events) create tourism draw

**County Commission Action:** Support farmers adopting precision ag, value-added ag processing, conservation practices that protect water supply
