Start Your Business Here

El Dorado for Entrepreneurs

No business license fees. Municipal support for startups. Low commercial real estate costs. Growing customer base. Vision 2040 actively recruits and supports new businesses.

📋

Zero Friction to Start

No business license fees — unlike Kansas City and most cities, El Dorado charges zero annual registration costs to operate a business. Save $500–1,500/year immediately.

Other advantages: Simple zoning for home-based business, quick permitting (60–90 days), and supportive city council that actively wants entrepreneurs to succeed.

🏢

Cheap Real Estate

Commercial space downtown: $4–8/sq ft/month (vs. $15–25 in Wichita). Available buildings, visible locations, high foot traffic from lake tourism and downtown investment.

Specific opportunities: Empty storefronts on Central Avenue (Historic Tax Credit incentives), industrial space near AMAZONE, lake-front commercial for hospitality.

💡

Growing Customer Base

1,200+ new housing units by 2035. Lake draws 200K+ visitors/year. AMAZONE employees (500+) need services. Healthcare expansion (100+ jobs). Butler CC growth.

Specific sectors ready: Restaurants (zero fine dining), outdoor recreation retail, healthcare services, professional services, craft manufacturing.

🤝

City Support & Mentorship

El Dorado Inc. (economic development agency) provides business counseling, connections, and incentives for job-creating startups. SBA resources and SCORE mentoring available.

Vision 2040 framework: If your business aligns with growth sectors (healthcare, tech, tourism, advanced manufacturing), city may offer tax incentives or infrastructure support.

📊

Access to Capital

SBA loans (25–30% less expensive than conventional), Kansas Start-Up loans (lower rates for new businesses), and local bank relationships that know the market.

Crowd-funding friendly community: Successful startups include breweries, retail shops, service businesses — all crowd-funded by local support.

🚀

Success Stories Template

Learn from successful entrepreneurs who've built here: Main Street retailers, craft businesses, healthcare practices, and tech startups. Failure is learning, not shame.

Fastest growing: Lake tourism businesses (restaurants, boat tours, lodging), healthcare services, outdoor retail, and digital/tech services.

Sector Opportunities (Vision 2040)

🏥 Healthcare Services

PT/OT clinics, dental, vision, mental health counseling, home healthcare, wellness coaching. Market is underserved — growing demand.

🍽️ Hospitality & Food

Farm-to-table restaurants, craft brewery, specialty foods, catering, boutique hotel. Lake tourism + downtown demand high.

🏊 Outdoor Recreation

Boat rental, sailing instruction, guides, sporting goods, fitness studios, yoga, outdoor outfitter. Tourism = year-round customers.

🛠️ Professional Services

Accounting, legal, engineering, architecture, consulting. Growing businesses need services. Less competition than metros.

Lower Retirement Cost

Property taxes on your primary residence are held down. No state income tax on Social Security (Kansas law). Utilities lower than national average. Stretch your retirement funds 30%+ further.

  • ✓ No state income tax on SS

Play Outside

El Dorado for Outdoor Enthusiasts

8,400-acre lake, sailing, hiking trails, bird watching, fishing, and a growing network of parks. Outdoor recreation is the city's identity and biggest draw.

⛵

World-Class Sailing

Walnut Valley Sailing Club has been racing on El Dorado Lake for 40+ years. Consistent wind, calm protected areas for beginners, regattas year-round.

Join a sailing community of 200+ members. Weekend races, weeknight socials, annual regattas. Instructional classes for new sailors.

🎣

Fishing Paradise

Bass, crappie, catfish, walleye. Launch ramps, fishing tournaments, guides available. Public fishing areas and private club membership options.

Year-round fishing (ice fishing in winter!). Tournament circuit with prizes. Shallow coves for kayak fishing, deep channels for boat fishing.

🥾

Hiking & Trail Running

15+ miles of developed trails around the lake. Walnut River Greenway (paved, perfect for running). Nature conservancy preserves for off-trail exploration.

Easy to technical trails. Scenic overlooks. Wildlife viewing (deer, eagle, turkey). Trail running community growing.

🚴

Biking Community

Lake loop (8 miles, paved). Mountain biking trails developing. Walnut River Greenway (future expanded). Community bike shop and group rides.

Road biking, mountain biking, casual cruising. Weekly group rides. Bike-friendly businesses with parking/repairs.

🦅

Birding & Nature

200+ bird species. Fall migrations, summer nesting. Nature preserve trails. Field guides & birding groups. Audubon-affiliated conservation area.

Early morning bird walks. Identification workshops. Photography opportunities. Peace and quiet in nature.

🏕️

Camping & Outdoor Base

RV park, tent camping, cabin rentals on the lake. Perfect base for regional road trips (Kansas prairie, Colorado mountains, Ozarks).

Full hookup sites. Casual atmosphere. Community campfire events. Gateway to outdoor adventures across the region.
