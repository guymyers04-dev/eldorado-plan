# El Dorado Vision 2040 Beyond: Strategic Framework
## Long-Term Vision & Legacy (2040–2055+)

---

## Overview

Vision 2040 Beyond extends El Dorado's strategic horizon past 2040 into a mature, globally competitive innovation economy. By 2050, El Dorado will be recognized as a model for sustainable rural development where climate resilience, innovation leadership, and multigenerational equity align to create lasting prosperity.

**Core Mission:** Build upon Vision 2040's foundation to create a thriving, regenerative, globally connected community that works for all generations and leaves the region healthier, more prosperous, and more resilient than it was in 2026.

---

## Part 1: Strategic Framework

### Vision Pillars

1. **Sustainability First**
   - Net-zero carbon city operations by 2050
   - Carbon-neutral regional economy (regenerative agriculture sequestering emissions)
   - Water stewardship and resilience
   - Net-positive environmental outcomes

2. **Innovation Leadership**
   - Research institutions and innovation labs embedded in the city
   - Testing ground for 21st-century technologies (autonomous agriculture, precision manufacturing, clean energy)
   - $50M+ innovation ecosystem investment

3. **Global Connectivity**
   - Despite rural Kansas location, digitally and logistically connected to world markets
   - Gigabit broadband + world-class logistics infrastructure
   - International partnerships and talent networks

4. **Generational Equity**
   - Youth: opportunity and wealth-building accessible in home community
   - Working adults: competitive wages, benefits, career pathways
   - Elders: dignity, healthcare, meaningful community participation
   - All demographics: pathways to ownership and prosperity

5. **Learning Economy**
   - K-12 excellence (K–12 → 95%+ graduation, 70%+ higher education participation)
   - Higher education: Butler CC evolves to 4-year university
   - Workforce development: just-in-time skills training
   - Lifelong learning: 200+ hours per resident per year (vs. 80 nationally)

6. **Community Resilience**
   - Deep social networks and civic participation (75%+ engaged)
   - Mutual aid and institutional trust
   - Prepared for climate shocks, economic displacement, technological disruption
   - Conflict resolution and social cohesion

## Part 2: Climate Resilience & Adaptation (2040–2055)

### Water Resilience Infrastructure
**Goal by 2050:** 80% water independence through rainwater harvesting, aquifer recharge, wastewater reclamation.

**Key Initiatives:**
- City-wide green infrastructure (permeable pavements, bioswales, retention ponds)
- El Dorado Lake as resilience reserve + recreation asset
- Walnut River corridor restored as functioning floodplain ecosystem
- All new development: water neutrality requirement
- Budget: $45M over 15 years

**Expected Impact:**
- Flood risk reduced 60%+
- Groundwater levels stabilize/rise
- Recreation and habitat restored
- Industrial/agricultural water security improved

### Urban Forest & Cooling Strategy
**Goal by 2050:** Triple tree canopy from 12% to 35% of city.

**Key Initiatives:**
- Plant 10,000 native trees (priority: east-side neighborhoods, industrial corridors)
- Tax credits for private property tree planting
- Rooftop garden/green roof mandates on new development
- Walnut River linear park as "cool corridor"
- Budget: $12M over 15 years

**Expected Impact:**
- Neighborhood temperatures 3-5°C lower
- Heat-related illness reduced 50%+
- Building cooling costs cut 30-40%
- Stormwater absorption improved

### 100% Renewable Energy Transition
**Goal by 2050:** 100% renewable energy (city operations + grid-wide commitment).

**Key Initiatives:**
- Long-term PPAs (power purchase agreements) with Kansas wind/solar operators
- Distributed solar: rooftop on public buildings + community solar gardens + marketplace for excess generation
- Battery + thermal storage for grid stability
- All city vehicles electric + ubiquitous public EV charging
- All buildings built/renovated after 2035: Passive House standards (75% reduction in heating/cooling)
- Budget: $80M over 20 years

**Expected Impact:**
- Operating carbon: net-zero
- Regional energy independence achieved
- 800–1,200 jobs in renewable manufacturing/installation
- Energy costs stable/declining vs. grid escalation

### Regenerative Agriculture Ecosystem
**Goal by 2050:** Butler County becomes model for regenerative practices (soil carbon rebuilding, biodiversity, farmer profitability).

**Key Initiatives:**
- Carbon farming cooperative: pay farmers for carbon credits (cover crops, reduced tillage, crop rotation)
- Agritech innovation hub: startups/research focused on soil health, precision ag, climate-resilient varieties
- Direct-to-consumer food system: farmer's markets + farm-to-table restaurants
- Soil health testing infrastructure + extension services
- Budget: $85M (public + private) over 15 years

**Expected Impact:**
- Regional soil carbon: sequestering more than regional emissions
- Farmer incomes: +15-25% through premium markets + carbon credits
- Ag jobs: +200-300 in agritech and value-added sectors
- Biodiversity: native species populations recovering

### Disaster Resilience Planning
**Goal by 2050:** Critical infrastructure hardened against extreme weather, supply chain disruptions.

**Key Initiatives:**
- Cooling centers + emergency shelters in every district (backup power, water, supplies)
- Critical infrastructure audit + redundancy upgrades (hospitals, water treatment, power, comms)
- Community early warning system (heat, flooding, air quality via SMS/app/radio)
- Regional mutual aid network with Butler County communities
- Indigenous knowledge integration (traditional ecological practices)
- Budget: $15M over 15 years
