## 🏠 HOUSING STRATEGY — STRONG BUT FUNDING UNCLEAR

### What's Covered ✅

**Housing Strategy Document (`housing-strategy.html`):**
- **Problem:** El Dorado median home price/income = 3.46x (above healthy 2.5-3.0x for rural Kansas)
- **Competitive disadvantage:** Emporia 2.97x, Dodge City 2.94x — El Dorado is overpriced relative to wages
- **Phase 1 target:** 200-300 new units (mixed-income: entry-level $130-160K, mid-range $180-250K, premium $280-400K+)
- **Infrastructure:** 5-7 miles roads ($2-3M), utilities ($1.5-2M), schools (USD 490 assessment), parks/retail ($1-2M) = $5-8M municipal investment
- **Financing:** EDA CDBG $1-2M, KHRB $500K-1M, private developer $3-5M (with tax abatement), philanthropic $300-500K
- **Incentives:** Tax abatement 5-10 years, infrastructure cost-sharing, density bonuses, fee waivers
- **Success metrics:** 200-300 units, home price/income improved to 3.0-3.2x, +500-800 new residents, +2-3% annual population growth
- **Critical success factors:** Coordinate with FDI recruitment, prioritize youth/young families, partner with developers, market aggressively
