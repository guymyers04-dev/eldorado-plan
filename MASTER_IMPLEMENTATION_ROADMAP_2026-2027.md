# El Dorado Vision 2040 — Master Implementation Roadmap
## Integrating All 22 Niche Enhancements into Unified Project Plan

**Document Date:** June 29, 2026  
**Planning Horizon:** July 2026 – April 2027 (20 weeks)  
**Status:** Ready for execution  
**Total Investment:** 280-370 hours of focused work  
**Expected Outcomes:** 22 tools deployed, 50%+ resident engagement, measurable trust increases

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The El Dorado Vision 2040 website currently has Phase 1 (scroll-spy nav) and Phase 2A-B (stakeholder directory + 2 engagement tools) complete. This roadmap adds 22 niche enhancement ideas across 5 phases that will:

1. **Address resident concerns** (affordability, jobs, safety, infrastructure)
2. **Build confidence** through visual proof + peer examples + transparent data
3. **Enable participation** (calendar, feedback loops, opportunities)
4. **Drive engagement** (community champions, goal-oriented tools)
5. **Measure impact** (surveys, data dashboards, quarterly feedback)

**By April 2027:**
- Website grows from 25 to 28+ pages (new dedicated pages for enhanced tools)
- Interactive tools increase from 2 (comment generator, countdown) to 22+ across all sections
- Engagement tools tie together in a cohesive ecosystem
- Residents have clear pathways to: jobs, housing, community participation, feedback
- City has data infrastructure for measurement + responsive governance

## RISKS & MITIGATION

### Risk 1: Data Availability (City Cooperation)
**Risk:** City departments slow to provide data (project status, tax rates, event calendars)  
**Impact:** Delays in phases 1, 4, 5  
**Mitigation:**
- Establish data-sharing MOU with City Manager upfront
- Designate single point of contact (Vision 2040 coordinator)
- Monthly data updates (automated if possible)

### Risk 2: Research Interviews
**Risk:** Peer city managers, potential champions unavailable/unresponsive  
**Impact:** Delays in E2, C1  
**Mitigation:**
- Begin outreach 4-6 weeks before each phase launch
- Have backup plans (use secondary data if interviews unavailable)
- Compensate for interview time if needed

### Risk 3: Photography/Videos
**Risk:** Photos or renderings hard to source (private property, permission issues)  
**Impact:** Delays in P2, P3, C1  
**Mitigation:**
- Use Street View as backup for current building photos
- Negotiate architect renderings access upfront with city planning
- Have stock photo library as fallback

### Risk 4: Scope Creep
**Risk:** Feature requests expand scope mid-phase  
**Impact:** Timeline slips, budget overruns  
**Mitigation:**
- Lock scope before phase launch (use checklist)
- Create "Phase 6+" backlog for out-of-scope requests
- Monthly steering meetings to assess progress

### Risk 5: Technical Challenges
**Risk:** Integration complexity (APIs, databases, responsive design) takes longer than estimated  
**Impact:** Timeline slips  
**Mitigation:**
- Spike/prototype highest-risk items (maps, APIs) in week 1
- Allocate 15% contingency in schedule
- Plan testing + QA from day 1
