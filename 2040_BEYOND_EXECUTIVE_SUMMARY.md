# Vision 2040 Beyond: Executive Summary (One-Page)

## El Dorado's Post-2040 Strategy: 2050 as the Target

**Vision:** El Dorado by 2050 is a globally competitive, climate-resilient innovation hub where economic prosperity and environmental health reinforce each other, and all generations can thrive.

---

## Six Strategic Pillars

| Pillar | 2050 Outcome | Key Investment |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Climate Resilience** | Net-zero city; region sequesters more carbon than it emits | $157M (water, forest, renewable, regenerative ag, resilience) |
| **Innovation Economy** | 50+ venture-backed startups; $500M+ capital raised; 500+ innovation jobs | $350M (campus, VC fund, infrastructure) |
| **Next-Gen Industries** | $2B+ economic activity: ag-tech, renewables, biotech, health-tech, robotics, impact | $500M+ (manufacturing, R&D, facilities) |
| **Regional Leadership** | Butler County model for rural excellence; 5-county healthcare/education hub | $550M+ (transportation, healthcare, education, digital) |
| **Knowledge Economy** | 30% workforce in knowledge sectors; continuous learning culture | $200M+ (education, talent, creative, partnerships) |
| **Generational Equity** | All generations (youth, adults, elders) thrive; disparities narrowed | Embedded across all initiatives |

## Climate Resilience: $157M Investment

- **Water Independence (80% by 2050):** Green infrastructure, aquifer recharge, wastewater reclamation | $45M
- **Urban Cooling:** 10K trees planted, canopy 12%→35%, rooftop gardens | $12M
- **100% Renewable Energy:** Wind/solar PPAs, distributed solar, EV charging, Passive House standards | $80M
- **Regenerative Agriculture:** Carbon farming cooperative, agritech hub, soil restoration | $85M (public+private)
- **Disaster Resilience:** Cooling centers, critical infrastructure hardening, early warning systems | $15M

## Why Now? Why Us?

**El Dorado's Unique Assets:**
1. **Geography:** 29 miles from Wichita; I-35 intersection; US-77 corridor; rail connectivity
2. **Water:** 8,400-acre lake; abundant groundwater; flowing Walnut River
3. **Talent:** Butler Community College (2nd largest in Kansas); USD 490 excellence
4. **Innovation Foundation:** Vision 2040 establishes diversified economy by 2040
5. **Community:** Cohesive, growth-oriented; proven project implementation capacity
6. **Regional Role:** Natural hub for Butler County and southern Kansas

**2040 Beyond Window:**
- Vision 2040 foundation completed (2040)
- Climate adaptation urgency (2050 is critical threshold)
- Rural innovation opportunity (post-pandemic remote work shift)
- Global talent mobility (immigration reform landscape shift)
- Clean energy cost curves (at parity by 2040)
