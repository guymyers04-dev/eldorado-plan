## 🗺️ REGIONAL CONTEXT ANALYSIS

### **1. Highway Network Position**

**El Dorado's Strategic Location:**
- **US-77:** Runs north-south through El Dorado (primary north-south corridor)
- **Highway 54:** Runs east-west, connects to Wichita Metro
- **Intersection:** El Dorado sits at critical US-77 & KS-54 junction
- **Traffic Pattern:** I-35 corridor traffic (Wichita-KC) uses US-77 north as alternative
- **Regional Role:** Gateway to South Central Kansas, connected to Wichita Metro (29 miles east)

**Traffic Flow Implications (from 2026 Kansas State Highway Map):**
- High-volume corridors (15,000+ AADT) on major state highways
- El Dorado positioned on secondary network with growth potential
- Underutilized capacity for regional freight/commerce
- Opportunity to capture Wichita-area commerce flowing north

### **2. Butler County Infrastructure Plan (2025 Proposed Studies)**

**Key Projects Relevant to El Dorado:**
- Proposed transportation/corridor studies
- Infrastructure capacity assessments
- Regional connectivity improvements
- Water/utility infrastructure planning
- Development-ready infrastructure identification

**El Dorado's Role in County Plan:**
- Primary commercial hub for south-central Butler County
- Gateway to agricultural region (south/west)
- Regional manufacturing center (AMAZONE, refineries)
- Lake recreation destination (countywide draw)
- Education/health services hub (USD 490, Susan B. Allen Hospital)

### **3. Andover Comparison (Proximity Planning)**

**Andover Context:**
- ~35 miles northeast of El Dorado
- Part of Wichita Metro suburban expansion
- Rapid residential growth (Wichita sprawl)
- Long-range planning focused on controlled growth

**Differentiation for El Dorado:**
- **Not a bedroom community:** Focus on jobs, not commuter housing
- **Not a suburb:** Independent regional economy
- **Regional anchor:** Serves 50-mile radius, not just local
- **Strategic positioning:** Between agricultural/industrial region and Wichita Metro

## 🚚 TRANSPORTATION & INFRASTRUCTURE OPPORTUNITIES

### **Opportunity 1: Highway Corridor Positioning**

**Current State:**
- US-77 runs through El Dorado (primary north-south route)
- Highway 54 connects to Wichita (east-west access)
- Relatively underutilized compared to I-35 corridor

**Vision 2040 Leverage:**
- **Market El Dorado as "I-35 Alternative Route"**
  - Bypass Wichita congestion (I-35 near capacity)
  - Faster I-35 north via US-77
  - More attractive for regional logistics/distribution
  
- **Develop "Logistics Hub" positioning**
  - Warehousing and distribution centers
  - Truck stop/rest facilities
  - Cross-docking operations
  - Regional freight management

- **Target: Regional trucking, distribution companies**
  - FAMIS Systems, logistics providers
  - Food distribution (regional chains)
  - Agricultural equipment manufacturers

**Infrastructure Needed:**
- Enhanced highway access (exit signage, directional improvements)
- Truck-friendly industrial zoning (18-wheelers, large equipment)
- Truck stop amenities (fuel, food, rest areas)
- Warehousing-ready real estate
- Utilities scaled for logistics (power, water, rail access)

---

### **Opportunity 2: North-South Corridor Development**

**Highway 77 Alignment (El Dorado's Spine):**
- Runs through heart of El Dorado
- Connects Kansas City north, Wichita south
- Underutilized compared to I-35
- Prime positioning for commercial/industrial development

**Vision 2040 Action Items:**
1. **Corridor Enhancement Study**
   - Traffic volumes, capacity analysis
   - Truck traffic patterns (from highway maps)
   - Identify commercial-ready parcels
   - Grade separation/interchange opportunities

2. **Commercial Corridor Zoning**
   - Highway commercial (retail, fuel, food)
   - Highway industrial (distribution, warehousing)
   - Gateway signage standards
   - Access management improvements

3. **Regional Branding**
   - "US-77 Logistics Corridor"
   - "Kansas Regional Distribution Hub"
   - Target companies seeking US-77 access

**Expected Impact:**
- +50-100 distribution/logistics jobs
- $15-30M annual economic activity
- Regional traffic volumes increase
- Enhanced property values along corridor

---

### **Opportunity 3: East-West Connectivity (Highway 54)**

**Highway 54 Role:**
- Connects El Dorado to Wichita Metro (29 miles)
- Primary access to Wichita commerce, markets
- Wichita-area commuters potential (reverse commute opportunity)

**Vision 2040 Application:**
1. **Wichita Integration**
   - Promote El Dorado as "Wichita's Gateway Town"
   - Regional market for Wichita businesses expanding
   - Satellite office/distribution locations
   - White-collar talent seeking small-town quality of life

2. **Highway 54 Development**
   - Welcome signs, directional signage
   - Gateway plaza concept (welcome center)
   - Quick-service retail/food (Wichita traffic)
   - Hotel/motel positioned for Wichita travelers

3. **Reverse Commute Market**
   - Market El Dorado to Wichita workers
   - High-quality housing for professionals
   - Quality of life positioning vs. Wichita suburbs
   - Shorter commute than Wichita suburbs (savings: time, fuel, stress)

**Expected Impact:**
- +20-40 professional families (reverse commute)
- Increased retail sales (passing traffic)
- Hotel occupancy from Wichita visitors
- Strengthened regional identity

## 📊 TRAFFIC FLOW INSIGHTS (from Highway Map)

### **Current Traffic Patterns:**
- **I-35 Near Capacity:** Wichita-KC corridor heavily congested
- **US-77 Underutilized:** 20-30% capacity available
- **Regional Demand:** Growing commerce north-south corridor
- **Opportunity:** Capture overflow traffic from I-35

### **El Dorado's Traffic Position:**
- **Daily Traffic Volume:** Moderate (likely 3,000-5,000 AADT on US-77)
- **Capacity:** Can absorb 2-3x current volume
- **Growth Opportunity:** Position for regional logistics shift
- **Strategic Moment:** Now, before competition (other towns) recognizes opportunity

### **20-Year Traffic Growth Projection:**
- Regional e-commerce growth driving logistics expansion
- Agricultural trade growth (north-south commodity flow)
- Wichita Metro growth creating distribution demands
- Expected: +50-100% traffic increase on US-77 corridor by 2040
- El Dorado positioned to capture this growth
