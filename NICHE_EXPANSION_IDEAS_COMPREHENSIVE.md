# El Dorado Vision 2040: Niche Expansion Ideas
## Comprehensive Research & Brainstorm for 9 Strategic Sections

**Prepared:** June 29, 2026  
**Audience:** City Leadership, Web Editorial, Engagement Strategy  
**Purpose:** Identify underexplored opportunities that differentiate Vision 2040 and deepen community/investor engagement

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document identifies 50+ **niche, specific, and often-overlooked expansion ideas** across all 9 Vision 2040 sections. Each idea is grounded in:
- Small-city realities (budget constraints, population limits, existing assets)
- Comparative benchmarking (what peer markets are doing)
- Emerging trends in their domain
- Actionable micro-insights (not just macro statistics)
- Why it matters specifically for El Dorado

**Format:** 5–10 ideas per section, organized by theme with brief context on implementation and impact.

## 1. ECONOMY & GROWTH
### Current State
6 priority districts, pro hyperscale section, health economy, anchor partners, manufacturing focus

### NICHE EXPANSION IDEAS

#### **1.1 Niche Sector: Agribusiness Technology & Equipment Manufacturing**
**Why:** German AMAZONE win proves international ag-tech manufacturers see El Dorado as viable. But most expansion messaging focuses on scale (data centers, FDI). Miss: the micro-supply chain.
- **Idea:** Create "AgTech Midwest Hub" sub-page mapping: AMAZONE supply chain partners, Ag equipment OEMs within 200-mile radius, precision agriculture software startups, drone/sensor manufacturers
- **Why it matters:** Most ag-tech supply chain jobs are $45–65K (workforce-quality wages), female-inclusive (25–35% of workforce), and sticky (equipment mfg job tenure 8+ years vs. gig economy)
- **Data viz:** Interactive map of 50–75 ag-equipment manufacturers in 200-mile radius; supply chain tiers (Tier 1 OEMs, Tier 2 component, Tier 3 materials); AMAZONE's sourcing radius marked
- **Action:** Reach out to 10–15 existing ag-equipment manufacturers for testimonials: "Why we located near El Dorado"
- **Timeline:** 2–3 weeks to map, 2–4 weeks to collect interviews

---

#### **1.2 Niche Opportunity: Data Center Soft Services & Operations Jobs**
**Why:** Hyperscale data centers get attention, but most residents don't know the job spectrum. 80–90% of data center jobs are operations (security, maintenance, HVAC, cabling, environmental monitoring), not tech.
- **Idea:** Create "Data Center Career Pathway" section: Operator, Technician, Environmental Specialist, Network Admin salary ranges (realistic $50–90K for skilled trades); prerequisites & certifications (Google Career Certificates, vendor certs); training partnerships
- **Why it matters:** De-mystifies data center jobs. Most small-city residents think "need PhD in computer science." Reality: high school + 6-month cert = $55K starting salary
- **Data viz:** Career pathway flowchart (HS Diploma → Google Cert → $55K Operator → 5-yr → $72K Senior Tech); comparison vs. HF Sinclair refinery jobs (similar pay, better growth trajectory)
- **Action:** Contact 3–5 existing data center operators (AWS, Google, Meta) for "day in the life" content; partner with Butler CC on curriculum mapping
- **Timeline:** 1 week to draft, 2 weeks for operator interviews

---

#### **1.3 Niche Sector: Logistics & Distribution Hub (US-77 Corridor Optimization)**
**Why:** Vision 2040 mentions I-35 proximity, but US-77 is the stealth advantage. US-77 connects I-35 (Wichita) to I-70 (Kansas City)—avoiding I-35 congestion. Retailers (Amazon, Walmart, Target) are actively seeking last-mile hubs on alternative routes.
- **Idea:** Create "El Dorado as Midwest Logistics Hub" section: Map US-77 corridor advantages vs. I-35 corridor; traffic flow data (KDOT); comparative rents (El Dorado warehousing $4–5/sq ft vs. Olathe/Lenexa $7–8/sq ft); last-mile delivery time advantage to KC/Wichita/Oklahoma City metros
- **Why it matters:** Logistics hubs create 150–300 jobs at $35–50K, require less specialized infrastructure than manufacturing, have faster decision-to-occupancy timelines (12–18 months vs. 24+ for mfg)
- **Data viz:** "Route Optimization" map showing I-35 vs. US-77 delivery times to top 15 retail DCs in Kansas/Oklahoma/Missouri; cost-per-mile comparison; potential anchor tenants (Amazon Fresh MF, regional DCs for Sysco, Martin Brower, etc.)
- **Action:** Contact 5–8 logistics brokers (CBRE Industrial, JLL, Cushman & Wakefield) for: "Why secondary markets on US-77 are gaining share from I-35"
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks research, 1 week interviews

---

#### **1.4 Niche Opportunity: "Secondhand Economy" & Circular Manufacturing Supply Chain**
**Why:** Emerging post-consumer electronics, packaging, and materials repurposing sector. Companies like Greystone, Renewd, and AMP Robotics are building disassembly/refurbishment/materials recovery facilities in secondary markets (wages, land, utilities cheaper). 
- **Idea:** Create "Circular Economy Recruitment" section: Market El Dorado to e-waste, packaging recovery, and materials science companies looking to locate disassembly/sorting/refurbishment centers
- **Why it matters:** 200–400 jobs at $38–55K; lower capital investment than manufacturing; aligns with ESG/carbon goals (investor appeal); recurring feedstock (consumer electronics, packaging are predictable volume)
- **Data viz:** Benchmark: where do leading circular economy companies locate (e.g., Greystone in upstate NY, Renewd in Southeast); show El Dorado's cost/utility/logistics advantage
- **Action:** Research 8–10 leading circular economy companies; identify decision-makers; build prospect list
- **Timeline:** 1 week research, 1 week prospect outreach

---

#### **1.5 Niche Data Visualization: "Cost of Doing Business" Real-Time Tracker**
**Why:** Businesses want to know: rent, utilities, labor, permitting speed, tax burden—all in one place. Most EDO sites bury this in PDFs.
- **Idea:** Interactive dashboard comparing El Dorado vs. benchmark competitors (Emporia, Great Bend, Hutchinson, Wichita suburbs) across 10 operational cost categories
- **Why it matters:** Reduces comparison friction. Shows El Dorado's 15–25% cost advantage clearly. Investor/site selector browsers spend 45 sec on page; give them the data in 45 sec
- **Data viz:** Side-by-side comparison matrix: industrial rent/sq ft, office rent, utility costs (electric, water, gas per 10K sq ft), labor cost index, permit turnaround days, tax burden (effective rate on $50M facility)
- **Action:** Gather 2026 Q2 data from municipal sources, utility companies, commercial real estate brokers
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks data collection and validation

---

#### **1.6 Niche Opportunity: HF Sinclair Supply Chain & Refinery Adjacent Sector Growth**
**Why:** HF Sinclair refinery is largest employer (400+ people) but rarely mentioned in growth narrative. Refineries create supply chain ecosystems (logistics, testing, equipment rental, maintenance, chemical distribution).
- **Idea:** Create "Energy Sector Ecosystem" section: Map HF Sinclair supply chain partners, third-party maintenance contractors, logistics partners; identify gaps where new businesses could locate. Example: specialty lubricant distributor, equipment rental, environmental services, heat recovery systems vendor
- **Why it matters:** Jobs 75–150 per opportunity; lower recruitment risk than new-to-area companies; proven demand from anchor tenant
- **Data viz:** Supply chain network diagram (HF Sinclair as center); concentric circles showing Tier 1 suppliers (currently within 50 mi), Tier 2 suppliers (100–200 mi), and opportunities for Tier 1/2 operators to relocate closer
- **Action:** Interview HF Sinclair procurement/supply chain manager; identify top 10–15 suppliers; assess relocation interest
- **Timeline:** 1 week interviews, 1 week mapping

---

#### **1.7 Niche Research: Small Business "Founder Relocation" Program Benchmarking**
**Why:** Austin, Des Moines, Wichita all running founder programs (subsidies, co-working, startup capital access). El Dorado could uniquely offer: cheap office space, low competition, founder-friendly town vibe.
- **Idea:** Research competitor "founder relocation" programs; design El Dorado's version: $5–10K relocation incentive for first 10 tech/software startups; free co-working year 1; "intro to city" program
- **Why it matters:** Founder-led companies typically create 3–5 employees per founder within 3 years; high-margin, exportable income; builds innovation narrative beyond manufacturing
- **Data viz:** Comparative program matrix: Austin, Des Moines, Wichita founder programs vs. proposed El Dorado program (cost to city, terms, cap, typical applicant profile)
- **Action:** Call 5–8 founder relocation programs; build business case study
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks research, 1 week business case

---

#### **1.8 Niche Data: "Economic Resilience Index" — Show Why El Dorado Is Recession-Proof**
**Why:** Investors worry about single-industry dependency. El Dorado's sector mix (refining, manufacturing, government jobs, ag, health) is naturally diversified. Show it.
- **Idea:** Create "Economic Resilience" dashboard: (a) sector employment distribution (pie chart); (b) recession impact scenarios—show how 2008 recession affected El Dorado vs. peer metros (employment, tax base, housing prices); (c) forward diversification plan (post-2030 sector mix)
- **Why it matters:** De-risks investor perception. Shows leadership is intentionally building resilience, not hoping for best
- **Data viz:** Recession scenario modeling (simple: if manufacturing down 15%, refining down 8%, government stable, what's aggregate impact?); compare to Wichita (heavily aerospace) or Oklahoma City (heavily oil/gas) single-sector risk
- **Action:** Source historical employment data (BLS, EMSI, local sources); build recession scenario model
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks data + modeling

## 2. HOUSING & DEVELOPMENT
### Current State
Affordability, new construction, workforce housing, inventory

### NICHE EXPANSION IDEAS

#### **2.1 Niche Demographic: "Aging in Place" Housing Ecosystem**
**Why:** El Dorado's median age 41.5 (vs. US 38.5). Boomer cohort (60–75) represents 18% of population—largest single demographic. Empty nesters want to stay, not move to nursing homes. But no housing product designed for them.
- **Idea:** Create "Active Aging Community" section: design criteria for "55+ walkable neighborhoods" (flat terrain, close to downtown, mixed housing types: single-family, townhome, condo); show market opportunity (national trend: 5x growth in 55+ communities 2010–2025)
- **Why it matters:** 55+ households have highest disposable income; lowest school-tax impact; proven development model (Robson Ranch, Sun City, Del Webb); 250–400 unit communities generate $25–40M private investment
- **Data viz:** Demographic pyramid showing age distribution; "aging in place" preference data (national survey); comparable 55+ projects' success metrics (absorption rate, price point, amenity ROI)
- **Action:** Identify 1–2 compatible development sites (flat, close to downtown, utilities); reach out to 3–5 senior housing developers for preliminary feasibility
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks planning, 3–4 weeks developer outreach

---

#### **2.2 Niche Segment: Multi-Generational Housing & Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs)**
**Why:** 20% of US households now multi-generational (grandparent, parent, child). No Vision 2040 messaging on this. But it's realistic for El Dorado—families want to stay together, lower housing costs.
- **Idea:** Create "Multi-Gen Housing Design Guide" section: Show examples of duplexes, mother-in-law suites, accessory dwelling units (ADUs); streamline zoning/permitting for these types; market to families ($35K–55K income) considering the choice: move away for affordability, or stay in El Dorado with multi-gen housing
- **Why it matters:** Unlocks 100–200 units of "hidden" housing (conversions, ADUs) without new construction; appeals to aging-in-place + young professionals simultaneously; politically safe (family values narrative)
- **Data viz:** "Multi-Gen Housing Types" visual guide with floor plans; zoning code comparison (El Dorado permitting ease vs. peer cities); ROI for homeowner adding ADU (rental income offset)
- **Action:** Survey current zoning for ADU-friendliness; identify 2–3 pilot blocks; partner with housing nonprofits on "ADU financing" program
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks zoning review, 2 weeks community outreach

---

#### **2.3 Niche Research: "Workforce Housing Finance" Benchmarking & Program Design**
**Why:** Vision 2040 targets 200+ workforce housing units by 2028, but no public messaging on how financing works. LIHTC? Bonds? TIF? Most residents (and prospective businesses) confused.
- **Idea:** Create "Housing Finance Playbook" section: Explainer on LIHTC, historic tax credits, HUD bonds, TIF-funded infrastructure, down payment assistance programs; show real examples (AMAZONE employees, HF Sinclair workers)—"Here's how housing gets funded"
- **Why it matters:** Demystifies process for developers, residents, elected officials; removes friction in housing approval process; shows proactive leadership on workforce housing (investor signal)
- **Data viz:** Flowchart: "How a Workforce Housing Project Gets Built" (land acquisition → financing stack → permitting → construction → occupancy); case study of 1–2 comparable projects (funding sources, timeline, per-unit cost)
- **Action:** Interview 2–3 housing finance specialists (LIHTC syndicators, state housing agencies); map existing financing programs El Dorado can access
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks research, 1 week interviews

---

#### **2.4 Niche Opportunity: "Modular Housing" Acceleration Program**
**Why:** Modular construction 20–30% faster, 10–15% cheaper, 40% less jobsite waste. Companies like Factory OS, Plant Prefab entering secondary markets. El Dorado could position as modular housing hub.
- **Idea:** Propose "Modular Housing Innovation District": Recruit modular housing manufacturer to locate near North Gateway District; use their product for 50–100 units of Vision 2040 housing; create case study for national scaling
- **Why it matters:** Solves El Dorado's construction timeline constraint (fast delivery for workforce housing); attracts novel sector (modular manufacturing); creates export/branding opportunity ("Built in El Dorado, shipped nationally")
- **Data viz:** Modular vs. traditional construction timeline comparison; cost per unit; environmental impact; photos of modular assembly process and finished product
- **Action:** Research 5–8 leading modular housing manufacturers; assess relocation interest; identify compatible site
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks research, 2 weeks site assessment

---

#### **2.5 Niche Data: "Neighborhood Character Preservation" + New Housing Growth**
**Why:** Growth threatens neighborhood identity. Show El Dorado how to add 200 units without demolishing what makes it special.
- **Idea:** Create "Growth Without Loss" section: Design standards for new development that respect El Dorado's character (lot sizes, setbacks, architectural guidelines echoing oil-boom era); show examples of towns that grew AND preserved character (e.g., Bend, OR; Bozeman, MT)
- **Why it matters:** Builds resident support for growth; attracts design-conscious newcomers; increases property values in existing neighborhoods (halo effect); differentiates from sprawl
- **Data viz:** Before/after comparisons (comparable towns); design standards visual guide (acceptable materials, colors, roof pitches, lot sizes); character study of El Dorado's distinctive features (oil-boom architecture, wide tree-lined streets, downtown density)
- **Action:** Conduct architectural character study; draft design guidelines; show 3–4 comparable towns' case studies
- **Timeline:** 3–4 weeks character study + design standards

---

#### **2.6 Niche Segment: "First-Time Homebuyer" Support Ecosystem**
**Why:** Down payment is biggest barrier for workforce-wage households. Most cities have 0–2 down payment assistance programs. El Dorado could lead.
- **Idea:** Build "First-Time Homebuyer Hub" section: Aggregate all down payment assistance programs (city, state, federal, nonprofit); create "path to homeownership" guide; partner with local lenders to offer preferential rates for El Dorado residents
- **Why it matters:** Converts renter to owner (wealth building); increases housing demand; builds long-term community attachment (owners vote in local elections, invest in neighborhoods more than renters)
- **Data viz:** Step-by-step homebuying guide; down payment assistance matrix (income eligibility, max assistance, application process); monthly mortgage payment calculator vs. rent comparison for key wage bands ($32K, $42K, $52K)
- **Action:** Audit 20+ down payment assistance programs; build aggregator; partner with 2–3 local lenders
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks program research, 2 weeks partnerships

---

#### **2.7 Niche Data: "Housing Demand Forecast" Scenario Planning**
**Why:** Show residents the connection between economic growth (data centers, FDI) and housing demand. Build support for near-term growth.
- **Idea:** Create "Housing Demand Projections" section: If Vision 2040 succeeds, what housing is needed? Build scenarios: conservative (500 new residents by 2030 = 180 units), moderate (1,000 residents = 350 units), aggressive (1,500 = 525 units). Show which neighborhoods, price points, and development phasing makes sense
- **Why it matters:** Prospective residents want to know: "Will there be a place for me?" Developers want to know demand profile before investing. Elected officials want clear justification for zoning changes
- **Data viz:** Housing need waterfall (population growth → household formation → unit demand); gap analysis (current permits in progress vs. demand); scenario planning (conservative/moderate/aggressive with corresponding prices/neighborhoods)
- **Action:** Build demand model using BLS, state demographer, local population forecasts; validate with regional planners
- **Timeline:** 1–2 weeks modeling

## 3. PLACE & RECREATION
### Current State
6 detail pages (lake, downtown, arts, tourism, parks, beautification); interactive maps emerging

### NICHE EXPANSION IDEAS

#### **3.1 Niche Opportunity: "Public Spaces Design" Showcase & Replication Program**
**Why:** Vision 2040 talks about downtown, parks, lake, but doesn't showcase design thinking behind public space creation. Yet design drives engagement and property values.
- **Idea:** Create "Placemaking Playbook" section: Show El Dorado's best public spaces with design analysis (why they work, who uses them, when, maintenance costs); provide replicable design templates for smaller gathering spaces; position El Dorado as "design-forward town"
- **Why it matters:** Attracts creative professionals, entrepreneurs, young families (place-quality matters more than price for these demographics); increases foot traffic and spending in downtown; builds brand differentiation
- **Data viz:** "Before/After" photos of renovated public spaces; design teardown (spatial arrangement, lighting, materials, wayfinding, accessibility); crowd heat maps (when/how spaces are used)
- **Action:** Photograph/analyze 5–8 El Dorado public spaces; partner with design school (KU, Kansas State) on formal design analysis; create pattern language for replication
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks photography, 3 weeks design analysis

---

#### **3.2 Niche Experience: "Micro-Mobility & First/Last-Mile Transport" Hub Design**
**Why:** E-bike adoption up 85% 2021–2024. Yet most small cities have no bike infrastructure beyond "share the road." El Dorado could lead.
- **Idea:** Create "Walkable/Bikeable El Dorado" section: Design network of protected bike lanes connecting downtown → lake → parks → North Gateway residential; map e-bike rental stations; show how micro-mobility reduces car trips, increases retail spending downtown, improves health outcomes
- **Why it matters:** Attracts younger demographics (25–40); increases downtown retail spending (bikers stop at cafes); reduces parking burden; improves public health (walkable towns = lower obesity rates)
- **Data viz:** Existing vs. proposed bike network maps; case studies (Boulder, Madison, Portland); cost/benefit analysis (bike infrastructure investment vs. car parking needs avoided)
- **Action:** Partner with regional bike advocacy groups; conduct origin-destination survey (where do residents want to bike?); design conceptual network
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks survey + planning, 2 weeks design

---

#### **3.3 Niche Data: "El Dorado Lake Visitor Economic Impact" & Expansion Plan**
**Why:** 1M+ visitors/year to lake currently undermonetized (most free/low-cost). But lake generates hotel stays, dining, retail—measurable economic activity.
- **Idea:** Create "Lake Economy Dashboard" section: Quantify visitor spending (accommodation, dining, retail, marina); benchmark against comparable lake destinations (Salton City, CA; Lake of the Ozarks, MO); show expansion opportunity (West Marina + hotel + RV park potential revenue)
- **Why it matters:** De-mystifies why lake development is high ROI; shows non-extractive economic model (tourism vs. manufacturing); builds case for public investment in lake amenities
- **Data viz:** Visitor origin map (where do 1M+ visitors come from?); spending breakdown (lodging, food, retail, activities); comparable lake destinations' economic profiles; phased development revenue projections
- **Action:** Partner with county tourism office to analyze visitor data; survey marina/campground/hotel operators for spending estimates; benchmark comparable destinations
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks data collection, 1 week benchmarking

---

#### **3.4 Niche Segment: "Heritage & Historic Tourism" Program**
**Why:** Oil-boom architecture, Coutts Museum, Oil Heritage Trail are assets but marketed fragmented. Comparable "heritage tourism" towns (Salina, KS; Lawrence, KS) drive $15–30M annual spend.
- **Idea:** Create "El Dorado Heritage District" section: Brand downtown as cohesive heritage destination; develop walking tour map, historic building registry, "museum quarter" concept (expand Coutts + Oil Museum + new small museums into district); market to regional heritage tourists
- **Why it matters:** Drives off-season tourism (heritage visitors less price-sensitive); increases downtown retail/dining traffic; justifies historic preservation investment; appeals to aging boomer demographic
- **Data viz:** Heritage assets map (historic buildings, museums, cultural sites); visitor flow patterns (where do heritage tourists go?); case studies of successful heritage districts (Salina, Lawrence, Fort Scott, KS); heritage tourism spend benchmark
- **Action:** Conduct heritage assets audit; develop walking tour; partner with museums on "district" positioning; market to heritage tourism networks
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks audit, 2 weeks marketing strategy

---

#### **3.5 Niche Experience: "Community Gathering Calendar" & Event Infrastructure**
**Why:** Vision 2040 mentions parks/recreation but not event programming. Yet events drive foot traffic, social connection, identity.
- **Idea:** Create "Community Calendar" section showing: seasonal events (lake festival, downtown street fair, farmers market, art walks), recurring community gatherings (parks, downtown, schools), infrastructure for events (stages, power, parking, vendor spaces)
- **Why it matters:** Shows residents there's always "something happening"; attracts newcomers looking for community engagement; increases downtown retail/dining revenue during events
- **Data viz:** Interactive event calendar (filterable by type, location, date); venue capacity/infrastructure map (where can city host 200-person, 1,000-person, 5,000-person events?); comparative event calendar (what events do peer towns host?)
- **Action:** Audit existing events; interview parks/downtown managers on event capacity; research comparable towns' calendars
- **Timeline:** 1 week audit, 1 week website integration

---

#### **3.6 Niche Data: "Public Space Accessibility & Inclusivity" Audit**
**Why:** Vision 2040 doesn't explicitly address disability access, aging population accessibility, economic diversity of access. But inclusive spaces increase usage and revenue.
- **Idea:** Create "Inclusive Placemaking" section: Audit parks, downtown, lake for ADA accessibility, senior-friendly design, economic barriers to access (parking fees, admission costs); show improvement roadmap
- **Why it matters:** Reaches underserved demographics (aging, disabled, low-income); increases overall participation; builds brand as inclusive/progressive city
- **Data viz:** Accessibility audit results (% of parks ADA-accessible, wayfinding clarity, seating availability, parking proximity, accessible restrooms); benchmark against peer cities; improvement roadmap with costs
- **Action:** Conduct accessibility audit with disability advocate group; survey seniors on access barriers; calculate remediation costs
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks audit, 1 week benchmarking

#### **3.7 Niche Opportunity: "Art & Culture as Economic Development" Strategic Program**
**Why:** Arts-driven development (Austin, Denver, smaller towns like Bozeman) shows 3–5x ROI on public arts investment. El Dorado has underutilized arts infrastructure.
- **Idea:** Create "Creative Economy Development" section: Map existing arts infrastructure (Coutts Museum, downtown galleries, performance venues); quantify creative workforce (artists, musicians, designers living in El Dorado); build strategy to attract more (artist residency program, affordable studio space, public art commissioning)
- **Why it matters:** Creative professionals drive innovation, walkable urbanism demand, and authenticity. They're early adopters of revitalization. Attract 50–100 artists, see downstream benefits (10–15 supporting businesses)
- **Data viz:** Creative workforce map; peer city creative economy comparison (% of workforce in creative industries); artist residency program benchmarking (costs, typical participants); ROI analysis of public arts investment
- **Action:** Survey current creative community; assess affordable studio space availability; design artist residency program; develop public art commissioning strategy
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks survey/assessment, 2 weeks program design

## 4. PEOPLE & TALENT
### Current State
Demographics, talent, education, workforce development

### NICHE EXPANSION IDEAS

#### **4.1 Niche Demographic: "Youth Retention & Boomerang Young Professional" Program**
**Why:** 55% of El Dorado HS graduates leave and don't return (Kansas brain drain typical pattern). But emerging "return migration" trend—young professionals priced out of metros moving back home, especially if remote work available.
- **Idea:** Create "Bring It Home" program section: Market El Dorado to young professionals (25–40) in Kansas/Missouri/Oklahoma metro areas; show value proposition: cheap housing, strong internet, walkable downtown, affordable outdoor recreation; create landing page with job listings, housing availabilities, "welcome back" events
- **Why it matters:** Returning young professionals = ready-made workforce + tax base + community investment; they know the town so integration cost is low; typically earn $50–75K (captures household income growth without external recruitment cost)
- **Data viz:** "Why young professionals are returning to small towns" data (survey, Pew research); El Dorado value prop comparison vs. KC/Wichita/OKC (housing cost index, cost of living, remote work salary gaps); testimonials from returned residents
- **Action:** Design landing page; partner with remote-work job boards; conduct outreach to Kansas State/KU alumni networks; create "welcome back" event
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks program design, 2 weeks marketing execution

---

#### **4.2 Niche Opportunity: "Mental Health & Wellness Hub" Positioning**
**Why:** Mental health crisis in rural US driving health workforce demand and quality-of-life narrative. Small towns with visible mental health infrastructure (therapists, yoga studios, community mental health programs) attract wellness-focused demographics.
- **Idea:** Create "Wellbeing & Mental Health" section: Map existing mental health services (therapists, counselors, support groups); show gaps and growth opportunities; market El Dorado as "mentally healthy community" (walkable, access to nature, low stress, strong social ties)
- **Why it matters:** Attracts health professionals, wellness-focused remote workers; differentiates from purely economic growth narrative; addresses real community need (rural mental health funding up 40% 2020–2025)
- **Data viz:** Mental health service provider map; peer comparison (El Dorado therapist-per-capita vs. peer cities); wellbeing indicator dashboard (access to nature, walkability, social connection measures); case studies of towns with thriving mental health sectors
- **Action:** Audit current mental health services; identify gaps and funding opportunities; interview therapists/counselors on location/growth potential
- **Timeline:** 1 week audit, 2 weeks strategy

---

#### **4.3 Niche Segment: "Immigrant & Refugee Welcoming Program" Certification**
**Why:** Immigration adds 10–15% of US population growth. Small towns increasingly hosting immigrant populations (cheaper housing, sponsoring employers like meatpacking, ag). El Dorado could formally position as "welcoming."
- **Idea:** Pursue "Welcoming Kansas" certification or similar; create "Immigrant & Refugee Resource Hub" section with translation services, employment resources, housing guides, ESL programs, cultural integration events
- **Why it matters:** Builds workforce for AMAZONE, ag sector, hospitality; addresses rural demographic decline; demonstrates inclusive values (brand building); immigrants typically become entrepreneurs at 2x US rate
- **Data viz:** Immigrant population trends (US, Kansas, El Dorado); peer city "welcoming" programs comparison; employment/entrepreneurship data for immigrant-accepting towns; resource guide in Spanish + English
- **Action:** Partner with refugee resettlement agencies; audit ESL/language services; design resource hub; pursue certification
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks planning, 4 weeks execution

#### **4.4 Niche Data: "Talent Attraction Value Proposition" Scorecard**
**Why:** Vision 2040 targets employees for data centers, FDI, etc., but doesn't quantify El Dorado's "total value prop" vs. Wichita, Kansas City, Austin. Job seekers use scorecards.
- **Idea:** Create "Why Talented People Choose El Dorado" dashboard: Score card comparing El Dorado vs. 5–8 comparable cities across 12 factors (salary ranges, housing affordability, schools quality, health care, commute, recreation, cost of living, taxes, walkability, safety, cultural diversity, job growth)
- **Why it matters:** Makes value prop explicit; removes guesswork for job seekers; helps HR teams at recruiting companies understand El Dorado as viable location
- **Data viz:** Radar chart comparing El Dorado vs. benchmark cities; salary + cost of living analysis showing net disposable income advantage; schools/safety/recreation index
- **Action:** Collect data on 12 factors across benchmark cities; validate with local HR/recruiting experts; create interactive tool
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks data collection, 1 week tool building

#### **4.5 Niche Opportunity: "Remote Work Infrastructure & Community" Program**
**Why:** Post-pandemic, 25–35% of workforce works remote. Yet most small towns don't market to remote workers. El Dorado could position as "remote-worker haven" (co-working space, high-speed internet via Velocity BEAD, low cost of living, outdoors access).
- **Idea:** Create "Remote Worker Welcome Program" section: Subsidized co-working (first 3 months free for relocating remote workers); partner with Velocity on BEAD coverage map; create "remote worker community" (Slack, monthly meetups, work-from-coffee-shop culture); target digital nomad platforms
- **Why it matters:** Remote workers = location-flexible, high-earning, low infrastructure cost, minimal time to productivity; typically $60–100K+ household income; no recruitment/training cost for employer
- **Data viz:** Broadband coverage map (BEAD deployment timeline); co-working space availability/pricing; cost of living comparison (El Dorado vs. Austin, San Francisco, Denver); testimonials from existing remote workers
- **Action:** Partner with Velocity on BEAD marketing; assess co-working space options; design community program; outreach to digital nomad networks
- **Timeline:** 2 weeks planning, 2–3 weeks execution

**Why:** High school grads don't know: which jobs are actually available locally? What training do they need? What do they earn? Vision 2040 creates jobs, but no messaging on pathways.

- **Why it matters:** De-mystifies job pipeline for students and parents; shows Vision 2040 jobs aren't theoretical (real, available); builds support for public investment in workforce development; reduces youth out-migration
- **Data viz:** Interactive pathway explorer (interest → jobs → salary → training → providers); local employer spotlights; internship opportunities; alumni success stories
- **Action:** Survey employers on current/future openings; map training providers (Butler CC, high school CTE, certifications); interview successful alumni
- **Timeline:** 3 weeks research/interviews, 2 weeks tool development

#### **4.7 Niche Demographic: "Aging Workforce" Knowledge Transfer & Mentorship Program**

**Why:** Residents have questions about Vision 2040 details (financing, timelines, neighborhood impacts). Current tools don't have structured Q&A.

#### **9.5 Niche Engagement: "Neighborhood Ambassador" Program**
**Why:** Grassroots engagement more effective than top-down. Formalize neighborhood "champions" as official ambassadors.

- **Data viz:** Ambassador map (who they are, what district); ambassador stories/profiles; listening session results by neighborhood

## CONCLUSION

El Dorado Vision 2040 has a strong strategic foundation. These **50+ niche expansion ideas** add specificity, interactivity, and emotional resonance to the narrative. Key themes:

1. **Demystify complexity** — Most Vision 2040 projects are abstract to residents. Break them into concrete examples, career paths, financial implications.

2. **Show small-city differentiation** — Don't compete with big cities on size. Compete on authenticity, character, community, walkability, inclusion.

3. **Build community ownership** — Shift from top-down (city leading) to multi-stakeholder (residents, businesses, organizations leading together). Gamification, pledges, ambassadors, idea incubators create ownership.

4. **Make progress visible** — 14-year plans lose political support if progress isn't visible every year. Report cards, milestone tracking, quarterly stories show momentum.

5. **Connect to personal impact** — "Vision 2040 is for me" resonates more than "Vision 2040 is for the city." Design tools that show individual/neighborhood benefits.

Implementation in 3 phases (Q3 2026 – Q1 2027) keeps the team focused and creates visible momentum every quarter.
