North Development Zone (NDZ)

320-acre mixed-use development zone. Primary focus: workforce housing, small-scale manufacturing, and complementary retail.

Development Timeline

The North Development Zone is positioned to absorb workforce housing demand from Vision 2040 FDI recruitment. Dedicated zoning allows for 200–300 residential units plus small-scale light manufacturing facilities to support advanced manufacturing companies.

Allowed Uses & Density

Primary Uses

Residential: Single-family homes ($130–$400K), townhomes, mixed-density development. Target 200–300 units across 120–150 acres.
Light Manufacturing: Assembly, precision manufacturing, light assembly for advanced manufacturing suppliers. Max 40,000 sq ft per facility.
Commercial/Retail: Neighborhood-scale: grocery, coffee, retail, services. Max 50,000 sq ft total commercial area.
Office: Professional services, tech offices, contractor offices. Up to 30,000 sq ft.

Prohibited Uses

  • Heavy manufacturing (refining, chemical processing)
  • Hazardous waste storage
  • Extraction industries (quarries, mining)
  • Outdoor storage/junkyards

Infrastructure & Requirements

Required Infrastructure

  • Water/sewer for 300+ residential + 50K sq ft commercial
  • Stormwater management system
  • 5–7 miles of new roads (minimum 40 ft ROW)
  • Utilities: electric, gas, broadband
  • Public transit connections

Incentives Available

  • Infrastructure cost-sharing (city covers 30–50% of roads/utilities)
  • Tax abatement (5–10 years, 30–50% reduction)
  • Density bonuses (allow higher residential density)
  • Fee waivers (permit, inspection fees)

Development Roadmap (3–5 Years)

Phase 1 (Yr 1–2)

Infrastructure design, land acquisition, first 50–75 units, retail/commercial

Phase 2 (Yr 2–4)

150–200 residential units, light mfg facilities, amenities (park, recreation)

Phase 3 (Yr 3–5)

Final 75–100 units, secondary retail, community center, full build-out
