Vision 2040: First 100 Days

From aspiration to action. How we move Vision 2040 from city council vote to tangible community momentum in the first quarter of execution.

Why the First 100 Days Matter

Vision 2040 is ambitious. It proposes transforming El Dorado from a stable, quiet community into a growing hub for precision agriculture, advanced manufacturing, and housing innovation. To earn community trust and city council commitment, we need immediate, visible proof that execution is real—not just a document.

The first 100 days establish the cadence: Council votes → Stakeholders engage → Research launches → First developer inquiries arrive → Community sees movement.

🎯 3 Phases of Action

  • Days 1–30: Foundation — Council vote, stakeholder meetings, research planning
  • Days 31–60: Momentum — Workforce pipeline launches, permitting roadmap drafted, dev inquiries flowing
  • Days 61–100: Acceleration — Housing pilot groundbreaking, national media, all-hands celebration

📊 Success Metrics (Day 100)

  • City council vote: ✅ APPROVED (committed resources)
  • Stakeholder meetings: 20+ completed
  • Research leads: 200+ collected (supply chain, developers, foundations)
  • Developer inquiries: 3–5 active conversations
  • Community engagement: 100+ volunteers committed
  • Media coverage: 5+ regional stories

💼 Key Dependencies

  • City Manager full-time commitment to Vision 2040 execution
  • City Engineer + Planning Director dedicated to permitting roadmap
  • Finance Director leading housing finance research
  • USD 490 + Butler CC leadership on workforce pipeline
  • Chamber of Commerce as community engagement hub

⚡ Quick Wins Tracker

Days 1–30: Foundation — Council Vote & Stakeholder Alignment

Objective: Convert Vision 2040 from proposal into official city commitment. Establish governance, assign owners, launch research.

Day 1: City Council Vote & Public Announcement

Governance Milestone

Action: City council formally adopts Vision 2040 as official city strategic plan.

  • Vote: All 4 commissioners + Mayor (5–0 approval expected)
  • Resolution: City commits resources (staff time, budget, incentive authority) to 100-day plan
  • Public announcement: Mayor holds press conference (local media, social channels)
  • Message: "El Dorado is growing. We're ready. We're moving."

Outcome: Council credibility + community awareness + state/national media pickup.

Days 3–15: Stakeholder Kickoff Meetings

Community Engagement

Meetings Scheduled:

  • Day 3: City leadership (City Manager, Finance, Engineer) alignment on Phase 10.5 roadmap
  • Day 4: Chamber of Commerce board (recruit ambassadors, plan July 4 event)
  • Day 5: USD 490 superintendent + Butler CC president (workforce pipeline planning)
  • Day 6: Main Street director + arts council (small business activation)
  • Day 8: County commissioners (regional alignment, tax base growth)
  • Days 10–15: Individual meetings with 20+ key stakeholders (business owners, school principals, civic leaders)

Outcome: 20+ stakeholders formally briefed, commitment letters signed, ambassadors recruited.

Days 6–30: Research Launch & Data Collection

5 Research Priorities Activated

Supply Chain Intelligence Team: Begins mapping precision ag, aerospace, data center supply chains. Contacts 10+ anchor company prospects.

Developer Feasibility Team: Contacts 5+ LIHTC developers (housing finance research). Gathers zoning/permitting docs.

Youth Outcomes Team: USD 490 + BCC jointly assess K-12 capacity + workforce program scalability.

Philanthropic Funding Team: Finance Director researches top 10 Kansas foundations + identifies Vision 2040 fundable projects.

Environmental Baseline Team: City Engineer collects Phase II ESA requirements, Walnut River restoration docs, permitting pathways.

Outcome: 30+ research tasks underway, 100+ initial leads collected, weekly research sync calls established.

Days 21–30: Community Awareness Campaign

Marketing & Communications

Content Published:

  • 100-day action plan (this page) live on Vision 2040 site
  • AMAZONE case study published (proof: local anchor companies are excited)
  • Climate resilience section added to beautification page
  • Social media campaign: Daily Vision 2040 post (jobs, growth, community events)
  • Newsletter: First Vision 2040 subscriber update (1,000+ subscribers by Day 30)
  • Local media: Chamber Chat presentation (Paige McDaniel spotlights Vision 2040)

Engagement Metrics (Day 30): 5,000+ website visitors, 500+ newsletter subscribers, 1,000+ social media impressions.

Days 31–60: Momentum — Workforce Pipeline & Permitting Clarity

Objective: Convert research into roadmaps. Demonstrate path to execution. Begin attracting developers + workforce program participants.

Days 31–40: Workforce Pipeline RFP & Roadmap

Talent Supply Chain

Action: USD 490 + Butler CC jointly issue RFP for new workforce development programs (precision ag, CNC, electrical, manufacturing).

  • Program Design: 50–75 technician trainees/year by 2029 (aligned with AMAZONE hiring ramp)
  • Funding: Kansas Department of Commerce + employer contributions fund 80% of program costs
  • Timeline: Programs operational by Fall 2027
  • Employers: AMAZONE, John Deere dealers, precision ag suppliers sit on advisory board

Deliverable: Workforce Development Roadmap (published online). Shows: program curriculum, instructor hiring timeline, facility needs, funding sources.

Outcome: 50+ educators + employers engaged, funding sources identified, programs ready to launch.

Days 41–50: Permitting Roadmap Publication

Development Acceleration

Action: City Engineer completes Permitting Roadmap (5–10 page guide showing LOI → occupancy timeline).

  • Contents: Pre-approved industrial/residential/mixed-use templates, approval timelines (target: 90–120 days), decision points, staff contacts
  • Mega-sites: 3 primary sites mapped with utility capacity + zoning confirmed
  • Incentives: TIF district framework, sales tax rebate eligibility, job tax credit summary
  • Environmental: Phase II ESA pathway, Walnut River restoration integration, utility interconnection requirements

Publication: Roadmap published on Vision 2040 site + sent to 50+ prospect developers.

Outcome: Developer confidence increases; inquiry pipeline grows to 3–5 active conversations.

Days 51–60: Developer Inquiry Response Protocol

FDI Activation

Action: City + ED team establish developer inquiry response SLA (service level agreement).

  • Inquiry Reception: First developer inquiry expected by Day 50 (LIHTC developer or industrial prospect)
  • Response SLA: 24-hour acknowledgment, 48-hour initial package (site maps, permits, incentives, contact info)
  • Coordination: City Manager coordinates Planning Director + Finance Director for developer meetings
  • Timeline: Developer site visit scheduled within 2 weeks of inquiry

Outcome: First 3–5 developer conversations underway. Housing pipeline activated.

Days 51–60: Butler County Fair (July 16–21) Activation

Community Engagement Peak

Action: Vision 2040 launches 4-station education bootcamp at Butler County Fair (7 days, 5,000–10,000 attendance).

  • Station 1: "Precision Ag" — AMAZONE + Precision ag suppliers demo equipment, discuss jobs
  • Station 2: "Build Your Future" — Housing pilot, developer photos, affordability calculator
  • Station 3: "Jobs & Training" — USD 490 + BCC showcase 50+ job openings + training programs
  • Station 4: "Community Hub" — Ambassador recruitment + Vision 2040 volunteer signup

Recruitment Goal: 100–150 new ambassadors signed up, 500+ interested leads collected.

Outcome: Vision 2040 is the talk of the fair. Community momentum visible.

Days 61–100: Acceleration — Housing Pilot & National Visibility

Objective: Shift from planning to visible construction. Prove execution + attract national media + secure city council commitment for Phase 10.5.

Days 61–75: Housing Pilot Site Selection & Approval

Development Pipeline

Action: First housing developer selected. City council approves zoning + incentives for 15–20 unit pilot project.

  • Developer: Regional LIHTC specialist with experience in Kansas rural communities
  • Project: 15–20 mixed-income units (5 affordable, 10 market-rate, shared amenities)
  • Site: Downtown or near lake (high visibility location for branding)
  • Financing: KHRB + CDBG + city gap funding + tax credit investor
  • Timeline: Groundbreaking by Day 85, completion by end of 2027

Council Vote: Anticipated Day 70–75 (final zoning + incentive approval).

Days 76–85: Housing Groundbreaking & Media Event

Symbolic Milestone

Action: Ceremonial groundbreaking for housing pilot project.

  • Event: Press conference + ceremonial shovel turn (Mayor, developers, city council, 100+ community members)
  • Media: Regional news crews (Lawrence, Topeka, Wichita markets), statewide newspaper coverage
  • Messaging: "First homes built under Vision 2040. 15–20 families moving to El Dorado this year. Growth is real."
  • Visibility: Photos + video distributed to national housing/development publications

Expected Coverage: 5+ regional news stories, 1–2 statewide features, potential national housing journals.

Days 86–100: All-Hands Celebration & Day 100+ Roadmap

Community Milestone

Action 1: 100-Day Celebration Event (Day 100)

  • Community gathering (200–300 people, City Hall or park)
  • Mayor speaks: Vision 2040 momentum, next 100 days roadmap, resource commitment
  • Stakeholder highlights: City Engineer (permitting roadmap), USD 490 (workforce pipeline), AMAZONE (case study), developers (housing pipeline)
  • Ambassador recognition: Celebrate 100+ volunteers who've spread Vision 2040 message
  • Next milestone: August city council presentation + quarterly all-hands schedule

Action 2: Publish "120-Day Expansion Plan" (Day 100)

  • Phase 10.5 details published online (permitting roadmap, workforce pipeline, housing funding, master calendar)
  • Research findings shared (supply chain analysis, developer feasibility, youth outcomes, philanthropic landscape)
  • Q1 2027 milestones clarified (workforce programs launch, Phase 1 permitting, housing development pipeline grows)
  • Decision gates documented: What needs to happen for Phase 10.5 to proceed on schedule

Outcome: Community sees Vision 2040 is not a one-time plan, but a living, evolving roadmap. City council confidence high for long-term commitment.
