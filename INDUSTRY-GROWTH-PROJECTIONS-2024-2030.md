### Site Selection Criteria (Critical for El Dorado)

**The "Power, Water, Permits" Trinity:**

1. **Power Access (60% site weighting)**
   - Actual MW available ≠ theoretical substation capacity
   - Critical: Verify that local power lines aren't already running at 95%+ capacity
   - If nearest suitable transmission line is 2+ miles away, cost and timeline expand dramatically
   - Midwest grid advantage: Existing interconnects, lower load than coasts

2. **Water Availability (25% site weighting)**
   - Gigawatt-scale facilities require cooling water access
   - Arid regions (Phoenix, Las Vegas) essentially ineligible
   - Kansas moderate advantage here vs. Southwest

3. **Permitting Timeline (15% site weighting)**
   - Coastal/urban markets: 2-3 years to secure permits (massive cost impact)
   - Rural Midwest: 6-12 months potential (El Dorado opportunity)
   - **Interconnection queue delays add 2-5 years to revenue timeline**

**Land Cost Paradox:** Cheapest land ≠ cheapest operating site over 10-20 years. Hidden transmission/water infrastructure costs often outweigh land savings.

**Source:** [Build Inc.](https://build.inc/insights/data-center-site-selection), [Giga Energy](https://www.gigaenergy.com/blog/data-center-site-selection), [Bloom Energy](https://www.bloomenergy.com/blog/data-center-site-selection-why-power-defines-where-you-can-build/)

### LIHTC Operating Margins & Financing Mechanics

**Important distinction:** LIHTC is NOT a traditional ROI model.

**How LIHTC Financing Works:**
1. Developer secures tax credits (9% or 4% credit rate)
2. Developer sells tax credits to institutional investors (banks, insurance cos.)
3. Investor equity payment = credit sale proceeds (typically 70-80 cents per $1 credit)
4. Developer recovers 70-80% of project cost in Year 1 from credit sale
5. Operating cash flow = rent collection - expenses
6. Tax equity investor receives: Credit flow (years 1-10) + equity distributions (years 1+)
7. Developer typically retains property after credit period (years 11+)

**Timeline:**
- Credits claimed over 10-year period in equal annual portions
- Full property yields after year 10
- Property long-term value capture years 11-30+

**Operating Margin Profile (Modern LIHTC):**
- EBITDARM (property level): 20-30% of revenues
- Net operating margin: 10-20%
- Property value at exit (year 10-15): 6-8X annual NOI
