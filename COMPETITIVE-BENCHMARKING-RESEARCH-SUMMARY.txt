EL DORADO'S COMPETITIVE POSITION:

THREE UNIQUE STRUCTURAL ADVANTAGES (Irreplicable by Competitors):
  
  1. OWNED WATER RIGHTS (23M gallons/day)
     - USACE long-term contract (50+ year security)
     - ONLY Kansas city with owned capacity (competitors rely on municipal)
     - Strategic Value: Data center recruitment, manufacturing, ag processing
     - Competitive Advantage: UNIQUE; no competitor can match
     - Message: "Only Kansas city with owned water rights. Zero municipal supply uncertainty."
     - Timeline to Leverage: 2026-2028 (BEAD broadband parity window)
  
  2. I-35 DIRECT ACCESS (Exits 71, 76)
     - Primary NAFTA trade corridor node (non-bypass)
     - BNSF Intermodal Park connection (Newton, 30 mi)
     - Logistics to Wichita (29 mi), Kansas City (200 mi), Dallas (450 mi)
     - Competitive Advantage: UNIQUE vs. competitors (Emporia junction, others off-corridor)
     - Daily traffic: 20,000 vehicles = secondary economy opportunity (truck stops, EV charging)
     - Message: "Direct I-35 corridor access. Gateway to Wichita aerospace & Kansas City MSA."
  
  3. STATE PARK ASSET (#1 Kansas, 1M+ annual visitors)
     - Currently UNDERDEVELOPED (26-venue activation plan)
     - Lake tourism opportunity: $75M-$150M annual visitor spending potential
     - Quality-of-life draw for young professionals & families
     - Competitive Advantage: UNIQUE; no competitor has comparable state park
     - Message: "Kansas's #1 state park. World-class outdoor recreation. Lake tourism destination."

SECONDARY ADVANTAGES:
  - FTZ #28 enrollment ($200K-$500K annual tariff savings for manufacturers)
  - AMAZONE FDI proof-of-concept (German company, operational 2025, expanding)
  - Business-friendly regulations (fastest permits, no license fees)
  - Safety (2nd lowest crime rate)
  - Affordability (lowest home prices, lowest cost of living)
  - Refining infrastructure (HF Sinclair, 135K bbl/day)

CRITICAL GAPS REQUIRING IMMEDIATE ACTION:
  
  1. HOUSING AFFORDABILITY CRISIS (URGENT)
     Current Status: 3.46x median home price-to-income ratio (PROBLEM; healthy = 2.5-3.0x)
     Root Cause: Only 12 new units/year; AMAZONE demand creating supply shortage
     Risk: FDI employers can't recruit workers if housing unavailable
     Action Required: Phase 1 North El Dorado (100 units) must open Q4 2026
     Timeline: Phase 1 (100 units by Q4 2026), Phase 2 (100 units mid-2027), Phase 3 (100 units 2028)
     Target: 300+ total units by 2028
  
  2. WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT LAG (HIGH PRIORITY)
     Current Status: Youth retention only 38% (vs. Salina 55%)
     Root Cause: Limited higher ed; fewer professional jobs; Wichita gravity
     Risk: Brain drain continues; can't fill FDI jobs with local workers
     Action Required: Match McPherson's programs (mechanical engineering, apprenticeships, childcare)
     Investment: $500K-$1M/year (state/federal grants available for match)
     Target: 50+ certifications/year by 2028
  
  3. BROADBAND PARITY WINDOW (TIME-SENSITIVE)
     Current Status: BEAD broadband deployment 2026-2027
     Risk: All competitors will have gigabit fiber by 2027-2028 = no differentiation
     Implication: Must close data center LOI by Q2 2027 before advantage erodes
     Action Required: Aggressive data center recruitment NOW (2026)
     Timeline: Feasibility study Q4 2026, LOI Q2 2027
  
  4. SHOVEL-READY SITE INVENTORY (MODERATE PRIORITY)
     Current Status: 450 acres (adequate but behind Dodge City 700+, Salina 1,200+)
     Action Required: Expand to 600+ acres by 2027 via North El Dorado mega-site
     Timeline: Platting & utilities 2026, lots available 2027

THREAT BY COMPETITOR:

McPherson (LOCAL THREAT: MODERATE)
  ✗ Same size (13K pop), same industries, shared labor market
  ✗ Aggressive workforce development (mechanical engineering 2026, childcare)
  ✗ Risk: Attracts El Dorado talent if not matched
  ✓ Mitigation: Match investments; emphasize FDI growth trajectory
  Verdict: Competitive threat on workforce retention; not FDI recruitment

Salina (SECTOR THREAT: HIGH for Aerospace)
  ✗ Largest (47K pop), K-State Salina aerospace heritage
  ✗ K-AIRES R&D facility (opening 2026) strengthens aerospace ecosystem
  ✗ Risk: May win aerospace supply companies
  ✓ Mitigation: Partner with Salina (logistics/supply hub role); emphasize I-35 + lower costs
  Verdict: Competitive threat for aerospace supply sector only; opportunity to partner

Dodge City (NOT A THREAT)
  • Hilmar Cheese ($600M, 270 jobs) is largest recent FDI in rural Kansas
  • BUT: Food/ag processing sector (NOT competitive with El Dorado's advanced mfg focus)
  • Validates FDI recruitment as viable; different sectors = no direct threat
  Verdict: No competitive threat; validates development strategy

Emporia (LOW THREAT)
  • Animal health corridor dominance (NOT competitive with El Dorado's sectors)
  • Natrium Reactor proposal (speculative, pending federal approval)
  Verdict: Different sectors; no direct threat

Ponca City (NO THREAT)
  • Declining (energy sector contraction)
  • No FDI wins announced
  Verdict: Defensible position; city in decline
