Artisan & Maker Cooperative Network

Economy
Quick Win
< $50K
< 6 months

Initiative Overview

What Is It?

Create a cooperative structure for local makers (woodworkers, ceramicists, metalworkers, bakers) to share studio space, equipment, and marketing. This overcomes the "one person can't afford a full studio" barrier that prevents creative entrepreneurs from launching.

Why El Dorado?

El Dorado's rural location attracts artisans seeking low cost of living and small-town community. A shared maker hub (potentially in a converted historic downtown building) creates employment, tourism draw, and cultural identity without heavy subsidy.

Implementation Plan

1
Design & Partnership
Partner with USD 490's vocational program. Secure location (historic theater annex or downtown building). Draft cooperative governance structure and membership agreements. Months: 1–2.
2
Pilot Launch
Outfit 3,000 sq ft studio with basic equipment (woodworking tools, ceramics kiln). Recruit first 12 makers. Establish $150/month membership model. Months: 3–4.
3
Scale & Sustain
Add second studio or expand first location. Market to regional artisans. Build retail/gallery space. Target $18K annual revenue by year 2. Ongoing.
