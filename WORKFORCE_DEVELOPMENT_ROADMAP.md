# El Dorado Vision 2040: Workforce Development Roadmap
## Training Pipeline for 500+ New Jobs (2027–2032)

**Prepared by:** USD 490 + Butler Community College  
**Date:** August 15, 2026  
**Status:** APPROVED FOR EXECUTION  
**Target:** 50–75 trainees/year by 2029, 100%+ job placement

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Vision 2040 targets 1,500–2,400 new jobs by 2032 across precision agriculture, aerospace, advanced manufacturing, data centers, and construction. **The bottleneck: trained workers.** 

This roadmap activates a partnership between USD 490 (K–12) and Butler Community College (post-secondary training) to:

1. **Build a talent pipeline** — K–12 CTE programs + apprenticeships = clear career path starting in high school
2. **Scale training capacity** — 50–75 technicians/year by 2029 (aligned with job creation pace)
3. **Secure funding** — Kansas Dept of Commerce covers 80% of training program costs
4. **Guarantee employment** — AMAZONE, aerospace suppliers, and manufacturers guarantee job placements

**Result:** Every Vision 2040 job filled by a trained El Dorado worker (or regional recruit). Zero skills gaps. High wages: $55K–85K for technician roles.

## SECTION 2: PROGRAM DETAILS & LAUNCH TIMELINE

### Program 1: Precision Agriculture Technician (BCC 2-Year)

**Start Date:** Fall 2027  
**Cohort Size:** 30–40 students/year  
**Admission:** High school diploma or GED  
**Timeline:** 4 semesters (2 years)

**Curriculum (60 credit hours):**
- **Year 1 Fall:** Hydraulics fundamentals, electrical systems basics, welding (3D and pipe), intro agriculture
- **Year 1 Spring:** CNC machining, hydraulic systems design, agricultural equipment operation, safety certification
- **Summer 1:** Internship at AMAZONE or regional equipment dealer (40–60 hrs/week, paid)
- **Year 2 Fall:** Advanced hydraulics, control systems, agricultural machinery repair, product support
- **Year 2 Spring:** Equipment diagnostics, customer service, technical documentation, capstone project
- **Summer 2:** Final internship (employer trial period → job offer if performance meets standards)

**Instructor Requirements:**
- Hydraulics specialist (AMAZONE or equipment supplier provides part-time instructor)
- CNC/machining instructor (regional technical consultant)
- Agricultural equipment technician (BCC hiring, posting open now)
- Safety coordinator (OSHA 30-hour certification trainer)

**Cost:** $1.2M startup (equipment, facility, instructor salaries), $400K/year operations  
**Funding:** Kansas Dept of Commerce workforce development grant ($400K), BCC foundation grant ($300K), AMAZONE in-kind support ($100K)  
**Employer Advisory Board:** AMAZONE, John Deere dealer, regional equipment suppliers (quarterly meetings to refine curriculum)

**Job Placement:**
- Target: 85%+ placement in precision ag jobs
- Wages: $55–70K starting
- Employers: AMAZONE (20–30 placements), regional dealers (10–15)
- Advancement: Technician → Lead technician ($75K by year 5)

### Program 2: Advanced Manufacturing Technician (BCC 1-Year Cert)

- Quality/metrology technician (manufacturing background)

**Cost:** $600K startup (equipment, instructor salaries), $250K/year operations  
**Funding:** Kansas Dept of Commerce apprenticeship fund ($200K), BCC operational budget ($150K), employer cost-share ($100K)

**Job Placement:**
- Target: 90%+ placement in manufacturing technician roles
- Wages: $60–75K starting
- Employers: AMAZONE (15–20), aerospace suppliers (10–15), regional manufacturers (15–20)
- Advancement: Technician → Lead/supervisor ($80K+ by year 5)

### Program 4: Electrical Apprenticeship (Union + Non-Union)

**Start Date:** Ongoing (structured pathway)  
**Sponsor:** International Brotherhood of Electrical Workers (IBEW Local) + BCC  
**Duration:** 4 years (2,000 hours on-the-job training + 240 classroom hours/year)

**Structure:**
- **Year 1:** Apprentice (earn 40% of journeyman wage) + 1 day/week classroom at BCC
- **Year 2–4:** Progression to journeyman (earn 100% of journeyman wage by year 4)
- **Classroom:** Electrical theory, NEC code, safety, equipment-specific training

**Instructor:** IBEW-certified instructors (union electricians teaching evening classes at BCC)

**Cost:** $50K/year (classroom + coordination), funded by apprenticeship fees + BCC budget  
**Employer Partners:** El Dorado utilities, construction firms, AMAZONE (on-site electrical maintenance)

**Job Placement:**
- Target: 100% placement (union dispatch system ensures employment)
- Wages: $30K (year 1) → $85K+ (journeyman, year 4+)
- Advancement: Journeyman → Foreman → Inspector

- Employers: AMAZONE (10–15), construction (10–15), fabrication shops (8–12)

### Program 6: USD 490 K–12 CTE Pathway (Career & Technical Education)

**Grades 9–10: Career Exploration**
- **Course:** "Industries of El Dorado" (1-semester elective)
  - Guest speakers from precision ag, aerospace, manufacturing, data centers
  - Site visits to AMAZONE, local manufacturers, utilities
  - Skills inventory + career interest assessment
  - Introduction to apprenticeships + post-secondary programs

**Grades 11–12: CTE Specialization**
- **Pathway A: Precision Agriculture**
  - Algebra + trigonometry (equipment design foundations)
  - Agricultural mechanics (equipment operation + basic repairs)
  - Senior capstone: "Design a farm implement" project

- **Pathway B: Manufacturing/Skilled Trades**
  - Technical math (blueprint reading, calculations)
  - Introduction to welding (SMAW basics, AWS prep)
  - Shop practices (CNC lathe/mill basics, safety certification)
  - Paid work-study: 2 days/week at AMAZONE or manufacturer (earn + learn)

- **Pathway C: Information Technology**
  - Computer networking fundamentals
  - IT support & troubleshooting
  - CompTIA A+ certification prep
  - Work-study at local IT shops or utilities (network support)

**Program Coordination:**
- **High school liaison to BCC:** Monthly meetings to align HS CTE with post-secondary programs
- **Employer advisory board:** Precision ag suppliers, manufacturers, IT companies guide curriculum
- **Dual enrollment options:** Qualified 11–12 graders take BCC courses for college credit (tuition waived)

**Instructor Requirements:**
- CTE teacher (new hire, industry background required)
- Shop/welding instructor (credentialed, AWS certified)
- IT teacher (CompTIA certified or industry experience)

**Cost:** $200K/year (new hires, equipment, facility), funded by USD 490 budget + state CTE grants ($150K state match)

## SECTION 6: RISK MITIGATION & CONTINGENCY PLANS

### Risk 1: Low Enrollment (fewer than 20 per cohort)
**Trigger:** Enrollment below 50% of target in Year 1  
**Impact:** Program viability questioned, employer confidence shaken  
**Mitigation:**
- Aggressive K–12 recruiting (high school career days, parent meetings, employer site visits)
- Employer scholarships (AMAZONE covers tuition for X students; other employers do the same)
- Fast-track high school diplomas for adult workers (accelerated pathway for mid-career changers)
- Marketing campaign (social media, local media, Chamber of Commerce promotion)

---

### Risk 2: Instructor Shortage (can't hire qualified CNC, hydraulics teachers)
**Trigger:** Candidate pool too small in rural Kansas  
**Impact:** Program delay, quality compromise  
**Mitigation:**
- Recruit from industry (AMAZONE & manufacturers loan experienced technicians as part-time instructors)
- Relocation assistance (recruiting bonus + housing assistance for instructors moving to El Dorado)
- Remote/hybrid teaching (bring in subject matter experts for 2–4 days/month; local instructor handles day-to-day)
- Certification support (cover costs for practitioners to get teaching credentials)

---

### Risk 3: Employer Hiring Freezes (companies lay off instead of expanding)
**Trigger:** Economic recession, industry downturn  
**Impact:** Job placement rates drop, program relevance questioned  
**Mitigation:**
- Job guarantee letters (AMAZONE, other employers commit to hiring X graduates/year regardless of economic cycle)
- Regional job placement (if local jobs freeze, connect graduates with employers in Wichita, Kansas City, Tulsa)
- Bridge funding (city provides temporary stipend for graduates waiting for placement, max 3 months)
- Incumbent worker training (shift to upskilling current workforce instead of new hires, maintains program relevance)

---

### Risk 4: Funding Cuts (state/federal grants reduced)
**Trigger:** Budget cuts to WIOA, state workforce programs  
**Impact:** Programs undergoing reduce operations, staff layoffs  
**Mitigation:**
- Diversified funding model (federal 40%, state 25%, local 25%, employer 10% → reduces dependency on any single source)
- Foundation grants (apply to Lerner, Ewing Marion Kauffman, Charles Koch foundations for rural workforce development)
- Employer cost-share (formalize: AMAZONE covers $100K/yr, manufacturers cover $50K collective, ensures sustainability)
