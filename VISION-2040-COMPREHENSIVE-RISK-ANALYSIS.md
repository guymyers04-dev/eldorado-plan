# El Dorado Vision 2040 — Comprehensive Risk Analysis (2026–2030)

**Prepared:** June 28, 2026  
**Plan Period:** 2026–2030 (Phase 1)  
**Budget:** $510K Year 1; $500M–$2B expected return  
**Key Metrics:** Data center LOI by Q4 2027; 2–3 FDI facilities; 200+ housing units; +2–3% annual population growth

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

El Dorado Vision 2040 pursues an aggressive 4-year transformation targeting $500M+ in data center investment, 2–3 international FDI facilities, lake activation (3–4 anchor tenants), and 200+ workforce housing units. This analysis identifies **32 material risks** across 8 categories, with probability-weighted impact assessments and mitigation strategies.

**Overall Risk Level:** MODERATE-HIGH
- **High-probability risks:** Staffing delays, broadband deployment lags, FDI momentum loss
- **High-impact risks:** Data center deal collapse, recession, grant defunding
- **Critical path risks:** Staff hiring (July 2026), LIHTC financing (by June 2027), data center LOI (by Q4 2027)

**Key Finding:** Plan is vulnerable to macro shocks (recession, AI bubble burst, federal budget cuts) AND execution risks (staffing gaps, timeline slippage). Success requires: (1) rapid, experienced staff hiring, (2) diversified prospect pipelines (avoid single-deal dependency), (3) contingency capital sources, (4) quarterly board review with pivot triggers.

### 2.4 Housing Market Risk: Regional Real Estate Correction

**Description:** Midwest housing prices decline 15–25% (2027–2028 recession); builder confidence collapses. LIHTC/USDA RD financing dries up. Mixed-income housing demand uncertainty.

**Probability:** MEDIUM (45%)

**Impact if Occurs:**
- **Financing:** LIHTC syndication interest drops; USDA RD becomes risk-averse on rural lending

- **Financial:** Projected 150–200 units in Phase 1 reduced to 75–100; Phase 2 timing extended

- LIHTC pricing declines (syndicators offer lower equity for tax credits)
- USDA RD lending standards tighten (require larger equity cushion, less leverage)
- Regional builder data shows declining starts (new housing permits drop >20%)
- Interest rates spike (mortgage rates >7%; reduces homebuyer demand, rental demand from people unable to buy)

**Early Warning Signals:**
- By September 2026: LIHTC syndicators slow application review; indicate funding constraints
- By December 2026: USDA RD loan officer signals stricter underwriting (requires higher local equity)
- By Q1 2027: Regional builder survey shows negative sentiment

**Mitigation Strategies (Preventive):**
1. **Diversified financing sources** — Don't rely on LIHTC alone; simultaneously pursue: (a) USDA RD, (b) State programs (KHIF), (c) Private investment (EB-5), (d) Local incentives (TIF)
2. **Early financing closes** — Pursue LIHTC and USDA RD closings by Q1 2027 (vs. Q2), before market deteriorates
3. **Flexible unit mix** — Design Phase 1 to be scalable (60–150 units); can reduce units if financing constraints
4. **Market-rate component** — Include 20–30% market-rate units (cross-subsidy model) to improve financial returns and appeal to private investors
5. **Ground lease model** — City retains land ownership, leases to developer (reduces equity need; improves financing feasibility)

**Mitigation Strategies (Detective):**
- Monthly LIHTC market assessment (pricing, syndicator activity)
- Quarterly USDA RD lending environment review (loan officer feedback, recent approvals in Kansas region)
- Track mortgage rates and homebuilder sentiment (leading indicators of residential real estate weakness)

**Contingency Plan (Responsive):**
- **If LIHTC pricing declines >20%:** Reduce unit count (100 units instead of 150) to maintain project economics
- **If USDA RD becomes unavailable:** Shift to KHIF or state programs; delay closing to Q2 2027
- **If construction costs spike:** Delay Phase 1 groundbreaking to 2028 Q1 (wait for cost moderation); still achievable within Phase 1 window
- **Phase 1 minimum:** 75 units minimum acceptable (maintains strategic housing competitive advantage)

**Responsible Party:** Housing Consultant (financing tracking), EDA CEO (market assessment and pivot decision)

## CONCLUSION

El Dorado Vision 2040's success depends on managing **10–12 critical-path risks** across execution, market, competitive, and financial domains. The plan is robust IF: (1) experienced staff hired immediately, (2) prospect pipelines diversified (not single-deal dependent), (3) federal funding sources diversified, (4) quarterly board review enables rapid pivots.

**Key success factors:**
- **Execution velocity:** Hit Week 1–4 milestones; momentum builds confidence
- **Prospect diversification:** Never fewer than 3 qualified prospects per sector
- **Contingency planning:** Accept that primary strategy (data center) may not close; have FDI/industrial user fallbacks
- **Quarterly pivots:** Be willing to shift sector emphasis if market signals change
- **Community confidence:** Transparent communication about risks, progress, learnings

**Worst-case scenario:** If all high-probability market risks occur (AI bubble burst, FDI momentum fades, recession, brain drain continues), Phase 1 timeline slips to 2029–2030 but Vision 2040 still achievable by 2035–2040 with Phase 2 focus on FDI clustering and housing ecosystem.

**Best-case scenario:** If staffing and timeline execution successful, and market cooperates (data center deal by Q4 2027, 2nd FDI by Q4 2027, housing groundbreaking Q2 2027), Phase 1 delivers 200–300 jobs, 2–3% annual population growth, and regional growth leader status by 2028.
