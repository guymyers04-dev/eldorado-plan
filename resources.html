Transportation Resources

State and federal transportation agencies, planning bodies, and specific project resources relevant to El Dorado's transportation network.

Transportation Study — Key Projects (2024)

The El Dorado Transportation Study (Sept. 2024) identified 20 near-, mid-, and long-term projects. All cost estimates are 2024 dollars.
NEAR-TERM

N Main St & 6th Ave — Signal Upgrade

Intersection improvement and traffic signal modernization. Est. cost: $851,000. Right-of-way: $56K. Priority intersection in city's top-20 crash locations.

NEAR-TERM

N Main St & McCollum Rd — Roundabout

New roundabout at N Main Street and McCollum Road. Est. cost: $1,683,000 (incl. $389K contingency). Supports northern growth corridor development.

NEAR-TERM

Country Club Rd, E 29th–E 30th — New 3-Lane Roadway

New 3-lane roadway section to support residential development. Est. cost: $3,493,000+. Opens access to eastern growth zones.

MID-TERM

Central Avenue (K-254) — Reconstruction

Major east-west corridor improvement from Boyer Rd to Haverhill Rd. Two concepts studied: lane consolidation with bike lanes, or complete streets retrofit. Study reviewed by KDOT.

LONG-TERM

Future Northwest Trafficway

New bypass corridor to serve northern development areas and relieve congestion on existing streets. Critical for mega-site and North Gateway District access. Costs TBD by KDOT separate study.

LONG-TERM

Southwest Trafficway Extension

Southern bypass extension to support industrial corridor growth. Connects HF Sinclair operations area with I-35 access. Reduces industrial truck traffic through downtown.

ACTIVE TRANSPORT

Bicycle Network Expansion

Converts recreational bike facilities to true transportation network. Protected intersections, bike lanes on Central Ave and Main St, connection to El Dorado Lake trail system. Strava heatmap data used in planning.

ACTIVE TRANSPORT

Sidewalk Gap Closure Program

Systematic closure of sidewalk gaps citywide. Priority on school routes (Safe Routes to School), transit stops, and downtown connections. ADA compliance throughout.

SS4A Priority Projects (April 2026)

8 prioritized safety improvement projects funded through USDOT Safe Streets and Roads for All grant program.
SS4A P1

Project 1: Central Ave — Boyer Rd to Haverhill Rd

Top-priority east-west safety corridor. Signal upgrades, retroreflective backplates, lane narrowing, enhanced pedestrian crossings. Addresses cluster of angle and rear-end crashes.

SS4A P2

Project 2: Central Ave — Oil Hill Rd to Main St

Continuation of Central Ave safety improvements into downtown core. Signal timing, pavement markings, pedestrian hybrid beacons at key mid-block crossings.

SS4A P3

Project 3: Main St — Locust Ave to 3rd Ave

Priority north-south gateway corridor into downtown. Addresses nighttime impaired driving and VRU crash patterns. Speed reduction measures, lighting upgrades.

SS4A P4

Project 4: Main St at 6th Ave — Intersection

Targeted signal and geometric improvements at key downtown intersection. Signal upgrades, retroreflective backplates, refreshed pavement markings.

SS4A P5

Project 5: Main St — 12th Ave to McCollum Rd

Safety improvements north of downtown including signal and lighting upgrades at 12th Ave and McCollum Rd intersections.

SS4A P6

Project 6: 6th Ave — Haverhill Rd to Main St

Safety and operations improvements on 6th Ave. Signal upgrades at Haverhill and Summit intersections. Addresses rear-end and parking-related crash cluster.

SS4A P7

Project 7: Haverhill Rd & Towanda Ave — Intersection

Signal upgrades, retroreflective backplates, refreshed pavement markings at Haverhill/Towanda intersection. Also includes policy recommendations and further study areas.

SS4A P8

Project 8: Country Club Rd — 12th to McCollum

Safety improvements along Country Club Road corridor serving residential and recreational areas. Speeds, sight lines, and pedestrian crossings.

Housing & Demographics

Housing market data, demographic research, and funding programs for residential development in El Dorado.

STATE TOOL

Kansas RHID — Residential Housing Incentive District

#1 recommended tool from WSU Housing Needs Assessment. Captures incremental sales tax generated by new housing construction. Self-financing mechanism for residential infrastructure.

FEDERAL

LIHTC 9% Rural — Low Income Housing Tax Credit

Most competitive affordable housing financing. 9% credit available for rural areas. Targets 60-80 AMI rental housing. Workforce Rental proposal: 80-100 units ($14M total). Kansas Housing Resources Corp. (KHRC) administers.

STATE

KHRC — Kansas Housing Resources Corp.

State housing finance agency. Homeownership Ladder ($8–16K per buyer), down payment assistance, first-generation homebuyer programs. Key partner for addressing El Dorado's $97,800 median home value gap.

FEDERAL + STATE

Historic Tax Credit (HTC) — 45% Stack

Federal 20% HTC + Kansas 25% credit = 45% total. NRHP Historic District designation unlocks this stack. 124 Lofts is a recent El Dorado example. Downtown core could qualify for district designation.

NATIONAL

ROC USA — Resident-Owned Communities

National nonprofit supporting manufactured/mobile home community conversion to resident ownership cooperatives. USDA Rural Development 515 financing. Protects 180+ El Dorado households in existing parks.

STATE TOOL

Kansas Land Bank Authority (K.S.A. 12-5901, 2021)

State statute enabling cities to create land banks for vacant, tax-delinquent properties. El Dorado can acquire, clear title, and redevelop problem properties. Critical for Vacant Lot Activation Program.
