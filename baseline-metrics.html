What This Means

  • ✅ Education Strength: USD 490's performance is above state average. College readiness (ACT 22.8) exceeds typical peer communities.
  • ⚠️ Population Stagnation: Unlike high-growth peers (Manhattan +3.1%), El Dorado lost 0.2% population 2010–2020. Vision 2040 targets +41% reversal.
  • ⚠️ Income Below Peers: Median household income ($48.5K) is $3–10K below similar Kansas communities. Higher-wage industry recruitment addresses this.
  • ✅ Homeownership Strong: 72% homeownership rate matches peer communities; stable foundation for growth.
  • ⚠️ Business Formation Lagging: 35 new businesses/year vs. peer average of 42. Vision 2040 targets 50+ via entrepreneurship support.

Data Sources & Methodology

All metrics are from authoritative public data sources. Data is current as of June 2026.

Primary Sources

  • U.S. Census Bureau — Population, housing, income, household data (2020 Census + ACS 5-Year Estimates)
  • Bureau of Labor Statistics — Employment, wages, industry breakdown, unemployment rates
  • Kansas Department of Labor — State-level labor market data, unemployment claims
  • Kansas Department of Education — K–12 performance metrics, graduation rates, ACT scores
  • Butler Community College — Enrollment, programs, regional reach
  • City of El Dorado — Fiscal data, capital projects, local assessments
  • Butler County Assessor — Property valuation, assessed value data
  • FBI Uniform Crime Reporting (UCR) — Crime statistics
  • Real Estate Market Data — Zillow, county assessor records, local MLS

Notes on Interpretation

Baseline metrics represent El Dorado as of 2024. Some data (e.g., Census) reflects 2020 estimates with city-level adjustments. Peer comparisons use the most recent available data for similar-sized Kansas communities (generally 2023–2024). Vision 2040 targets represent projections based on research, market analysis, and comparative benchmarks. Actual 2040 outcomes will depend on execution of strategic initiatives, market conditions, and external factors.
