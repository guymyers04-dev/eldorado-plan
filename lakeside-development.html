Lake-Side Development Zones

Regional Recreation & Destination Development

Comprehensive waterfront strategy: boutique hospitality, water sports, RV parks, dining, and regional destination positioning

Why Lake Development: Gateway to Regional Recreation Market

Market Opportunity

El Dorado Lake offers significant untapped potential:

  • Geographic position: 60-minute drive from Wichita (600K+ metro), 90 min from Kansas City
  • Competitive set: Table Rock Lake, Beaver Lake, Perry Lake—all with 2+ hours drive time
  • Visitor profile: Weekend recreationists (sailing, fishing, boating), families, retirees
  • Current state: Basic recreational use; limited hospitality/dining infrastructure
  • Vision: Boutique destination with premium experiences, year-round programming

📊 Market Baseline

  • Lake size: 3,570 acres
  • Current annual visitors: ~40,000–50,000
  • Projected with marketing: ~65,000–80,000 (+30–60%)
  • Hotel rooms available: 40–60 (inadequate for demand)
  • RV park capacity: 60–80 sites (seasonal, 70–80% occupancy in summer)

Development Zones (Lakefront Strategy)

Divide lakefront into specialized zones:

  • North zone: Residential + marina (see North Expansion Zone page)
  • West zone: Water sports, rentals, watersports school
  • South zone: Nature preserve, quiet recreation
  • East zone: Hospitality core (hotels, restaurants, retail)

15-Year Vision: Premium Regional Destination Development

Phase 1: Core Amenities (Years 1–3)

  • Hospitality: 75–100 room boutique hotel (upscale casual, waterfront views)
  • Dining: 2–3 restaurants (fine dining, casual waterfront, quick service)
  • Water sports: Rentals (kayaks, paddleboards, sailboats), instruction school
  • RV park: Expand to 100–120 sites with hookups
  • Retail/services: Marina store, gift shop, ice cream, casual retail
  • Investment: $20–30M

Phase 2: Experience Enhancement (Years 4–7)

  • Event programming: Regattas, swimming races, fishing tournaments, summer concerts
  • Premium experiences: Sunset cruises, wine tastings, waterfront weddings
  • Infrastructure: Waterfront promenade, event pavilion, amphitheater
  • Additional hospitality: Cabin rentals, glamping, vacation homes
  • Investment: $30–45M (cumulative)

Phase 3: Destination Status (Years 8–15)

  • Destination branding: "El Dorado Lake — Kansas Premier Waterfront Destination"
  • Additional amenities: Spa/wellness center, fine dining expansion, specialty retail
  • Conference/event venue: Capacity for 300–500 person events
  • Investment: $50–60M (cumulative)

💡 The Experience Model

Lake development is about creating memorable experiences, not just infrastructure:

  • Weekend getaway packages (hotel + dining + watersports)
  • Summer events calendar (regattas, concerts, festivals)
  • Specialty experiences (sunset sailing, private dinners, team-building events)
  • Social media moments (photo-worthy locations, Instagram appeal)

Economic Impact: Tourism Revenue & Job Creation

Direct Revenue

  • Hotel revenue: $3–5M annually (100 rooms × 65% occupancy × $120 avg rate)
  • Restaurant/retail: $4–6M annually
  • Water sports: $800K–1.2M annually
  • RV park: $800K–1M annually
  • Total annual revenue: $8–13M (at Phase 3 maturity)

Employment & Payroll

  • Direct jobs: 150–200 (hotel, restaurants, retail, recreation staff)
  • Indirect jobs: 100–150 (suppliers, services, support roles)
  • Total payroll: $6–8M annually
  • Tax revenue: Sales tax ($300–500K annually), property tax from new development

Stakeholder Roles: City, Private Operators, Marketing

🏛️ City of El Dorado

  • Adopt Lake Development Strategy (zoning, design guidelines, permits)
  • Public-private partnerships for core amenities (hotel, event venue)
  • Infrastructure (parking, utilities, wastewater treatment upgrades)

💼 Private Hospitality Operators

  • Develop boutique hotel (50–100 rooms)
  • Operate restaurants, retail, services
  • Program events, experiences, marketing

📢 Marketing & Tourism

  • El Dorado Convention Bureau: destination marketing
  • Regional tourism boards: cross-promotion
  • Social media campaigns, event marketing, partnership with Wichita tourism

⛵ Water Sports & Recreation

  • Private concession or City partnership for rentals/instruction
  • Event coordination (regattas, swimming races, competitions)
  • Safety management, environmental stewardship
