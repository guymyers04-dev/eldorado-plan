1 Million Visitors, Zero Economic Capture

El Dorado Lake is Kansas's most-visited state park, drawing 1M+ annual visitors. Yet the city captures effectively zero lakefront dining, boutique lodging, or commercial amenity spending. Every visitor dollar exits Butler County before reaching a single El Dorado register. Vision 2040's visitor economy strategy transforms this missed opportunity into a $25M+ annual revenue stream.

Food & Beverage Strategy

Waterfront Dining: The Anchor Experience

Zero lakefront dining is the single biggest competitive disadvantage. Visitors currently spend $20-50/meal at distant restaurants in Wichita or El Dorado proper. Waterfront dining captures that spending and anchors a full ecosystem of secondary spending (lodging, retail, boat rentals).

Accommodation Strategy

Boutique Lodging: Longer Stays, Higher Revenue

Dining anchors a day visit. Lodging anchors multi-day stays and visitor groups. A 30-50 room lakefront boutique resort attracts regional events (weddings, conferences, family reunions) that generate $8M+ in secondary spending annually.

Proposed Resort Profile

  • Scale: 40-50 rooms
  • Style: Contemporary waterfront (Aman-inspired)
  • Average Rate: $180-250/night
  • Amenities:
    • Full-service restaurant + bar
    • Waterfront spa (10-12 treatment rooms)
    • Meeting/event space (500 pax capacity)
    • Private beach access
    • Boat dock + rental concession
    • Fitness center + yoga studio
    • Firepit lounge area

Water-Based Infrastructure

Marina Strategy: Two-Facility Model

El Dorado Lake currently has only one marina (Shady Creek, 100 slips) serving 1M annual visitors. Vision 2040 adds a modern West Marina (150 slips) on the North Gateway District waterfront, creating seamless access to lodging, dining, retail, and services.
