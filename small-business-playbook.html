Your Journey: 7 Phases to Success

Building a business is a journey, not a destination. Here's the complete roadmap with checkpoints, resources, and support at every step.

1
Validate Your Idea
Test your concept, confirm market demand, and refine your business model.
  • Identify target market
  • Survey potential customers
  • Analyze competition
  • Define value proposition
2
Legal & Regulatory
Handle licensing, permits, and legal structure. 90% of new businesses complete this in 2-4 weeks.
  • Choose business structure (LLC/Corp)
  • Register with state
  • Get EIN from IRS
  • Obtain local permits
3
Funding & Finances
Secure capital and set up accounting systems. Multiple funding options available locally.
  • Calculate startup costs
  • Explore funding sources
  • Open business account
  • Set up accounting
4
Branding & Marketing
Build your brand and create marketing strategy. El Dorado business community supports launches.
  • Develop brand identity
  • Create website/social
  • Plan marketing strategy
  • Set pricing strategy
5
Operations & Hiring
Set up operations, hire team, and train staff. Local workforce is ready.
  • Establish location/office
  • Procure equipment
  • Hire initial team
  • Train staff
6
Launch
Soft launch with friends/family, then public launch. Community will welcome you.
  • Soft launch testing
  • Gather feedback
  • Make final tweaks
  • Official launch
7
Grow & Scale
Expand operations, increase revenue, and prepare for next phase of growth.
  • Monitor metrics
  • Scale operations
  • Reinvest profits
  • Plan expansion

💰 Funding Options Available

SBA Loans
$25K - $5M+
Government-backed loans with favorable terms. 10-year repayment. Contact El Dorado SBDC for guidance.
Business Grants
$5K - $50K
Kansas small business grants (non-repayable). Eligibility varies. SBDC can help you apply.
Angel Investors
$25K - $250K
Local investors interested in supporting new ventures. Chamber can connect you.
Bank Loans
$10K - $500K
Traditional term loans from El Dorado banks. 5-10 year terms. Lower rates available.
Microloans
$5K - $50K
Smaller loans for startups. Faster approval, flexible terms. SBDC administers locally.
Bootstrapping
Variable
Use personal savings, friends/family loans, or revenue reinvestment. 40% of new businesses use this method.
