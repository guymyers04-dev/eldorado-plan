# El Dorado, KS Competitive Benchmarking Report 2026
## Detailed Metrics Comparison: El Dorado vs. Salina, McPherson, Emporia, Dodge City

**Research Date:** June 2026  
**Scope:** Five competing Kansas cities + regional analysis  
**Focus:** Seven competitive dimensions with quantified benchmarking data

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

El Dorado competes against four primary rivals (Emporia, Dodge City, Salina, McPherson) and one secondary competitor (Ponca City, OK). Despite being the **smallest population (12,500 vs. 13K–47K)**, El Dorado has the **strongest unique structural advantages**:

| **Competitive Edge** | **El Dorado Status** | **Competitor Status** |
|---|---|---|
| **Water Rights** | 23M gal/day (owned) | None (municipal) |
| **I-35 Direct Access** | Exits 71/76 direct | Bypass or off-corridor |
| **FDI Proof-of-Concept** | AMAZONE (2025, operational) | Hilmar, Natrium, K-AIRES (larger/speculative) |
| **State Park Asset** | #1 in Kansas (1M+ visitors) | Regional parks only |
| **FTZ Enrollment** | #28 (active) | Regional access |
| **Refining Infrastructure** | HF Sinclair 135K bbl/day | CP (McPherson), P66 (Ponca) |
| **Lake Tourism Potential** | Underdeveloped (HUGE) | Minimal |

**Bottom Line:** El Dorado can win the advanced manufacturing, data center, and aerospace supply sectors if it executes aggressively on water, FDI brand, and workforce development. The 24-month window (2026–2028) is critical before broadband parity erodes data center advantage.

- Position El Dorado as "Only Kansas city with owned water rights"
- Lead every FDI pitch with water security story
- Target data centers, advanced manufacturing, ag processing
- Timeline: Close data center LOI by Q2 2027

### Priority 2: HOUSING — Solve the affordability crisis
- Accelerate North El Dorado Phase 1 (100+ units) to open Q4 2026
- Ensure workforce rental component (150+ units) to support FDI employment
- Increase new construction to 40–50 units/year by 2027
- Target: 300+ total units by 2028

### Priority 3: LAKE TOURISM — Unlock the #1 state park asset
- Execute Phase 1 (3–4 anchor tenants) by end of 2026
- Target: $75M+ annual tourism impact by 2030
- Waterfront dining, hotel, retail, water sports, events

### Priority 4: WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT — Match McPherson's investments
- Expand Butler CC technical programs (apprenticeships, CNC, robotics)
- Launch mechanical engineering pathway program
- Partner with AMAZONE for co-training
- Timeline: First cohort 2026; 50+ certifications/year by 2028

### Priority 5: FDI DIVERSIFICATION — Don't over-rely on AMAZONE
- Close second major FDI by 2027 (aerospace supply, ag-tech, or industrial machinery)
- Build FDI pipeline with 5+ qualified prospects by 2027
- Partner with JETRO, GTAI, KOTRA for international recruitment
- Leverage AMAZONE as proof-of-concept

### Priority 6: REGIONAL WATER AUTHORITY — Create 50-year revenue stream
- Form authority 2027
- Begin water export negotiations with Butler County municipalities
- Projected revenue: $2M–$5M annually by 2030
