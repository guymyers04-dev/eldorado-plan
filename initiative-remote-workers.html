Remote Worker Attraction & Retention Program

Economy
Quick Win
< $50K
< 6 months

Initiative Overview

What Is It?

Create a structured program (community events, co-working space partnership, tax incentives, networking platform) to recruit remote workers from larger metro areas and integrate them into El Dorado's economy. This expands the tax base and injects digital talent.

Why El Dorado?

COVID normalized remote work. 15–20% of tech/finance/creative workers now live in smaller cities by choice. El Dorado's low housing costs ($120K median home price) are compelling vs. Austin ($450K) or Denver ($550K).

Implementation Plan

1
Setup & Branding
Create "El Dorado Remote Workers" membership community (free first year). Establish co-working partnership with Chamber office ($25/month credits). Build landing page: "Move to El Dorado for $XX/month rent + community." Months: 1–2.
2
Launch & Recruit
Host welcome events (quarterly). Partner with RemoteOK + WeWorkRemotely to list El Dorado. Social media campaign targeting Austin/Denver remote workers. Target: 15 remote workers in first 6 months. Months: 3–6.
3
Retention & Growth
Add commercial property discount for startups. Create mentorship with local entrepreneurs. Monthly community gatherings. Scale to 25 remote workers by end of Year 1.

Budget Breakdown

Landing page + marketing website $8,000
Co-working space partnership (subsidized credits, 12 months) $8,000
Social media + digital marketing campaign $6,000
Welcome events (4 quarterly, $1K each) $4,000
Directory listing fees + platform integrations $3,000
Total Year 1 Investment $29,000

Funding: El Dorado Inc. ($15K), city economic development ($10K), Chamber contribution ($4K).
