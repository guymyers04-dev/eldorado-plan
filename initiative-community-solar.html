Initiative Overview

What Is It?

Develop a 250-kW community solar farm on city-owned land (8 acres), structured as an energy cooperative where residents and businesses own shares. Members receive 15–20% discounts on electricity bills. Potential to generate $40K–$60K annual net revenue for city after debt service.

Why El Dorado?

El Dorado sits on the High Plains with 280 days of sunshine annually. Current electricity costs are 12% above state average. A community solar co-op reduces energy bills, attracts climate-conscious residents/businesses, and creates 3–5 permanent tech jobs for operations & maintenance.

Implementation Plan

1
Feasibility & Permitting
Complete solar resource assessment (DNV-GL or similar). Engage Kansas Corporation Commission for permitting. Secure land use agreement. Establish cooperative legal structure & bylaws. Finalize utility interconnection agreement. Months: 1–4.
2
Financing & Community Launch
Secure $300K via USDA/NRCS grants + green bonds. Launch share purchase campaign (target: 200+ members). Conduct community education sessions. Finalize equipment procurement. Months: 4–9.
3
Construction & Operations
Install 1,000+ solar panels. Conduct final inspections & grid interconnection. Launch member billing system. Begin annual dividend distributions. Scale to Phase 2 (additional 250 kW) by Year 3.
