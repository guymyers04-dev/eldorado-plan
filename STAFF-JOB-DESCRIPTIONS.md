## POSITION 1: DATA CENTER DEVELOPMENT DIRECTOR

**Position Title:** Data Center Development Director  
**Reports To:** Economic Development Authority (EDA) Board  
**Salary Range:** $120,000–$140,000 annually  
**Benefits:** Health insurance, 401(k), professional development budget ($5K/year)  
**Location:** El Dorado, Kansas (office-based with travel)  
**Start Date:** Immediate (Week 1 July 2026)  
**Full-Time:** Yes  

### Position Summary

El Dorado is pursuing a transformational data center investment as part of Vision 2040, a 15-year development plan targeting $500M–$2B in economic investment. The Data Center Development Director will lead recruitment of hyperscale data center operators (CoreWeave, Equinix, Digital Realty, Lambda Labs) to secure a $500M+ facility by Q4 2027. This is a critical, high-impact role that will determine whether El Dorado capitalizes on unique competitive advantages (water rights, I-35 access, Kansas SB 98 tax exemption, BEAD broadband) in the next 24–36 months.

### Primary Responsibilities

**Recruitment & Business Development (60%)**
- Lead data center prospect identification, qualification, and recruitment
- Partner with CBRE, JLL, Cushman & Wakefield brokers to access site selection networks
- Target top 10 hyperscale operators (CoreWeave, Equinix, Digital Realty, Lambda Labs, Megaport, others)
- Prepare and deliver site presentations to C-suite executives and development teams
- Negotiate preliminary terms (water supply, land cost, tax incentives, utility infrastructure)
- Manage sales pipeline (prospects → site visits → LOI → groundbreaking)

**Site & Infrastructure Management (25%)**
- Coordinate with city engineering on water supply capacity, grid access, broadband deployment
- Prepare site packages (maps, utility specs, environmental permits, broadband coverage)
- Facilitate feasibility studies (environmental, water quality, soil conditions)
- Fast-track permitting process (target: 72-hour permit turnaround)
- Communicate with HF Sinclair (energy partner), utilities, water authority

**Incentive & Financing (15%)**
- Coordinate Kansas SB 98 data center sales tax exemption implementation
- Package tax incentives, utility rebates, workforce training grants
- Work with EDA on financing/incentive structure (bonds, direct payments, cost-sharing)
- Negotiate with potential industrial park developers (if needed)

**Reporting & Accountability (5%)**
- Weekly written update to EDA leadership (10 minutes)
- Monthly metrics dashboard (prospects, visits, LOI status)
- Quarterly presentation to City Commission (progress, budget, next steps)
- Annual impact report (jobs created, capital invested, tax revenue generated)

### Required Qualifications

**Must Have:**
- 5+ years of economic development, site selection, or real estate business development experience
- Demonstrated success recruiting or closing major industrial/commercial projects ($100M+)
- Deep knowledge of hyperscale data center sector (operators, site criteria, competitive landscape)
- Existing relationships with site selection brokers (CBRE, JLL, Cushman & Wakefield) or ability to rapidly establish them
- Understanding of data center technology, power/cooling requirements, water needs
- Strong negotiation and executive communication skills
- Ability to manage complex multi-stakeholder processes (city, county, utilities, private partners)

**Strongly Preferred:**
- Experience in rural/secondary market data center recruitment (Midwest, Great Plains, or similar)
- Knowledge of Kansas economic incentives (SB 98, KDHE programs, EDA grants)
- Background in water/utility coordination for industrial projects
- Network of contacts at major hyperscale operators (CoreWeave, Equinix, Digital Realty)
- Experience managing broker relationships and commission structures

**Education:**
- Bachelor's degree in business, economics, real estate, engineering, or related field
- OR equivalent 7+ years of professional experience without degree

### Key Success Metrics

**Year 1 (2026):**
- Month 3: 5–10 qualified data center prospects identified
- Month 6: 1–2 site visits scheduled
- Month 9: Active LOI discussions with 1–2 operators

**Year 2 (2027):**
- Q4 2027: LOI signed with qualified operator
- Q1 2028: Groundbreaking announced; construction begins

**3-Year Target (2026–2028):**
- $500M+ capital investment
- 100–300 direct jobs
- $5M–$10M annual tax revenue

### Compensation & Benefits

**Salary:** $120K–$140K annually (based on experience)  
**Bonus:** Performance bonus (up to 15% of salary) if LOI signed by Q4 2027  
**Health Insurance:** Employer-covered medical, dental, vision  
**401(k):** 3% employer match  
**PTO:** 20 days annually  
**Professional Development:** $5K annually for conferences, training, certifications  
**Vehicle:** Mileage reimbursement (0.67/mile) for travel to site visits, broker meetings, events

## POSITION 2: FDI DEVELOPMENT OFFICER

**Position Title:** FDI (Foreign Direct Investment) Development Officer  
**Reports To:** EDA CEO  
**Salary Range:** $100,000–$120,000 annually  
**Benefits:** Health insurance, 401(k), professional development budget ($4K/year)  
**Location:** El Dorado, Kansas (office-based with travel)  
**Start Date:** Immediate (Week 1 July 2026)  
**Full-Time:** Yes  

### Position Summary

AMAZONE (German agricultural machinery manufacturer) validated El Dorado's attractiveness to international FDI in December 2025. The FDI Development Officer will capitalize on this momentum to recruit 2–3 additional international manufacturers (German, Japanese, Korean) in agriculture equipment, advanced manufacturing, automotive supply, and related sectors. Success will create a critical mass of international businesses, generate 150–400 jobs, and position El Dorado as a regional FDI hub by 2030.

### Primary Responsibilities

**International Prospect Development (60%)**
- Lead outreach to JETRO (Japan), GTAI/AHK (Germany), KOTRA (South Korea)
- Identify and contact 10–15 qualified international manufacturers in target sectors
- Build relationships with German, Japanese, Korean companies evaluating U.S. locations
- Present El Dorado opportunity (AMAZONE case study, I-35 access, FTZ benefits, workforce, water rights)
- Manage multilingual communications (coordinate translation if needed)
- Attend international site selection events (Frankfurt, Tokyo, Seoul) as needed

**AMAZONE Momentum Leverage (20%)**
- Develop AMAZONE case study (video testimonial, written case study, success metrics)
- Use AMAZONE as proof-of-concept for JETRO, GTAI, KOTRA pitch
- Network with AMAZONE partners, suppliers, related companies
- Identify secondary/tertiary opportunities from AMAZONE supply chain

**Site & Infrastructure (15%)**
- Prepare El Dorado site packages (maps, utility capacity, FTZ benefits, workforce data)
- Coordinate with city on permitting, zoning, utility capacity for manufacturing
- Facilitate feasibility studies (environmental, labor market, supply chain)
- Fast-track permitting for second FDI (72-hour target)

**Incentive & Financing (5%)**
- Coordinate FTZ #28 customs duty savings documentation
- Package tax incentives, workforce training grants, workforce housing programs
- Work with EDA on "fast-track" incentive package ($5M–$15M for second FDI)

**Reporting (5%)**
- Weekly update to EDA leadership
- Monthly metrics dashboard (prospects, visits, LOI status, case study development)
- Quarterly presentation to City Commission
- Annual report on FDI investment, jobs created, supply chain development

### Required Qualifications

**Must Have:**
- 5+ years of international business development, FDI recruitment, or global trade experience
- Proven relationships with German, Japanese, or Korean companies OR network with international business organizations
- Understanding of German (preferred) or Japanese or Korean business culture
- Experience with foreign direct investment recruitment in U.S. (state/local level)
- Knowledge of EB-5 visas, foreign investment incentives, trade agreements (NAFTA/USMCA)
- Strong cross-cultural communication and negotiation skills
- Ability to manage complex multi-stakeholder international relationships

**Strongly Preferred:**
- Prior contact/relationships with JETRO, GTAI/AHK, KOTRA
- Experience with agricultural equipment manufacturing or automotive supply chain
- Knowledge of FTZ (Foreign Trade Zone) benefits and compliance
- Background in Midwest economic development or rural FDI recruitment
- Fluency in German, Japanese, or Korean (language skills)
- Experience with case study development and thought leadership content

**Education:**
- Bachelor's degree in international business, economics, trade, or related field
- OR equivalent 7+ years of professional international business experience

### Key Success Metrics

**Year 1 (2026):**
- Month 2: 5–10 qualified FDI prospects identified
- Month 3: JETRO/GTAI/KOTRA relationships established; case study in progress
- Month 6: 1–2 site visits scheduled
- Month 9: Active LOI discussions with 1–2 operators

**Year 2 (2027):**
- Q4 2027: 2nd FDI LOI signed; 3rd FDI in advanced discussion
- Q1 2028: 2nd FDI groundbreaking

**3-Year Target (2026–2028):**
- 2–3 international manufacturing facilities
- $50M–$150M capital investment
- 150–400 direct jobs
- Demonstrated supply chain clustering (network effect)

### Compensation & Benefits

**Salary:** $100K–$120K annually (based on international business experience)  
**Bonus:** Performance bonus (up to 15% of salary) if 2nd FDI LOI signed by Q4 2027  
**Health Insurance:** Employer-covered medical, dental, vision  
**401(k):** 3% employer match  
**PTO:** 20 days annually  
**Professional Development:** $4K annually (includes international travel, conferences, language training)  
**International Travel:** Covered for JETRO, GTAI, KOTRA events and prospecting missions

## POSITION 3: HOUSING DEVELOPMENT CONSULTANT

**Position Title:** Housing Development Consultant  
**Reports To:** EDA CEO  
**Salary Range:** $60,000–$75,000 annually (or $50/hour contract)  
**Benefits:** Health insurance (if full-time), or contractor independent  
**Location:** El Dorado, Kansas (office-based with travel to meetings)  
**Start Date:** Immediate (Month 1, early July 2026)  
**Full-Time:** Yes (or 40+ hours/week contract)  

### Position Summary

El Dorado's 1,100-unit workforce housing plan is a critical competitive advantage and Phase 1 enabler. The Housing Development Consultant will lead Phase 1 implementation (150–200 workforce rental units) by securing $8–$12M in financing (LIHTC, USDA RD, state programs), acquiring the development site, completing permitting, and positioning for groundbreaking by end of 2027. This role bridges financing, development, permitting, and community partnership.

### Primary Responsibilities

**Financing & Fundraising (40%)**
- Lead LIHTC (Low-Income Housing Tax Credit) application process
  - Identify 3–5 experienced tax credit syndicators
  - Prepare pre-development phase application
  - Target: $1.5M–$2M equity for 60–80 unit LIHTC project
- Lead USDA RD (Rural Development) loan application
  - Section 514/516 programs (if applicable)
  - Prepare application for $4M–$6M loan
- Apply to Kansas state programs (KHIF, KDHE affordable housing)
- Coordinate with local nonprofits (NeighborWorks, Habitat for Humanity)
- Manage fundraising timeline (applications, reviews, underwriting, closing)

**Site Acquisition & Master Planning (30%)**
- Identify 20–30 acre parcel in North Gateway District for Phase 1
- Conduct site analysis (utilities, environmental, zoning, accessibility)
- Negotiate land purchase/lease terms with owner or city
- Commission master plan (site layout, utilities, parking, landscaping, phasing)
- Ensure mixed-income unit breakdown (workforce rental, senior, manufactured homes, family)

**Permitting & Infrastructure (20%)**
- Coordinate with city engineering on water, sewer, utilities
- Manage stormwater compliance, environmental permitting
- Fast-track site plan approval (target: 4–6 weeks)
- Prepare for TIF (Tax Increment Financing) or other local incentive programming
- Ensure ADA compliance, fair housing compliance, accessibility standards

**Contractor & Developer Management (10%)**
- Solicit construction contractor bids
- Manage design/build process (architect, engineers, contractor)
- Timeline management (design 3 months, permitting 2 months, construction 18–24 months)
- Quarterly progress reviews with financing partners (LIHTC, USDA RD)

### Required Qualifications

**Must Have:**
- 10+ years of affordable housing development, community development, or LIHTC experience
- Deep knowledge of LIHTC application process, tax credit syndication, permanent financing
- Familiarity with USDA RD programs (Section 514/516 or other rural housing programs)
- Experience developing mixed-income housing projects (workforce rental + family + senior + MHC)
- Strong relationships with LIHTC syndicators, USDA RD loan officers, state housing authorities
- Project management skills (timeline, budget, stakeholder coordination)
- Understanding of rural housing markets and financing

**Strongly Preferred:**
- Kansas-specific experience (LIHTC process, state programs, regional syndicators)
- Experience with Midwest rural communities (population <20K)
- Knowledge of TAX INCREMENT FINANCING (TIF) or other local development incentives
- Construction management background
- Experience working with nonprofits on housing development
- Experience with density, zoning, and land use approvals in small towns

**Education:**
- Bachelor's degree in real estate, finance, business, planning, or related field
- OR equivalent 12+ years of professional affordable housing experience
- LIHTC certification or professional development coursework preferred

### Key Success Metrics

**Year 1 (2026):**
- Month 3: LIHTC and USDA RD applications submitted
- Month 6: Financing pre-approval received from syndicators/USDA RD
- Month 9: Site acquired; master plan completed

**Year 2 (2027):**
- Q1 2027: Construction financing closed
- Q2 2027: Groundbreaking
- Q4 2027: First units under construction

**3-Year Target (2026–2028):**
- $8–$12M financing secured
- 150–200 workforce units constructed
- Phase 1 complete; Phase 2 planning underway
- Housing availability advantage visible in workforce recruitment

### Compensation & Benefits

**Salary:** $60K–$75K annually (full-time) OR $50/hour (contract basis)  
**Health Insurance:** If full-time, employer-covered medical, dental, vision (OR contractor provides own)  
**401(k):** 2% employer match (if full-time)  
**PTO:** 15 days annually (if full-time)  
**Professional Development:** $2K annually for LIHTC training, state housing programs updates  
**Mileage:** Reimbursement for travel to financing meetings, site visits, contractor coordination

## POSITION 4: COMMUNICATIONS & WEB MANAGER

**Position Title:** Communications & Web Manager  
**Reports To:** EDA CEO  
**Salary Range:** $55,000–$70,000 annually  
**Benefits:** Health insurance, 401(k), professional development budget ($3K/year)  
**Location:** El Dorado, Kansas (office-based, remote-friendly for content production)  
**Start Date:** Immediate (Week 1 July 2026)  
**Full-Time:** Yes  

### Position Summary

El Dorado's Vision 2040 website and communications are critical investor relations tools. The Communications & Web Manager will ensure the website is flawless (mobile-responsive, fast, compelling), produce video content highlighting the vision, manage lead generation and investor inquiry follow-up, and coordinate community and media communications. This role bridges web development, content production, investor relations, and community engagement.

### Primary Responsibilities

**Website Management (40%)**
- Website maintenance and optimization (performance, security, updates)
- Implement CSS/UI fixes for mobile responsiveness, contrast, text-fitting (Week 1–2)
- Create and launch 4 investor-specific landing pages:
  - Data Center Page (water rights, tax incentives, broadband, grid capacity)
  - FDI Page (AMAZONE case study, international contacts, site options, workforce)
  - Hospitality/Lake Page (state park data, lodging/dining opportunity, site options)
  - Housing Page (workforce gap, mixed-income strategy, financing, timeline)
- Implement lead capture forms and email nurturing sequences
- Set up Google Analytics 4, conversion tracking, funnel analysis
- Monthly reporting: traffic, visitors, form submissions, conversion rates
- Coordinate with website developer for technical issues and updates

**Content Production (35%)**
- Video production coordination:
  - 3–5 minute Vision 2040 overview video (leadership message, opportunity, call to action)
  - 1–2 minute sector-specific videos (data center, FDI, lake tourism, housing)
  - AMAZONE case study video/testimonial
  - Drone footage of El Dorado, lake, mega-site, I-35 access
- Create written content:
  - AMAZONE case study (1–2 pages)
  - Investor fact sheets (data center, FDI, hospitality, housing)
  - Lake activation overview and opportunity brief
  - Quarterly newsletter/updates on Vision 2040 progress
- Manage videographer/contractor for production
- Ensure all video/content is high-quality, professional, branded consistently

**Lead Generation & Investor Relations (15%)**
- Manage lead capture forms (inquiry routing, follow-up)
- Set up email nurturing sequences for different prospect types:
  - Data center operator sequence (water, incentives, timeline)
  - International FDI sequence (AMAZONE case study, I-35, workforce)
  - Senior living operator sequence (market analysis, healthcare gap, lake amenity)
  - Hospitality operator sequence (state park visitation, site options, market opportunity)
- Qualification process: Respond to inquiries within 24 hours; triage to appropriate staff member
- Maintain CRM/database of investor inquiries and follow-up status
- Quarterly report on leads generated, converted, source attribution

**Communications & Media (10%)**
- Draft and coordinate press releases for announcements (groundbreakings, LOI signings, partnerships)
- Manage relationships with local media (El Dorado Times, regional papers, TV)
- Coordinate community town halls/presentations on Vision 2040 progress
- Social media management (LinkedIn, Facebook, occasional Twitter/Instagram)
- Monthly updates to residents, chamber, county commission on progress

### Required Qualifications

**Must Have:**
- 3+ years of web management, digital marketing, or communications experience
- Proficiency in website platforms (WordPress, Wix, Squarespace, or custom CMS)
- Understanding of HTML/CSS basics (ability to coordinate with developers on CSS changes)
- Video production or direction experience (or willingness to learn/hire contractor)
- Google Analytics experience; conversion tracking, funnel analysis
- Strong writing skills (blog posts, fact sheets, email copy, press releases)
- Project management: ability to manage multiple content projects simultaneously

**Strongly Preferred:**
- Economic development, real estate, or investor relations communication experience
- Experience managing investor/prospect inquiries and lead nurturing
- Video editing or production management (Adobe Creative Suite, Premiere, Final Cut)
- Email marketing platform experience (HubSpot, Mailchimp, Klaviyo)
- Social media management experience
- CRM experience (Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho)
- Knowledge of SEO (search engine optimization)

**Education:**
- Bachelor's degree in communications, marketing, digital media, or related field
- OR equivalent 5+ years of professional web/communications experience

### Key Success Metrics

**Year 1 (2026):**
- Week 1: CSS/UI fixes live; mobile-responsive
- Week 3: 4 investor landing pages live; lead capture active
- Month 2: 500+ monthly website visitors; 10–15 qualified inquiries
- Month 3: 3–5 videos in production or live
- Month 4: 1,000 monthly visitors; 20–25 qualified inquiries
