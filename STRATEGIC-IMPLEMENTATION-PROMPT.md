# El Dorado Vision 2040 — Strategic Implementation Prompt
## Engineering a 15-Year Development Transformation at the Highest Level

**Prepared:** June 27, 2027  
**Scope:** Execution blueprint for City Leadership, Economic Development Authority, and Regional Partners  
**Horizon:** 2026–2040 (Phase 1 Critical: 2026–2028)

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

El Dorado, Kansas stands at an inflection point. With AMAZONE's operational facility (Dec. 2025), $166.6M BEAD broadband funding approved (Dec. 2025), and a comprehensive 15-year blueprint ready, the city has **24–36 months to capitalize on a unique convergence of advantages: water infrastructure scarcity, I-35 corridor positioning, FDI proof-of-concept, and Kansas tax incentives.**

**This prompt synthesizes market research, competitive intelligence, UI/UX requirements, partnership opportunities, and implementation strategy to position El Dorado for transformational growth.**

## PART 1: THE STRATEGIC CASE FOR ACTION

### A. Current Situation (2027)

**Assets:**
- ✅ AMAZONE facility operational (German FDI validation)
- ✅ #1 most-visited Kansas state park (1M+ annual visitors)
- ✅ 23M gal/day owned water capacity (unique among Kansas cities)
- ✅ I-35 direct access (exits 71/76, not bypass)
- ✅ FTZ #28 enrollment (customs duty savings)
- ✅ HF Sinclair refinery (135K bbl/day, stable energy/skilled workforce)
- ✅ $166.6M BEAD broadband approved; deployment 2026–2027
- ✅ #1 state workforce ranking in skilled manufacturing
- ✅ Comprehensive, research-backed 15-year vision (33 sections, 6,882 lines of HTML)
- ✅ Kansas SB 98: 20-year data center sales tax exemption (July 2025)

**Constraints:**
- 🔴 12,500 population → need 5,100 new residents (41% growth) by 2040
- 🔴 Housing shortage (competitive disadvantage vs. peers)
- 🔴 Regional competition (Emporia, Salina, McPherson, Dodge City all recruiting)
- 🔴 BEAD broadband parity erodes data center advantage by 2028 (window closing)
- 🔴 Website UI/UX needs refinement (text-fitting, mobile responsiveness, section expansion)
- 🔴 FDI recruitment infrastructure not yet built (no investor relations, no international outreach team)

### B. The Market Window (2026–2028)

**Why Now?**

Three macroeconomic trends create a 24–36 month window to execute Phase 1:

1. **Data Center Boom** (2025–2028)
   - Meta, Google, OpenAI, Oracle all announcing Midwest AI data center sites
   - Kansas broadband parity (BEAD) erodes advantage by 2028
   - **El Dorado's water rights are THE scarcity driver** in Kansas
   - Post-2028, every rural Kansas town will have gigabit fiber; water becomes only differentiator
   - **ACTION WINDOW: 24 months to secure data center agreement**

2. **Agricultural Equipment FDI Cycle** (2025–2027)
   - AMAZONE (German) just opened; JETRO/GTAI/KOTRA actively recruiting U.S. locations
   - Network effect: If El Dorado lands 2–3 German/Japanese equipment companies, critical mass forms
   - John Deere, CLAAS, Lemken, CNH Industrial all evaluating secondary/satellite locations
   - **ACTION WINDOW: 18 months to convert AMAZONE momentum into second/third FDI win**

3. **Senior Living/Healthcare Boom** (2025–2032)
   - Sunrise Senior Living 20+ expansion in 18 months; divesting/consolidating
   - Brookdale downsizing; selective Midwest expansion phase
   - Baby boomer aging curve hits peak demand 2026–2032
   - El Dorado lake amenity + healthcare gap = ideal positioning
   - **ACTION WINDOW: 36 months to position senior living project for groundbreaking 2028–2029**

4. **Regional Housing Shortage** (2025–ongoing)
   - McPherson, Emporia, Salina all reporting housing gaps for incoming workers
   - Rural workforce housing is scarce; shortage = competitive advantage for cities with housing pipelines
   - El Dorado's 1,100+ unit North Gateway plan positions it ahead of competitors
   - **ACTION WINDOW: 36 months to break ground on Phase 1 workforce housing (2027–2028)**

## PART 2: IMPLEMENTATION STRATEGY

### PHASE 1 EXECUTION (2026–2028) — The Critical 24 Months

#### **Priority 1: Data Center Recruitment (Weeks 1–12, then continuous)**

**Objective:** Secure letter of intent (LOI) or groundbreaking announcement by Q4 2027.

**Why This First?**
- Highest ROI (1–2 data center facilities = 100–300 direct jobs, $500M–$1B+ capital investment)
- Fastest execution timeline (LOI to groundbreaking = 12–18 months)
- Unique El Dorado advantage: water rights + SB 98 tax exemption + BEAD broadband
- Competitive window: Closes by 2028 when broadband parity erodes advantage

**Tactical Steps:**

1. **Recruit/Hire Data Center Development Director** (Week 1–4)
   - Expertise: Hyperscale data center site selection, negotiations, incentives
   - Salary: $120K–$150K; contract possible
   - Reporting: Economic Development Authority Board
   - Networks: CoreWeave, Equinix, Digital Realty, Megaport, regional brokers
   - Job description: "Attract $500M+ data center investment by Q4 2027"

2. **Launch Investor Relations Campaign** (Week 2–6)
   - Create **data center-specific landing page** (separate from main Vision 2040 site)
   - Assets: Water rights documentation, BEAD fiber map, grid capacity, tax incentive fact sheet, site maps
   - Target: CoreWeave, Lambda Labs, Crusoe Energy, Nara Logics, NVIDIA-backed data center companies
   - Channels: Direct outreach to site selection teams (LinkedIn, broker networks, CBRE, JLL, Cushman & Wakefield)
   - Messaging: "Water scarcity + tax exemption + I-35 access + 99.99% uptime grid"

3. **Negotiate Water Supply Agreement** (Week 1–12)
   - Formalize water commitment for major user (data center or industrial)
   - Volume: Offer 5–10M gal/day for 20–30 year term
   - Price: Market rate ($1.50–$3.50 per 1,000 gal, regional standard) or incentivized (0–2 years free/reduced for anchor tenant)
   - Partner: Create regional water authority with surrounding counties (scales water as competitive asset)
   - Legal: Work with USACE on contract terms, environmental compliance

4. **Participate in Site Selection Events** (Month 1–24, quarterly)
   - **Area Development Summit** (Oct 2026, Nashville) — 500+ site selectors, brokers
   - **NAIOP Data Center Summit** (May 2026, Denver) — hyperscale operators
   - **Heartland Economic Development Conference** (April 2026, Kansas City) — Midwest focus
   - Cost: $5K–$10K per event; ROI: 1–2 data center leads per major event

5. **Create Partnership with Broker/Site Selection Firm** (Month 1–2)
   - Hire broker (e.g., Jones Lang LaSalle, CBRE, Cushman & Wakefield) to represent El Dorado
   - Commission: Typical 1% of project value (shared); deal-dependent
   - Scope: Identify 5–10 qualified prospects (CoreWeave, Equinix, Digital Realty, etc.), facilitate initial conversations
   - Timeline: LOI by Q4 2027

**Success Metrics:**
- Month 6: 3–5 qualified data center inquiries
- Month 12: 1–2 site visits
- Month 18: LOI signed
- Month 24: Groundbreaking

#### **Priority 3: Phase 1 Lake Activation — Anchor Tenants (Month 1–24)**

**Objective:** Secure 3–4 "anchor" tenants for lake development (restaurant, boutique hotel, water sports school, RV park) by end of 2027.

**Why?**
- Visible, tangible progress for residents/stakeholders
- Quick wins (6–12 month build timelines)
- Supports broader narrative ("Vision 2040 is happening NOW")
- Attracts secondary investment

**Tactical Steps:**

1. **Lake Master Plan Update** (Month 1–3)
   - Work with landscape architect to refine 26-venue activation strategy into **phased priorities**
   - Phase 1 (2026–2028): 3–4 high-impact anchors (waterfront restaurant, boutique hotel, water sports school)
   - Phase 2 (2028–2032): 8–10 secondary venues (parking, trails, pavilions, marinas)
   - Phase 3 (2032–2040): Full ecosystem (25+ venues)
   - Cost: $20K–$50K planning; ROI: $500M+ private investment over 15 years

2. **Recruit 3–4 Anchor Tenants** (Month 2–18)
   - **Waterfront Restaurant Operator** (upscale casual, 100–150 seats)
     - Target: Local restaurateurs with success track record, OR chains like Bonefish Grill, Ocean Prime, Cheesecake Factory
     - Incentive: Low-cost land/lease (5–10 years), workforce training grants, tax incentives
   - **Boutique Hotel** (30–60 rooms, elevated amenity)
     - Target: Hilton Outset Collection, Marriott Autograph, Voco, or independent boutique operator
     - Incentive: EB-5 financing available (Kansas Regional Center); LIHTC stack if mixed-use
   - **Water Sports School/Equipment Rental** (kayak, paddleboard, sailing lessons)
     - Target: Paddle sports franchises (Stand-Up Paddle Board franchises, Adventure Sports centers)
     - Incentive: Reduced facility cost, equipment grants
   - **RV Park/Campground** (80–120 sites)
     - Target: KOA Campgrounds franchisee, or independent operator
     - Incentive: Land lease, utility infrastructure, marketing support

3. **Develop Site Packages** (Month 1–4)
   - Maps: Identify 3–4 optimal sites for each anchor tenant (waterfront, water access, parking)
   - Specs: Utility capacity, infrastructure details, environmental constraints
   - Financial: Lease/purchase options, incentive packages, ROI scenarios for operators
   - Documents: Environmental assessments, permitting roadmap

4. **Launch Recruitment Campaign** (Month 3–12)
   - Direct outreach: Phone/email to target operators (restaurant groups, hotel brands, RV chains)
   - Industry events: National Restaurant Association conference, Hospitality Investment Summit, RV Industry events
   - Brokers: Hire CBRE, JLL to source operators for boutique hotel and restaurant concepts
   - Cost: $10K–$20K for broker; high ROI given developer cost (typically 10% of project, so $50M project = $5M cost to operator)

5. **Fast-Track Permitting** (Month 1–24, ongoing)
   - City approves expedited building permits for lake projects (72-hour turnaround)
   - State coordinates environmental permits (water, wetlands)
   - Messaging: "We move fast; you can break ground in 90 days"

#### **Priority 4: Phase 1 Workforce Housing (Month 1–24)**

**Objective:** Break ground on 150–200 workforce rental units (Phase 1 of 1,100-unit North Gateway plan) by end of 2027.

**Why?**
- Competitive advantage: McPherson, Salina, Emporia all struggling with housing
- Data center + FDI projects will demand 200–400 new housing units
- Breaks ground before demand hits (avoid 12–18 month permitting delays)
- Workforce housing financing available (LIHTC, USDA, RHID, state programs)

**Tactical Steps:**

1. **Secure Financing/Equity** (Month 1–6)
   - **Primary: Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC)**
     - Target: 60–80 units mixed-income (LIHTC 4% credits + tax-exempt bonds)
     - Equity: ~$1.5M per 60 units
     - Partners: Related Companies, Community Development Trust, local nonprofits
   - **Secondary: USDA Rural Housing Loans** (Section 514/516)
     - Target: 50–75 single-family workforce homes
     - Loan amount: $4–$6M
     - Underwriting: 4–6 months
   - **Tertiary: Kansas Housing Initiative Fund (KHIF)**
     - Target: $2–$4M gap financing for mixed-income units
   - **Total Capital Raised: $8–$12M** (sufficient for 150–200 units)

2. **Hire Housing Development Consultant** (Month 1–3)
   - Expertise: LIHTC, USDA programs, mixed-income housing development, rural markets
   - Role: Lead financing negotiations, site planning, permitting, contractor selection
   - Fee: $50K–$100K for Phase 1 delivery (2026–2027)

3. **Expedite Site Acquisition & Master Planning** (Month 1–6)
   - Identify 20–30 acre parcel in North Gateway for Phase 1 development
   - Engineering: Utilities, stormwater, traffic study
   - Zoning: Ensure zoning permits mixed-income, multifamily
   - Timeline: Acquire site by Month 6; site plan complete by Month 9

4. **Break Ground by End of 2027** (Month 18–24)
   - Construction timeline: 18–24 months (occupancy late 2028–early 2029)
   - Visible progress: First units occupied before 2029 municipal elections
   - Narrative: "We're delivering housing NOW; workforce can move to El Dorado"

**Success Metrics:**
- Month 3: Financing consultant hired
- Month 6: $8–$12M financing secured
- Month 9: Site acquired, master plan complete
- Month 12: Construction financing closed
- Month 18: Ground broken
- Month 24: First phase under construction; opening timeline announced

## PART 7: RISK MITIGATION & CONTINGENCY

### Major Risks

**Risk 1: Data Center Deal Delays (Probability: MEDIUM)**
- **Mitigation:** Have 3–5 qualified prospects; start conversations simultaneously. If CoreWeave slow, activate Equinix, Digital Realty, Lambda Labs in parallel.
- **Contingency:** If no data center LOI by Q4 2027, shift focus to 2nd/3rd FDI (manufacturing > data center). Still achieves job creation.

**Risk 2: Broadband Delays (BEAD timeline slips)** (Probability: LOW)
- **Mitigation:** Build redundancy; HF Sinclair has grid capacity independent of BEAD. FTZ #28 provides logistics advantage independent of broadband.
- **Contingency:** Fiber deployments typically on-schedule; if delayed, emphasizes water scarcity argument for data center recruitment.

**Risk 3: FDI Momentum Fades (No 2nd/3rd international facility by 2028)** (Probability: LOW)
- **Mitigation:** AMAZONE validates market; JETRO/GTAI pipeline is robust. Diversify: target German, Japanese, Korean suppliers.
- **Contingency:** Shift to domestic manufacturers (Boeing supply chain, ag equipment OEMs). Still achieves 75–200 jobs per facility.

**Risk 4: Senior Living Deal Doesn't Close (Probability: MEDIUM)**
- **Mitigation:** Start conversations with 3–4 operators (Sunrise, Brookdale, Atria, etc.). Have 2–3 site options.
- **Contingency:** If senior living delayed, prioritize boutique hotel/RV park (shorter timelines, similar ROI). Senior living can follow 2028–2030.

**Risk 5: Housing Financing Delayed** (Probability: LOW-MEDIUM)**
- **Mitigation:** Apply to multiple LIHTC syndicators; have USDA backup. Build relationships early.
- **Contingency:** If LIHTC delayed, use USDA RD only (slower, but reliable). Push timeline to 2027–2028 completion vs. 2026–2027.

**Risk 6: Community Resistance (NIMBYism, "growth too fast")** (Probability: MEDIUM)**
