Allowed Uses

  • Cold Chain Warehousing: Temperature-controlled storage (pharma, food, flowers)
  • LTL Distribution: Less-than-truckload (LTL) regional hubs
  • Last-Mile Fulfillment: E-commerce micro-fulfillment centers
  • Truck Services: Rest stops, EV charging, repair facilities
  • Logistics Tech: Dispatch centers, tracking facilities
  • Cross-Dock Operations: Inventory consolidation/deconsolidation

Location Advantage

  • I-35 Corridor: 20,000+ vehicles/day pass through El Dorado
  • Newton Intermodal Park: 30 miles north (opening 2025–2026)
  • Kansas City: 200 miles north via I-35
  • Dallas: 450 miles south via I-35
  • Wichita: 29 miles west (regional hub)

Infrastructure & Services

Facility Requirements

  • 150K–300K sq ft modern warehouse (climate-controlled for cold chain)
  • 40–60 dock doors for truck access
  • Employee parking (100+ spaces)
  • Truck apron/staging area (15–20 acres)
  • Power (2–5 MW for HVAC + refrigeration)
  • Water (essential for cold chain operations)

Incentives Package

  • EDA Grant: $1.5M (logistics is infrastructure priority)
  • STAR Bond District: 6.5% TIF rebate (20-year term)
  • KDOT Access Grant: $400K (I-35 on/off ramps, rail spurs)
  • Tax Abatement: 50% reduction, 10 years (for 100+ jobs @ $45K+)
  • Workforce Training: $200K (CDL, logistics management)
  • Water Rate: Discounted industrial rate ($1.50/1K gal)

Development Timeline (2–3 Years)

Months 1–3

Site prep, utility design, LOI negotiation

Months 3–18

Construction, hiring, equipment installation

Month 18+

Operations, hiring (100–200 jobs), expansion

Why El Dorado for Logistics?

  • I-35 Direct Access: Only Kansas city with direct I-35 exits (71, 76)
  • Newton Intermodal Proximity: Ideal feeder hub for BNSF Newton (30 mi north)
  • Water Infrastructure: Essential for cold chain (cooling, sanitation)
  • Cost Advantage: Land + labor + utilities cheaper than competitors
  • Strategic Location: Perfect 12-hour positioning between Kansas City and Dallas
