At a Glance

El Dorado's downtown has good bones — a walkable Victorian grid, an active Main Street program, and a world-class Kansas Oil Museum. Vision 2040's downtown strategy focuses on acceleration through streetscape improvements, heritage tourism, residential conversion, and targeted business recruitment. The goal: make Central Avenue the social and cultural heart of the city.

12 Major Downtown Initiatives

🏗️

01 · Central Avenue Streetscape Redesign

Widen sidewalks to 12 ft, add 80 new street trees in structural soil cells, install consistent period-appropriate lighting, decorative crosswalks, and 20 new public benches. Calmed traffic design — speed tables and curb extensions — improves pedestrian comfort and has been shown to increase retail sales 20–40% on comparable Main Street programs.

Comparable benchmarks: Emporia, Pittsburg, and Leavenworth, KS all saw measurable sales tax increases within 3 years.

🛢️

02 · Kansas Oil Museum Expansion & Experience Center

Triple the museum's footprint into the adjacent vacant block. Add an immersive oil-boom era experience, an interactive Stapleton No. 1 drilling rig simulator, and rotating exhibitions on Kansas energy's future. Partner with HF Sinclair for naming rights. Target: 50,000 visitors per year — making El Dorado a required stop on every Kansas heritage tourism itinerary.

Current state: 12,000 visitors/year

🎪

03 · Central Plaza & Farmers Market Pavilion

Convert the underused surface parking lot at Main & Central into a permanent public plaza — covered market pavilion, performance stage, year-round splash pad, and winter ice feature. Weekly farmers market (spring–fall), monthly food truck rally, and the annual Oil Jubilee festival. This becomes the social heart of downtown.

🏛️

04 · Historic Facade Improvement Program

50% matching grants (up to $30,000 per building) for exterior renovation of downtown commercial properties — windows, awnings, masonry, signage. Funded by a dedicated downtown TIF district. 40+ eligible buildings; program runs 10 years. High-quality storefronts signal city investment.

🍳

05 · Small Business Incubator & Pop-up Food Hall

Convert a vacant warehouse into a flexible co-working and retail incubator with 15 micro-storefronts (200–500 sq ft), shared commercial kitchen, event space with 150-seat capacity, and 20 co-working desks. 2-year below-market leases with graduated rent increases. Graduates tenants into permanent Central Avenue storefronts.

🏨

06 · Boutique Hotel — Adaptive Reuse

Convert one of El Dorado's large vacant downtown buildings into a 35-room boutique hotel with rooftop bar and event space. Visitor accommodation is the missing link preventing overnight tourism stays. Kansas Historic Tax Credits (25%) + New Market Tax Credits (39%) make the financial stack viable for a private developer.

🗺️

07 · Oil Heritage Walking Tour & Wayfinding

Commission a professionally designed self-guided walking tour of downtown El Dorado's oil-boom landmarks — the Stapleton #1 oil discovery marker, 1915 derrick sites, heritage commercial buildings, and the Kansas Oil Museum — linked by custom period-appropriate wayfinding markers with QR codes connecting to audio narration and archival photography.

🎨

08 · Historic Alley Activation — El Dorado Alley Art District

Transform downtown's service alleys into a curated art destination — commissioning large-scale murals from Kansas artists, installing accent LED lighting, adding weather-protected gallery alcoves, and hosting guided alley art tours as part of First Friday gallery walks. Budget per alley: $15,000–$40,000 — extremely cost-effective activation.

🍽️

09 · Outdoor Dining Expansion & Parklet Program

Streamline outdoor dining permits to a single application, 15-day approval, and flat $250 annual fee. Launch a city-funded "parklet" program converting 2–3 on-street parking spaces per block into small public dining areas with planters, shade structures, and movable furniture. Outdoor dining increases restaurant revenue 15–30% and is the single most effective strategy for increasing dwell time on a pedestrian street.

💻

10 · Digital Business District & Tech Infrastructure

Designate downtown El Dorado as a "Digital Business District" with dedicated gigabit fiber drops to every storefront, standardized outdoor LED display infrastructure, free WiFi in all public spaces, and a Central Avenue co-working hub with podcast studios, streaming-quality broadband, and drop-in meeting rooms.

🥬

11 · Culinary Destination Strategy — Farm-to-Table

Develop and market El Dorado as the culinary hub of the Flint Hills — a "farm-to-table capital" where the wheat, beef, and produce of Butler County agriculture appear on downtown menus. Partner with Butler CC's Culinary & Hospitality Institute to host annual cooking competitions, chef residencies, and a "Harvest Festival" celebrating the seasonal agricultural calendar.

👶

12 · Children's Discovery Museum & STEM Center

Partner with a children's science museum organization to open a 4,000-sq-ft satellite branch — hands-on exhibits in physics, biology, engineering, and local natural history. Operating model: membership-based with per-visit admission for out-of-towners. A children's museum is a proven anchor for young-family downtown traffic on weekday afternoons and weekends.

Vision 2040 Downtown Strategy

El Dorado's downtown has excellent bones: a walkable Victorian commercial grid, active Main Street program, and world-class Kansas Oil Museum. Vision 2040 accelerates transformation through streetscape improvements, heritage tourism development, residential conversion, and strategic business recruitment — making Central Avenue the social and cultural heart of the city.

🏛️ Historic Preservation

25% state + 20% federal tax credits on Victorian building rehabilitation. Façade improvement grants (up to $30K per building). Target: 40+ buildings rehabbed by 2035.

🏘️ Residential Activation

60–80 market-rate lofts in upper floors. ROOMS grant ($50K awarded 2025). Loft dwellers support ground-floor retail 7 days/week.

🎭 Cultural Anchor

Kansas Oil Museum expansion (3× footprint). Target: 50K+ annual visitors (from 12K today). Arts district with subsidized artist leases.
