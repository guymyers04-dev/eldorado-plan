Who's Driving This Vision

Governance Structure

Vision 2040 is a collaborative strategic plan developed by the City of El Dorado, Butler County leadership, local institutions, and the business community. This plan represents broad consensus on El Dorado's future.

🏛️ City of El Dorado

Primary Authority: The City Council is the lead decision-making body for this vision. The City Manager and Planning Department are responsible for day-to-day implementation oversight and coordination.

Role: Policy approval, infrastructure investment, zoning changes, incentive programs

📊 El Dorado Inc.

Economic Development: El Dorado Inc. (the city's economic development organization) leads business recruitment, industrial site marketing, investor relations, and competitive positioning.

Role: FDI recruitment, competitive marketing, deal flow, site attraction

🤝 Butler County Commission

Regional Alignment: The county provides land-use planning coordination, infrastructure support (water, roads, emergency services), and ensures that El Dorado's growth aligns with county-wide economic development strategy.

Role: Regional coordination, infrastructure planning, emergency services

🎓 Institutional Partners

Education & Health: USD 490, Butler Community College, and Susan B. Allen Memorial Hospital are anchor institutions shaping workforce development, healthcare strategy, and community quality of life.

Role: Workforce pipeline, healthcare expansion, community anchor

💼 Business Community

Private Sector Leadership: Local business leaders and developers provide strategic input on market feasibility, capital investment opportunities, and implementation sequencing.

Role: Market feedback, investment, partnerships, execution

👥 Community Input

Resident & Stakeholder Voice: Residents, workers, and community members can provide feedback through the comment process, public meetings, and engagement forums.

Role: Feedback, public comment, community validation

Methodology & Process

How This Plan Was Developed

Vision 2040 is rooted in three years of research, community engagement, and strategic analysis. This is not a speculative vision—it's grounded in assets that already exist and advantages that no comparable Kansas city can match simultaneously.

Research & Analysis Foundation

  • Economic Data Analysis — Census, Bureau of Labor Statistics, state economic databases, and proprietary market research
  • Competitive Benchmarking — Comparison to 12+ peer communities in Kansas, Oklahoma, and Texas with similar geographies and opportunities
  • Market Feasibility Studies — Third-party validation on industrial site viability, housing demand, tourism potential, and investment returns
  • Community Input Sessions — Stakeholder interviews with 50+ local leaders (business, government, education, healthcare, civic)
  • Resident Surveys — Community feedback on quality of life, priorities, and vision for El Dorado's future
  • Asset Audit — Comprehensive inventory of El Dorado's competitive advantages (lake, location, water, college, institutions, workforce)

Strategic Pillars Framework

The plan is organized around 8 interdependent pillars that work together to create a resilient, diversified economy:

  • Economy & Growth — Industrial diversification, FDI recruitment
  • Housing & Community — Mixed-income residential development
  • Place & Recreation — Lake tourism, downtown revitalization
  • People & Education — Workforce pipeline, K–12 excellence
  • Infrastructure & Build — Smart city, transportation, utilities
  • Finance & Investment — Sustainable funding model
  • Growth & Development — Neighborhood revitalization
  • Region & Planning — County integration, zoning alignment

Feedback Process

All feedback is reviewed by the City Manager and shared with relevant department heads and El Dorado Inc. Major themes or suggestions are discussed at quarterly City Council work sessions. If feedback indicates a need to revise strategy, that revision is presented to City Council and community for approval.

Living Document

How Vision 2040 Evolves

This is a living strategic plan, not a static document. It will be updated as market conditions change, new opportunities emerge, and community priorities shift.

📅 Quarterly Review

El Dorado Inc. and City leadership review implementation progress against 2026–2027 Phase 1 targets. Milestones tracking, funding status, and any critical blockers are discussed.

📊 Annual Update

Each January, the complete Vision 2040 plan is reviewed. Updated metrics (population, jobs, tax base), completed initiatives, and revised projections are published.

🔄 Major Revisions (Every 5 Years)

Every 5 years (2031, 2036), Vision 2040 undergoes a comprehensive review. Community input, market analysis, and updated financial modeling inform revisions.

🚨 Ad Hoc Updates (When Needed)

If major market shifts occur (tech boom, recession, supply chain disruption), the plan can be updated in response.
