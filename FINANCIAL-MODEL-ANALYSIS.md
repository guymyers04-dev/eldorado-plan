## SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

### Key Variables & Impact (Base Case)

**If Data Center Delays 12 Months:**
- Impact: -$100M capex in 2027, +$100M in 2028
- Population impact: -150 persons by 2030, neutral by 2035
- Tax revenue impact: -$600K Year 1, neutral by Year 2
- Verdict: Manageable delay; contingency activation recommended

**If Recession Hits 2027-2028:**
- Impact: -30% private capex; -50% FDI prospects; housing delayed 6 months
- Population impact: Base case → Conservative scenario
- Tax revenue impact: -40% 2028-2030, recover by 2032
- Verdict: Major hit; requires contingency activation; consider 2029 reset

**If Housing Financing Blocked:**
- Impact: Housing Phase 1 delayed 12-18 months or scaled to 50 units
- Population impact: -200 persons per year delay
- Tax revenue impact: -$300K annual until construction resumes
- Verdict: Manageable; activate CDFI backup; reduce capex scope

**If Water Rights Challenged:**
- Impact: Lose $10-20M annual revenue stream; data center advantage erodes
- Population impact: -5% growth; data center prospect probability -60%
- Tax revenue impact: -$50M cumulative loss (5-year)
- Verdict: Critical risk; USACE legal defense; diversify prospects

**If Broadband Parity Achieved Early (2027 vs. 2028):**
- Impact: El Dorado's internet competitive advantage timing shifts
- Population impact: Accelerated Wichita spillover migration (POSITIVE)
- Tax revenue impact: Upside potential +10% if captures remote worker cohort
- Verdict: Opportunity; marketing acceleration recommended
