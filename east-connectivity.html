East El Dorado Connectivity

Infrastructure & Neighborhood Integration

Bridging disconnected east-side neighborhoods with pedestrian/bike infrastructure, commercial access roads, utilities, and mixed-income housing

Why East El Dorado: Addressing Infrastructure Gaps

Current Challenges

East El Dorado neighborhoods face infrastructure isolation:

  • Limited pedestrian/bike infrastructure (no connected greenways)
  • Older residential areas disconnected from downtown core
  • Industrial areas lack modern commercial road access
  • Utility constraints (water/sewer) limit new development
  • Opportunity: Strategic infrastructure investments unlock new development potential

📊 East Side Overview

  • Current population: ~3,500–4,000
  • Growth potential: 1,000–1,500 new residents (with infrastructure)
  • Land available: 200–300 acres developable
  • Employment centers: Industrial park, small manufacturer, warehouse

Connectivity Strategy: Three Pillars

  • Greenways & Pedestrian: Connected trail system linking neighborhoods to downtown, schools, parks
  • Roads & Commercial Access: Modern arterial roads supporting light industrial, logistics, commerce
  • Mixed-Income Housing: New residential development (starter homes, townhomes, apartments)

15-Year Connectivity Plan: Infrastructure-Led Development

Phase 1: Foundation (Years 1–3)

  • Greenways: 3–4 miles of pedestrian/bike trails linking neighborhoods
  • Road improvements: New arterial road(s) with modern standards (wider, turn lanes)
  • Utilities: Water/sewer extension design; capacity planning
  • Housing: 100–150 new units (starter homes, townhomes)
  • Investment: $12–16M

Phase 2: Acceleration (Years 4–7)

  • Greenway completion: Full network connecting all neighborhoods to downtown
  • Commercial nodes: 2–3 micro-commercial centers (grocery, retail, services)
  • Mixed-use development: Apartments, townhomes, small office buildings
  • Housing: 200–250 additional units
  • Investment: $22–32M (cumulative)

Phase 3: Integration (Years 8–15)

  • Complete connectivity: East El Dorado fully integrated with downtown core
  • Full build-out: Total 500–600 new units
  • Employment centers: Light industrial, logistics, small manufacturing hub
  • Investment: $35–45M (cumulative)

Economic Impact: Housing Supply, Job Creation, Tax Base

Direct Benefits

  • Housing supply: 500–600 new units (addresses regional shortage)
  • Tax base: $35–45M new residential value = $280K–360K annual property tax
  • Employment: 200–300 jobs (light industrial, logistics, retail, services)
  • Payroll: $8–12M annually

Neighborhood Quality

  • Walkability improvement: Greenways enable pedestrian access to downtown, schools, parks
  • Safety: Better-lit, maintained public spaces; increased foot traffic
  • Community identity: Unified east side; neighborhood cohesion

Implementation: City, County, Private Developers

🏛️ City of El Dorado

  • Adopt East Connectivity Master Plan
  • Design and construct greenway network
  • Plan arterial road improvements (coordinate with County)
  • Identify sites for micro-commercial centers

💧 Utilities

  • Water/sewer extension capacity planning
  • Electric/telecom underground infrastructure
  • Investment: $6–10M (utilities infrastructure)

💼 Private Developers

  • Residential development (500–600 units)
  • Commercial/industrial development (light manufacturing, logistics)
  • Retail/services at micro-centers
