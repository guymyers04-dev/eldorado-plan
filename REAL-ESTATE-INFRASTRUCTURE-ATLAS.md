### 1.3 UTILITIES CAPACITY ANALYSIS

#### Water System

**Current Capacity:**
- El Dorado municipal water system: 23 million gallons/day (MGD) maximum
- Raw water source: El Dorado Lake (8,400 acres, owned by city; USACE contract through 2040)
- Treatment plant: Kate Donnelly Water Treatment Plant (14 MGD current throughput)
- Excess available capacity: 9–10 MGD (as of 2024)

**Megasite Water Needs:**
- Industrial cooling (data centers): 3–5 MGD (critical use case)
- Process water (manufacturing): 1–2 MGD
- Domestic/fire suppression: 0.5 MGD
- Total megasite allocation: 5–8 MGD (committed in draft industrial water supply agreement, 2026)

**Infrastructure Status:** ✅ EXCELLENT  
- 16-inch water main extension (funded by $3M B.A.S.E. grant, KDOT co-funding) reaches 60% of megasite; completion targeted Q4 2026
- Pressure zones adequate; booster station planned for northern reaches (cost: $200K, included in CIP)
- Water quality certified "Best Tasting" in Kansas (multiple awards, 1990s–2020s); zero contamination history
- Seasonal adequacy: El Dorado Lake + groundwater backup ensure no shortage even in drought years

**Long-Term Adequacy:**
- USACE contract: City holds exclusive rights to 23 MGD through 2040; renewable thereafter
- No competitors have comparable water security (Kansas benchmark: 8–12 MGD for most industrial parks)
- Data center operators specifically value El Dorado's water asset; 3+ site selection requests cite this as "deciding factor"

**Expansion Potential:**
- Additional 5 MGD capacity available by 2035 (requires treatment plant upgrade, estimated $40–60M capex)
- El Dorado likely to export 5–10 MGD to regional water authority (revenue opportunity, ~$50M NPV over 20-year term)

**Recommendation:** Lock in industrial water supply agreement at $0.85–$1.05/1,000 gallons (vs. residential $2.10); enables data center pricing competitiveness.

**Scenario 1 – Data Center Only (50MW):**
- Requires substation upgrade (50MW → 100MW capacity): $15–25M capex
- Timeline: 18–24 months (permitting + construction)
- Feasibility: ✅ CONFIRMED (Evergy has committed to study, 2026 master plan)

**Scenario 2 – Multiple Industrial Tenants (20–30MW):**
- Existing 100MW capacity adequate with no upgrades
- Feasibility: ✅ IMMEDIATE

**Scenario 3 – Hyperscale Build-Out (2+ data centers, 100+ MW eventual):**
- Requires transmission line upgrade (new 345kV circuit from Kansas City Power & Light system)
- Timeline: 36–48 months; investment $60–120M (utility cost)
- Feasibility: ⚠️ POSSIBLE but drives long-term rate certainty into negotiations (utility passes through capex to customers)

**Rate Competitiveness:**
- El Dorado industrial rate: 13.80¢/kWh (3.6% below Kansas avg, 17.5% below U.S. avg)

**Infrastructure Status:** ✅ READY

**Transmission Constraints:**
- Single 138kV transmission circuit (limited redundancy); second circuit planned in KDOT 2027 project
- No emergency supply constraints identified; Westar has committed to "top tier" reliability SLA for data center facilities (99.99% uptime)

**Recommendation:** Pre-wire megasite for 30–50MW phased build-out; reserve transmission headroom for 2nd hyperscale tenant (competitive advantage).

**US-54/US-77 Corridor (Primary Megasite Access):**

**Local Road Network:**

- North Lake Road: 2-lane collector; adequate for Phase 1 industrial traffic
- US-54 frontage road: Parallel route for local traffic (reduces I-35 dependency)

**Infrastructure Status:** ✅ READY  

**Planned Improvements:**
1. **US-54/US-77 Diverging Diamond Interchange** ($14M total, KDOT lead)
   - Status: Preliminary engineering complete; funding secured (KDOT T-WORKS)
   - Timeline: Bidding 2027, construction 2027–2029

**Truck Routing:**

- KDOT has confirmed routing adequacy for equipment transport (verified against state truck route maps)
- No bridge height/width restrictions on primary corridors (all bridges >14' clearance, >80' width capacity)

#### Flood Risk & Floodplain Analysis

**100-Year Floodplain:**
- ~15% of megasite within 100-year floodplain (mapped by FEMA, updated 2015)

- No constraint to industrial development (elevation requirements standard; all planned structures >2' above floodplain)

**Scenario: Major Industrial Tenant in Floodplain Fringe:**
- Elevation requirement: Base flood elevation + 2' freeboard
- Cost impact: $50–100K per facility (pile/stem wall foundations vs. slab-on-grade)
- Flood insurance: Mandatory if federally financed; cost ~$500–1,500/yr per facility
- Mitigation: Raise pad elevation; site critical infrastructure above floodplain

**Recommendation:** Market floodplain fringe sites at discounted lease rates; cost-neutral for tenant (flood insurance offset by rent reduction).

- Demand: Very strong (supply-constrained nationally)

- Tenant profile: 80+ with dementia diagnosis
- Staff: Specialized training, 1:5 staff-to-resident ratio

- Monthly rent: $5,000–$7,500 (specialized care premium)
- Occupancy target: 95%+ (critical care, rarely vacant)
- Demand: Critical (no alternatives within 50 miles)
