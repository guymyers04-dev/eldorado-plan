Why a Mega-Site Matters

Manufacturing and logistics operations need contiguous, carefully-planned industrial land. A 500+ acre master-planned park offers competitive advantages over scattered development:

✓ Site Certainty

Pre-planned utility capacity, zoning, environmental clearance, and infrastructure ready for immediate deployment.

✓ Cost Efficiency

Shared infrastructure reduces per-acre costs compared to scattered sites. Regional developer incentives + bulk procurement reduce site prep costs.

✓ Ecosystem

Multiple anchor tenants + suppliers create supply-chain advantages and skilled workforce concentration.

✓ Transportation Network

Regional highway access (US-77, US-54, I-35 proximity) makes for efficient logistics operations.

Land Assembly & Location

Mega-Site Site Analysis & Positioning

El Dorado's location between Wichita and the Oklahoma border, combined with available land and existing industrial infrastructure, makes it the ideal location for a regional industrial hub. The mega-site sits on the US-77 corridor, the state's primary north-south industrial transportation axis.

Site Characteristics

Physical Attributes

  • Total Land: 500-600 acres
  • Developable: 450-550 acres (5% reserved for stormwater, wetlands)
  • Topography: Relatively flat (ideal for manufacturing)
  • Soil Conditions: Industrial-suitable
  • Environmental: No major wetlands or protected habitat
  • Zoning: Existing heavy industrial or easy conversion

Strategic Position

  • Highway Access: US-77 (primary site frontage), US-54 (10 mi), I-35 (25 mi)
  • Distance to Wichita: 40 miles south (1-hour drive)
  • Distance to Oklahoma: 35 miles south
  • Regional Population: 350K+ within 100-mile radius
  • Labor Shed: 50K+ available workers
  • Railway Access: BNSF rail line adjacent to site

Competitive Advantages vs. Wichita

El Dorado Mega-Site

  • Land cost: $3,500-4,500/acre
  • Site prep: $75-100K/acre
  • Real estate tax: 18-20% discount
  • Local incentives: $1-2M per anchor
  • Permitting: 90-120 days
  • Workforce: Lower wage expectations

Typical Wichita Industrial

  • Land cost: $6,500-9,500/acre
  • Site prep: $120-180K/acre
  • Real estate tax: Market rate
  • Local incentives: $500K-1M
  • Permitting: 150-200 days
  • Workforce: Higher wage expectations

El Dorado's cost advantage = $40-60 per square foot for a 100,000 sf facility vs. Wichita. For a 500,000 sf plant, that's $20-30M in total cost savings.

Site Development

Mega-Site Infrastructure Requirements & Investment

Developing a 500+ acre industrial park requires significant upfront infrastructure investment: internal road networks, utility extensions, stormwater management, environmental compliance. Vision 2040 funds this through a combination of city capital, state infrastructure incentives, and private developer contributions.
