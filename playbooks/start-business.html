Overview

Starting a business in El Dorado is straightforward. This guide walks you through business formation, licensing, and accessing startup incentives designed to help new entrepreneurs succeed.

Whether you're a sole proprietor, LLC, or corporation, you'll need to register with the State of Kansas, obtain a local business license, get a federal EIN, and understand local permitting requirements. El Dorado offers tax incentive programs like PEAK to reduce your tax burden in your first years.

Timeline & Steps

Here are the key milestones for starting your business:

1

Choose Business Structure

Days 1-2
▼

What to do: Decide between sole proprietor, partnership, LLC, S-corp, or C-corp. Most El Dorado startups choose LLC for simplicity and tax benefits.

Documents needed: EIN application (IRS Form SS-4), business plan outline

Cost: Free to research; $100-300 filing fee to state

Common blockers: Not sure which structure? Contact City Clerk — they'll guide you based on your industry.

2

Register with Kansas Secretary of State

Days 2-3
▼

What to do: File Articles of Organization (LLC) or Articles of Incorporation (Corp) with the Kansas Secretary of State online at sos.kansas.gov.

Documents needed: Business name, registered agent, member/shareholder names, addresses

Cost: $110 (LLC), $125 (Corp)

Timeline: 2-3 business days for approval (or same-day rush for extra fee)

3

Apply for Federal EIN

Days 3-4
▼

What to do: Apply for an Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS at irs.gov/ein. This is free and takes 5 minutes online.

Documents needed: Business name, structure, principal business activity, responsible party SSN

Cost: Free

Timeline: Instant (digital assignment)

4

Obtain Local Business License

Days 4-5
▼

What to do: Apply for City of El Dorado business license at City Hall (403 W Main). Bring state registration and EIN letter.

Documents needed: State certificate of organization, EIN letter, proof of address, photo ID

Cost: $50-150 depending on business type

Timeline: 1-2 business days

5

Explore Startup Incentives

Days 5-7
▼

What to do: Apply for PEAK program (property tax abatement) if eligible. Eligible businesses can receive 50-75% property tax abatement for 5-10 years.

Documents needed: Business license, financial projections, application form

Cost: Free to apply

Timeline: 30 days for approval; retroactive to filing date

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can I get approved? ▼
Most businesses get licensed within 5-7 business days. State registration (Kansas Secretary of State) takes 2-3 days; EIN is instant; local license is 1-2 days. If you pay for expedited state filing, you can compress this to 3-4 days total.
What's the difference between LLC and Corporation? ▼
LLC (Limited Liability Company) offers liability protection with simpler taxation and fewer compliance requirements. Corporation offers more formal structure and is better for multi-investor companies. For most El Dorado startups, LLC is recommended. Talk to a tax advisor for your specific situation.
How much will it cost to start my business? ▼
Legal formation costs: $200-400 (state filing + local license). Add $1,000-5,000+ for actual business setup (location, equipment, inventory). El Dorado's PEAK program can reduce property taxes by 50-75% once you're licensed, paying back your setup costs over time.
Can I get a business license if I don't have a physical location yet? ▼
Yes. You can register with a home address or your personal residence initially. Once you secure a commercial location, update your registration. Many El Dorado startups begin from home offices, then move to downtown spaces or the business park.
What's the PEAK program and am I eligible? ▼
PEAK (Property Evaluation and Community Kickstart) provides property tax abatement for new or expanding businesses. You're eligible if your business is new to El Dorado, expanding operations, or relocating. Abatement is 50-75% for 5-10 years. Apply at City Hall within 60 days of starting. Brandon Erickson (Economic Development) can walk you through it.
