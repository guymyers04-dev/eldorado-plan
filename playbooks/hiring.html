Overview

El Dorado has a strong labor force with 25,000+ workers within 30-minute commute. Unemployment in Butler County is 3.5%, and local training programs (USD 490, Butler Community College) actively prepare workers for manufacturing, logistics, healthcare, and skilled trades positions.

This guide helps you post jobs, access pre-trained candidates, leverage workforce development funding (WIOA), and execute relocation assistance programs. Most employers fill positions within 30-45 days through local channels. Training subsidies can reduce hiring costs by 30-50%.

Timeline & Steps

Here's the typical process for recruiting and hiring workers in El Dorado:

1

Define Roles & Partner with Training Programs

Days 1-10
▼

What to do: Identify positions you need to fill. Define job titles, skills, wages, start dates. Contact USD 490 and Butler CC to discuss pre-training partnerships (CDL, welding, CNC, general manufacturing).

Available programs: CDL (Commercial Driver License) training, welding certification, CNC operator, manufacturing basics, healthcare certifications

Cost: Free consultation; training subsidies 30-50% available through WIOA

Timeline: 5-10 days to define roles and initiate partnerships

2

Post Job Openings & Screen Candidates

Days 10-30
▼

What to do: Post jobs on your website, local job boards, and Kansas Department of Labor site (free). Work with Workforce Development to identify pre-screened candidates from training programs.

Channels: Kansas DOL (free), Indeed, LinkedIn, local chamber, direct referrals from training programs

Pre-training candidates: Graduates from USD 490 CDL/welding/CNC programs often available (50+ trained annually)

Timeline: 15-20 days from posting to candidate interviews

3

Interview, Assess, & Offer Employment

Days 25-40
▼

What to do: Conduct interviews with candidates. Assess technical skills, cultural fit, background check. Make employment offers. If sourced from training programs, eligible candidates may trigger WIOA incentive payments ($1K-5K per hire).

WIOA incentives: Employers who hire WIOA-eligible candidates receive $1K-5K per hire from state funding (no cost to you)

Cost: None (incentives paid by state)

Timeline: 15 days from interviews to offer acceptance

4

On-Board & Execute Training

Days 40-60
▼

What to do: Employee starts on agreed date. Begin on-boarding and on-the-job training. If hired from training program, you may be eligible for employer training incentives (wage subsidies for first 90 days).

Training support: On-the-job training grants (OJT) can subsidize 50% of wages for first 90 days for eligible candidates

Cost: Your standard wages; training subsidies cover portion (no additional cost)

Timeline: 20 days

5

Retain & Support Long-Term Employment

Ongoing (Days 60+)
▼

What to do: Provide mentorship, advancement opportunities, and career development. Access continuing education programs through Butler CC and USD 490. Address housing/relocation needs (if hiring from out-of-area).

Retention support: Continuing education grants, apprenticeship programs, career advancement planning

Relocation assistance: Housing down-payment assistance available through city (up to $25K for new residents)

Timeline: Ongoing

Workforce Training & Funding Programs

CDL (Commercial Driver License) Training
30-40 graduates per year | 50% of wages subsidized (first 90 days)

What it is: 4-6 week intensive training for commercial driver licenses. Pre-trained graduates available for logistics, transportation, and delivery roles.

Cost to employer: Free (training funded by state). WIOA incentive: $2K-3K per hire. Training subsidy: 50% wage reimbursement for 90-day OJT.

How to access: Contact USD 490 Workforce Coordinator to identify graduates and pre-hire candidates. Coordinate start dates.

WIOA (Workforce Innovation & Opportunity Act)
Up to $5K per hire | 50-100% wage subsidy for 90 days | Free training

What it is: Federal workforce program provides hiring incentives, training subsidies, and case management for eligible workers.

Eligibility: Hire workers ages 18-24 (youth), 25+ (adults), or dislocated workers. Preference for low-income, welfare recipients, veterans.

Employer benefits: $1K-5K per hire + wage subsidy (50-100% of wages for first 90 days) + free case management.

How to access: Submit job requisitions to Workforce Development. They pre-screen and refer eligible candidates. No cost to you.

Manufacturing & Skilled Trades Training
15-25 graduates per year | Welding, CNC, industrial maintenance

What it is: Butler Community College offers welding, CNC, and industrial maintenance certificates (6-12 months). USD 490 offers manufacturing basics (4 weeks).

Cost to employer: Free training (funded by state grants). Graduates typically available for hire within 2-4 weeks of course completion.

Custom training: If you need specialized skills, Butler CC can develop customized training (subsidized).

How to access: Contact Butler CC workforce liaison. Provide job descriptions. Program coordinator matches graduates to your needs.

Employer Training Incentive (On-the-Job Training)
50-100% wage subsidy for first 90 days | Up to $5K per employee

What it is: Reimburses employers for wage costs of newly hired workers during training period.

Eligibility: Hire WIOA-eligible workers (low-income, dislocated, youth, or veterans). Standard wage training acceptable.

How it works: You pay normal wages. State reimburses 50-100% based on wage level and worker category. No out-of-pocket training cost.

How to access: Contact Workforce Development. They process reimbursement directly once worker is hired and tracking begins.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will hiring local workers cost me? ▼
Your standard wage (market rate for the position). No training surcharge. WIOA-eligible workers may trigger $1K-5K hiring incentives (paid to you by state). On-the-job training subsidy can reimburse 50-100% of wages for first 90 days. Net cost for first 90 days can be 0-50% of standard wages depending on worker eligibility and training level.
How long does it take to fill a position? ▼
Typical timeline: 30-45 days from posting to hire. Fast-track (pre-trained CDL/welding candidates): 2-3 weeks. Slow (specialized skills not available locally): 60-90 days. Work with Workforce Development to post jobs — they actively recruit and screen candidates, accelerating timeline. Get job descriptions to them early and update as candidates apply.
Are there housing assistance programs for relocated workers? ▼
Yes. El Dorado offers down-payment assistance (up to $25K) for new residents relocating for employment. Low-interest mortgages (3-4%) available through city partnerships. Rental assistance also available. Contact City Hall (Amanda Foster or Brandon Erickson) for application and eligibility. This is a powerful recruitment tool when hiring from out-of-area.
Can I get custom training if I need specialized skills? ▼
Yes. Butler Community College develops customized training programs (2-12 weeks depending on skill). Contact Thomas Blackwell with your job descriptions and skill requirements. College works with you to design curriculum, source instructors, and deliver training. Typically partially subsidized by state grants. Timeline: 4-8 weeks to launch custom program.
What happens if I need to hire quickly — can I get people trained faster? ▼
Fast options: (1) Hire pre-trained CDL/welding graduates immediately available (2-3 weeks), (2) WIOA on-the-job training model (hire and train internally, state subsidizes wages), (3) Accelerated custom training (intensive 2-4 week programs), (4) Interim staffing through temp agencies while training. Talk to Workforce Development about your timeline — they can recommend fastest path.
Manufacturing Company Workforce Expansion
Manufacturing company needed to hire 15 production workers for facility expansion. Worked with USD 490 to identify 8 pre-trained CDL and manufacturing-certified workers available immediately. Applied for WIOA funding for remaining 7 positions.
"We posted positions in January. By March, we had all 15 hires on board. 8 came from pre-training programs (ready in 2 weeks). 7 came through WIOA (initial hire + 90-day training). Total subsidy value: $25K (5 x $5K WIOA incentive + wage subsidies). Without these programs, hiring would have taken 4-5 months and cost $200K+ in recruiting fees. El Dorado's workforce ecosystem is world-class." — HR Director

Today: 70 employees, retention rate 92% (first year), planning Phase 2 with 20 more hires
