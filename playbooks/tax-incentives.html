Overview

El Dorado offers multiple tax incentive programs that can save your business $100K to $5M+ depending on your investment size and job creation. Whether you're starting a new business, expanding operations, or relocating to our community, these programs are designed to offset capital costs and accelerate profitability.

This guide explains the PEAK program (property tax abatement), REAP program (rural economic incentives), local grants, and how to calculate your total net incentive value. Most applications take 2-3 months and can be filed retroactively once you're licensed.

Tax Incentive Programs Explained

PEAK Program (Property Evaluation & Community Kickstart)
Typical Value: $500K - $5M+ over 10 years

What it is: Property tax abatement for new or expanding businesses. Reduces your property tax bill by 50-75% for 5-10 years.

Eligibility: New business to El Dorado, expanding operations, or relocating from out-of-state. Must create minimum 3 new jobs or invest $500K+.

How to apply: Submit to City Hall within 60 days of business licensing. Retroactive to licensing date.

Example: $2M facility, 10% property tax rate = $200K/year tax. 50% abatement over 10 years = $1M savings.

REAP Program (Rural Economic Area Program)
Typical Value: $250K - $2M+ over 10 years

What it is: State-level rural incentive providing sales tax exemption on equipment and reduced income tax for 5 years.

Eligibility: Business must be in designated rural economic area (El Dorado qualifies), create jobs, and manufacturing or agriculture-related.

How to apply: Apply through state with local city endorsement. Processing: 20-30 days.

Example: $1M equipment purchase = $70K sales tax exemption (7% KS rate). Plus 5% income tax reduction on first $100K profit = $5K/year.

Equipment Sales Tax Exemption
Typical Value: $50K - $500K (one-time)

What it is: Manufacturing and ag equipment purchases exempt from 7% Kansas sales tax. Available to qualifying manufacturers.

Eligibility: Licensed manufacturer in El Dorado with equipment purchases for business use.

How to apply: Register with Tax Assessor and get exemption certificate. Present to vendors at time of purchase.

Example: $500K equipment purchase x 7% = $35K in tax savings.

Local Discretionary Grants
Typical Value: $25K - $250K (one-time)

What it is: City of El Dorado discretionary grants for workforce training, infrastructure, or job creation in priority areas.

Eligibility: Highest priority for strategic industries (manufacturing, logistics, technology). Requires job creation and matching investment.

How to apply: Submit request to Economic Development Director. Reviewed quarterly.

Example: $100K grant for CDL training program (50% of $200K training budget) for logistics company.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I stack multiple incentive programs together? ▼
Yes! You can typically combine PEAK (local property tax) + REAP (state equipment) + equipment exemption. However, some restrictions apply — for example, you cannot receive PEAK abatement and a local discretionary grant for the same asset. Contact Tax Assessor for your specific situation. Typical strategy: PEAK for real estate, REAP + equipment exemption for equipment purchases.
How long does the incentive period last? ▼
PEAK: Typically 5-10 years depending on project size and job creation. REAP: 5 years for equipment, 5 years for income tax reduction. Equipment exemption: One-time, applied at purchase. Local grants: One-time cash awards. Most businesses see full payback of incentive applications within first 2-3 years.
What happens if I don't meet my job creation commitment? ▼
PEAK includes clawback provisions — if you don't meet job commitments within the agreed timeline, the incentive may be reduced or terminated. Typical grace period: 1-2 years. Work with Economic Development if you face headwinds — they have flexibility and can adjust timelines. Transparent communication is key.
Can I apply retroactively if I already started my business? ▼
Yes! PEAK incentives can be applied retroactively to the date you filed your business license (within 60 days). REAP must be applied within 180 days of facility startup. If you're already operating, apply immediately — you can recapture past incentive value. Contact Brandon Erickson (Economic Development) to check if you qualify.
What's the approval rate for incentive applications? ▼
PEAK: ~85% approval rate for qualified applicants. Denials are rare and typically due to incorrect filings or ineligible business types. REAP: ~70% (more competitive, state-level). Equipment exemptions: 100% for registered manufacturers. Local discretionary grants: ~40% (most selective). Work with Economic Development to maximize approval odds — they'll help you strengthen your application.
