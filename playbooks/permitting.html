Overview

El Dorado uses Kansas adopted building codes (IECC 2015 residential, IBC 2015 commercial) and has streamlined permit processes to support construction. Whether you're building a new facility, expanding existing space, or renovating, you'll need building permits and will pass through multiple inspection checkpoints.

This guide walks you through permit applications, the inspection process, code compliance, expedited permitting options, and appeals procedures. Most construction permits are issued within 5-10 business days, and projects can proceed immediately after permit issuance.

Timeline & Steps

Here's the typical permitting and construction timeline:

1

Permit Application & Document Submission

Days 1-5
▼

What to do: Submit completed building permit application to City Building Official with all required plans, specs, and drawings.

Documents needed: Completed permit form, site plans (2 sets), architectural/engineering drawings (3 sets), electrical plans, MEP (mechanical/electrical/plumbing) plans

Cost: Permit fee $300-2,000 depending on project value (typically $0.50-1.00 per square foot)

Timeline: Submit in morning, informal review same day (identify missing items)

2

Plan Review & Code Compliance

Days 5-15
▼

What to do: Building Official and inspector review plans against Kansas codes. Identify any deviations or missing information. Work with your engineer to address comments.

Documents needed: Responses to Building Official's comments, revised plans if applicable

Cost: Free (included in permit fee)

Timeline: 7-10 days typically; expedited plan review available (add $500-1K, 3-5 days)

Common issues: Egress windows, fire separation, accessibility (ADA), mechanical adequacy

3

Permit Issuance & Foundation Inspection

Days 15-25
▼

What to do: Permit is issued. Contractor receives permit documents and schedule. Foundation/excavation work begins. Call for foundation inspection before pouring concrete or backfilling.

Documents needed: Permit issuance letter, construction schedule

Cost: Included in permit fee

Inspection requirements: Inspector verifies footer depth, rebar placement, forms, soil conditions. Must pass before concrete pour.

4

Framing & Rough-In Inspections

Days 25-50
▼

What to do: Frame construction proceeds. Call for framing inspection before drywall. Inspector verifies structural integrity, stud spacing, lintels, blocking.

Rough-in inspections: Electrical (wiring before drywall), plumbing (pipes before walls close), HVAC (ductwork before sealing)

Cost: Included in permit fee (typically 4-5 inspections total)

Inspection timeline: Inspector typically available within 2-3 business days of request. Call early to schedule.

5

Final Inspections & Certificate of Occupancy

Days 50-75
▼

What to do: Final inspection verifies all work complete and code compliant. Inspector walks through with contractor, notes any punch-list items. Once passed, Certificate of Occupancy (CO) issued.

Inspection checklist: Electrical final, plumbing final, HVAC final, fire safety (extinguishers, alarms), accessibility (handrails, ramps), code compliance

Punch list: Minor items (paint, trim, touch-ups) can proceed even if CO is conditional. Must be completed within 30 days.

Certificate of Occupancy: Allows building to be occupied. Required before business can open.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get a building permit? ▼
Standard timeline: 10-15 business days from complete submission to permit issuance. Expedited option: 3-5 business days for additional $500-1K fee. Small projects (under 5,000 sqft): Often approved within 5 days. Large projects (over 50,000 sqft): May take 20 days if plan review reveals issues. Submit on Monday morning for fastest turnaround; avoid Friday submissions.
Which Kansas building codes apply in El Dorado? ▼
El Dorado adopts: International Building Code (IBC 2015) for commercial, International Residential Code (IRC 2015) for residential. Kansas wind loads and environmental requirements apply. Energy Code: IECC 2015 with Kansas amendments. ADA accessibility standards mandatory. Building Official can clarify specific code questions. Get code summary before design phase to avoid costly redesigns.
What happens if my project fails inspection? ▼
Inspector issues written report with specific deficiencies. Most failures are minor (framing spacing, improper blocking, electrical labeling). Contractor makes corrections and requests re-inspection (usually within 2-3 days). Re-inspection is free. Major failures (structural issues, egress problems) require engineer review. Process typically adds 5-10 days. Prepare contractors for inspections with review meetings.
Can I expedite the permitting process? ▼
Yes. Options: (1) Expedited plan review ($500-1K, 3-5 days), (2) Pre-submission meeting with Building Official (clarify code questions before formal submission, saves 10 days), (3) Parallel inspections (multiple inspections on same day), (4) Priority scheduling (if project is city-priority). Maximum acceleration: 15-20 days saved. Talk to Building Official about your timeline early.
What if I disagree with an inspection decision? ▼
Appeals process: (1) Request clarification from inspector in writing, (2) If unsatisfied, request appeal meeting with Building Official, (3) If still disagreed, appeal to City's Board of Appeals. Include engineer's opinion with your appeal. Most appeals are resolved within 5-10 days. Building Official is willing to discuss — don't ignore. Document all conversations.

El Dorado's process is efficient." — Project Manager
