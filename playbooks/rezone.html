Overview

El Dorado zoning regulations cover residential, commercial, industrial, and mixed-use development. If your property's current zoning doesn't match your intended use, you have two options: request a variance (minor adjustments to current zoning rules) or apply for rezoning (formal change to a different zone classification).

This guide explains the difference, walks you through the approval process, and shows you how to prepare a strong application that passes Planning Commission and City Council review. Most applications take 60-120 days and have a 75%+ approval rate when properly prepared.

Timeline & Steps

Here's the typical process for rezoning or variance requests:

1

Determine Current Zoning & Permitted Uses

Days 1-5
▼

What to do: Look up your property on the City zoning map. Review the current zone classification and what uses are permitted (as-of-right vs. conditional).

Documents needed: Property address, current zoning map printout, parcel ID

Cost: Free (zoning map available at City Hall or online)

Resource: City Planner can clarify permitted uses for your current zone.

2

Determine Variance vs. Rezone Necessity

Days 5-15
▼

What to do: Meet with City Planner to determine if your request needs a variance (minor adjustment) or full rezoning. Variance is faster (45-60 days); rezoning is longer (90-120 days) but broader.

Variance use case: "I want to use my commercial zone property for mixed-use residential + retail" — minor adjustment to existing commercial zone

Rezone use case: "I want to convert residential property to industrial manufacturing" — full zone change required

Cost: Free consultation

3

Prepare Application & Site Plan

Days 15-35
▼

What to do: Prepare formal application with site plan, building renderings, use description, and justification. Explain why the variance/rezone serves the public interest.

Documents needed: Site plan (engineer-prepared), architectural renderings, property survey, traffic impact analysis (if applicable), parking plan

Cost: $500-2,000 for professional site plan/renderings

Timeline: 15-20 days with professional help

4

Submit to Planning Commission & Publish Notice

Days 35-50
▼

What to do: Submit complete application to City Planner. City will advertise public hearing in local newspaper. You must notify adjacent property owners.

Documents needed: All items from Step 3

Cost: Application fee $250-500; noticing costs ~$100

Timeline: 15 days (required legal notice period)

5

Planning Commission Public Hearing

Days 45-65
▼

What to do: Attend Planning Commission meeting, present your case, respond to questions from commission and public. Commission votes on recommendation (approve/deny/conditional).

Documents needed: Your presentation materials, response to any public comments

Cost: Your time (or attorney if needed)

Timeline: 15-20 days from notice deadline

6

City Council Vote & Final Approval

Days 65-120
▼

What to do: City Council holds second public hearing, considers Planning Commission recommendation, votes on final approval. If approved, ordinance is effective 10 days after signing.

Documents needed: Same as Planning Commission hearing

Cost: None additional

Timeline: 20-55 days (depends on City Council schedule)

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a variance and rezoning? ▼
Variance: Allows you to deviate from current zoning rules (e.g., build 5% closer to property line in a commercial zone). Faster, cheaper, temporary relief. Rezoning: Changes the property's zone classification entirely (e.g., residential to commercial). Slower, permanent, affects future uses. Use variance for minor adjustments; use rezoning for major changes. City Planner can advise.
How long does the approval process take? ▼
Variance: 45-60 days. Rezoning: 90-120 days. Timeline starts when you submit a complete application and ends when ordinance is final (10 days after City Council vote). Delays can occur if application is incomplete or controversial. Prepare thoroughly and work closely with City Planner to avoid delays.
What's the approval rate? ▼
Variance: 85% approval rate (most are compatible with neighborhood). Rezoning: 75% approval rate (more scrutiny, especially residential to commercial). Success depends on: (1) Complete application, (2) Support from neighbors, (3) Alignment with comprehensive plan, (4) Good presentation. Work with City Planner to strengthen your case.
Can I accelerate the timeline? ▼
Slightly. Fast-track options: (1) Prepare impeccable application upfront (saves 10 days on back-and-forth), (2) Get neighbor support letters before submission (removes delays from public comment), (3) Ask City to schedule hearing early in planning cycle (saves 15-20 days). Maximum acceleration: 10-20 days. Plan for 60-90 days as base timeline.
What if the Planning Commission recommends denial? ▼
You can still appeal to City Council. City Council can override commission recommendation. However, this is difficult and requires strong evidence that commission erred. Better strategy: Work with City Planner and neighbors before commission hearing to build consensus. If denial is likely, ask for continuance and revise your proposal.
