Overview

El Dorado offers strategic advantages for manufacturing operations: prime location on US-77 for supply chain efficiency, competitive utility rates, skilled workforce access via USD 490 and community college partnerships, and attractive tax incentives (REAP, PEAK, and abatement programs) that can offset 30-50% of capital expansion costs.

This guide walks you through site evaluation, utility assessment, workforce planning, incentive applications, and financial closeout. Whether you're expanding your current facility or relocating from out-of-state, we'll help you navigate the process efficiently.

Timeline & Steps

Here's the typical 60-90 day process for manufacturing expansion or relocation:

1

Site Evaluation & Selection

Days 1-10
▼

What to do: Review available industrial sites in El Dorado and surrounding areas. Evaluate location vs. supply chain needs (I-35/US-77 corridor, rail access).

Documents needed: Site specifications, floor plan requirements, utilities load estimates

Cost: Broker fees (if applicable), site visits

Key resources: Contact economic development director for current industrial inventory.

2

Zoning & Regulatory Review

Days 5-20
▼

What to do: Confirm zoning compatibility, environmental compliance (air quality, water discharge), and any required variance or variance requests.

Documents needed: Site plan, operational descriptions, environmental impact summary

Cost: Variance applications $500-2,000 if needed

Timeline: 15-30 days if variance required

3

Utility Infrastructure Assessment

Days 10-25
▼

What to do: Work with city utilities director to assess electrical, water, and wastewater capacity. Determine upgrade needs and costs.

Documents needed: Utility load calculations, equipment specifications, water/wastewater volume projections

Cost: Utility upgrades typically $50K-$1M+ depending on facility size; El Dorado shares ~30% of costs for priority projects

Common blockers: Undersized capacity. Solutions: Phase expansion or upgrade municipal infrastructure (eligible for REAP grants).

4

Workforce Planning & Training

Days 15-45
▼

What to do: Define job categories, skill requirements, and wage scales. Engage USD 490 and Butler Community College for pre-training partnerships.

Documents needed: Job descriptions, wage projections, training timeline

Cost: Free assessments; training typically $5K-$50K (often shared with state WIOA funding)

Available programs: CDL training for logistics, manufacturing cert programs, apprenticeships

5

Tax Incentive Applications

Days 30-60
▼

What to do: Apply for REAP (Rural Economic Area Program), PEAK abatement, and equipment exemptions. Coordinate with economic development director and tax assessor.

Documents needed: Business plan, financial projections, job creation commitment, equipment lists

Cost: Free to apply; typical incentive value: $100K-$5M+ depending on project

Timeline: 20-30 days for approval; retroactive to filing date

6

Financial Closeout & Lease/Purchase

Days 60-90
▼

What to do: Finalize real estate terms, secure financing, close on property or execute lease. Execute equipment orders and facility construction.

Documents needed: Loan approvals, purchase agreements or leases, insurance policies

Cost: Standard real estate closing costs (2-5% of purchase price)

Timeline: 30-60 days depending on financing

Frequently Asked Questions

What manufacturing sites are available in El Dorado? ▼
El Dorado has 3 main industrial zones: Business Park (15 acres available, $8-12/sqft), South Industrial Corridor (20+ acres, $6-10/sqft), and the emerging US-77 Logistics Hub (50+ acres planned for larger facilities). Contact Brandon Erickson for current inventory and site packages.
How much can utilities cost for a manufacturing facility? ▼
Water & wastewater: $2-4 per 1,000 gallons for industrial rates. Electrical: $0.08-0.11 per kWh (favorable for Kansas). Natural gas: $4-6 per MMBtu. For a 50,000 sqft facility with moderate utility load, budget $15K-25K/month in utility costs. Larger projects may qualify for negotiated rates.
What kind of workforce is available locally? ▼
Butler County has 25,000+ potential workers within 30-minute commute. Current unemployment is 3.5%. USD 490 operates CDL training (50 graduates/year), welding certification (30/year), and CNC training (20/year). Butler CC offers manufacturing and logistics cert programs. For specialized roles, WIOA funding can subsidize up to 50% of training costs.
What tax incentives apply to manufacturing expansion? ▼
REAP Program: 5-year property tax holiday for new equipment and facility. PEAK: 50-75% property tax abatement for 5-10 years. Equipment exemption: Manufacturing equipment purchases exempt from sales tax if registered. Typical incentive value: $500K-$5M over 10 years depending on capital investment and job creation.
Can I accelerate the 60-90 day timeline? ▼
Yes. Fast-track options: (1) Parallel processing — start workforce planning and incentive apps while evaluating sites, (2) Pre-approved sites — some industrial zones have pre-zoned configurations saving 10-15 days, (3) City support — mark city projects as "priority" in planning queue for 25% timeline reduction. Talk to City Manager for expedited pathway.
