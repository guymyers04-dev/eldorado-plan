El Dorado Lake & Sailing

Kansas's most-visited state park — 8,000 acres, 1M+ annual visitors, zero commercial infrastructure

📷 Gallery photos are placeholders — to update with real El Dorado Lake images, replace URLs in image tags

📍 At a Glance

El Dorado Lake is Kansas's largest and most-visited state park, drawing over 1 million visitors annually. Yet the city captures virtually no economic value because there is zero lakefront dining, no boutique lodging, and no commercial amenities. Vision 2040's lake strategy focuses on building the commercial infrastructure to serve demand that already exists.

🏆 Current State

  • 8,000-acre lake surface
  • 1.1M+ annual visitors (2022+)
  • 1,100 campsites across 4 areas
  • 6 boat ramps, 2 swimming beaches
  • 7 hiking trails year-round
  • Walnut Valley Sailing Club
  • $0 lakefront dining
  • $0 boutique lodging

💡 The Opportunity

  • Every visitor dollar currently exits Butler County
  • 1.1M visitor days = massive untapped demand
  • Comparable lakes (Clinton Lake, Lawrence, KS) generate $2M+ annually
  • State park rules restrict commercial activity inside park boundaries
  • Vision 2040 builds commercial zones on city land adjacent to the lake
  • Private operators lease from city — zero risk to municipal budget

📊 Vision 2040 Strategy

  • Phase 1: West Marina (150 slips), Fishing Infrastructure, Wildlife Observation
  • Phase 2: East Marina Expansion, Lakeside Commercial Strip, Glamping Village
  • Phase 3: Amphitheater, Watersports Center, Wake Sports Park
  • Est. $65M+ total investment
  • Est. $15M-20M annual visitor spending capture
  • Est. 200-300 permanent jobs

Vision 2040 Lake Projects

Phase 1 Priority · $9M
⛵

West Side Marina Complex

The most impactful single project for El Dorado Lake is a new full-service marina on the western shore — closest to the city and the North Gateway District. The existing east marina is undersized and difficult to access from downtown. A west-side facility creates a seamless connection between the new neighborhood and the water, unlocking everything else on this list.

Specifications:

  • 150 full-service boat slips
  • Fuel dock & ship's store
  • Electric boat charging (first in western KS)
  • Pump-out & water service stations
  • Wave-attenuating breakwater
  • 80 seasonal dry-stack storage
  • 25-year private operator lease

26-Mile Circumnavigation Trail

Paved multi-use trail encircling El Dorado Lake, seamlessly connecting to the Walnut River Trail into downtown. A signature recreational route establishing El Dorado Lake as a major cycling and walking destination in Kansas.

Trail Infrastructure:

  • 26 miles of paved trail
  • 4 major trailhead parking areas
  • 8 rest shelters & shade structures
  • Interpretive signage (watershed, history)
  • ADA-accessible sections
  • $100–250 per visitor daily spend (comparable trails)

Fishing Infrastructure Upgrades

El Dorado Lake has some of the best walleye and crappie fishing in Kansas — currently underdeveloped. Strategic infrastructure investment positions the lake as a regional competitive fishing tournament destination.

New Infrastructure:

  • 4 ADA-compliant lighted fishing piers
  • Expanded fish cleaning station
  • Fish attractor structures (6 coves)
  • Trophy walleye stocking program (KDWPT)
  • Tournament infrastructure
  • $1.5M+ annual tournament visitor spending (comp. lakes)

Vision 2040 Lake Strategy

El Dorado Lake draws over 1 million visitors annually — yet the city captures virtually no economic benefit. Vision 2040 focuses on building commercial infrastructure to serve existing demand: boutique lodging, dining, and entertainment on city-owned land adjacent to the state park.

🏖️ Commercial Infrastructure

West Marina expansion (150 slips), lakefront restaurants, boutique hotel, amphitheater for events

🏕️ Lodging & Hospitality

35-room boutique hotel, 40-unit glamping village (private concession), RV park with full hookups

🚴 Recreation & Access

26-mile circumnavigation trail, expanded boat launches, watersports center, nature education
