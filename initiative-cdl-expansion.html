Initiative Overview

What Is It?

Scale USD 490's CDL training program from initial 40-50 graduates/year to 80-100 by Year 3-4, with partnerships to local CDL schools to increase capacity. Prepares for 150+ driver demand from regional logistics hub.

Why It Matters

Logistics hub anchors multiple warehouse/distribution centers needing 150+ drivers by 2030. Local CDL training reduces recruitment costs, improves retention (workers with family ties), and strengthens community job stability.

Expansion Strategy

1
USD 490 In-House Growth (Months 1-12)
Expand USD 490 program from 40-50 to 60-70 graduates. Add second instructor, second training vehicle, facility expansion (2,000 sf).
2
School District Partnership (Months 13-24)
Formalize partnerships with 2-3 commercial CDL schools. Joint branding, referral agreements, articulation pathways. Combined output: 80-100/year.
3
Dedicated Training Center (Months 25-36)
Build small dedicated CDL center (5,000 sf) with classroom, range, simulator. Capacity: 100+ graduates/year sustained.
