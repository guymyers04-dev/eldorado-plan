## THE THREE STRUCTURAL ADVANTAGES THAT MATTER

### 1. WATER RIGHTS (El Dorado's #1 Differentiator)
- **Owned:** 23M gallons/day capacity (USACE contract, 50+ year security)
- **Competitors:** All rely on municipal supply (uncertain)
- **Strategic Use:** Data center recruitment (water = deal-maker/breaker); advanced manufacturing; ag processing
- **Market Value:** Single data center deal = $300M+ capital investment, 500+ jobs, $50M+ tax revenue
- **Action:** Lead every FDI pitch with water security story

### 2. I-35 DIRECT ACCESS (El Dorado's #2 Differentiator)
- **Geography:** Exits 71, 76 (direct node, not bypass)
- **Competitors:** Emporia (junction, requires detour); others off-corridor
- **Advantage:** BNSF Intermodal Park (Newton, 30 mi) + I-35 corridor = seamless supply chain for logistics/manufacturing
- **Daily Traffic:** 20,000 vehicles/day = opportunity for truck stops, EV charging, service economy
- **Action:** Position as "I-35 Advanced Manufacturing Corridor" hub

### 3. STATE PARK ASSET (El Dorado's #3 Differentiator)
- **Ranking:** #1 most-visited Kansas state park (1M+ annual visitors)
- **Competitors:** Regional parks only; none comparable
- **Opportunity:** Currently UNDERDEVELOPED; 26-venue activation plan captures $75M–$150M annual tourist spending
- **Quality-of-Life:** Young professionals, families attracted to outdoor recreation
- **Action:** Prioritize lake activation (Phase 1: 3–4 anchor tenants by end of 2026)

## THREAT ASSESSMENT: WHO'S ACTUALLY A THREAT?

### McPherson (Local Threat: MODERATE)
- Same size, same industries, shared labor market
- Aggressive workforce development (mechanical engineering program 2026, childcare expansion)
- **Risk:** May attract El Dorado talent if not matched
- **Mitigation:** Match investments; emphasize El Dorado's FDI growth trajectory

### Salina (Sector Threat: HIGH for Aerospace)
- K-AIRES aerospace R&D facility (opening 2026) strengthens aerospace ecosystem
- 4x larger population; stronger K-State workforce pipeline
- **Risk:** May win aerospace supply companies over El Dorado
- **Mitigation:** Partner with Salina (logistics/supply hub role); emphasize I-35 access + lower costs

### Dodge City (Strategic Advantage: NOT a Threat to El Dorado)
- Hilmar Cheese ($600M, 270 jobs) proves mega-investments possible in rural Kansas
- BUT: Food/ag processing sector; NOT competitive with El Dorado's advanced manufacturing/data centers
- **Implication:** Validates FDI recruitment; different sectors = no direct competition

### Ponca City (No Threat: DECLINING)
- Energy sector contraction; losing jobs
- No FDI wins announced
- **Risk:** None; city is in defensive mode

### Emporia (Sector Threat: LOW)
- Animal health corridor dominance; NOT competitive with El Dorado
- **Risk:** Low; different sectors

## CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS (Do These or Lose)

### 1. HOUSING AFFORDABILITY CRISIS
**Current Status:** 3.46x median home price-to-income ratio (PROBLEM; healthy = 2.5–3.0x)  
**Root Cause:** Only 12 units/year new construction; AMAZONE demand creating supply squeeze  
**Risk:** Without housing, FDI employers can't recruit 200+ workers; company will move to competitor  
**Action Required:** Deliver 300+ units (2026–2028); ensure 150+ units are "workforce affordable" (<$1,100/month rent)  
**Timeline:** Phase 1 (100 units) open Q4 2026; Phase 2 (100 units) mid-2027; Phase 3 (100 units) 2028

### 2. WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT GAP
**Current Status:** Only 38% of young adults age 22–34 stay in El Dorado (Salina: 55%)  
**Root Cause:** Limited higher-ed options; fewer professional jobs; Wichita's gravity  
**Risk:** Talent drain to Wichita continues; can't fill FDI jobs with local workers  
**Action Required:** Match McPherson's programs (mechanical engineering, apprenticeships, childcare); add 40–50 certifications/year  
**Timeline:** Launch first cohort 2026; 50+ certifications/year by 2028

### 3. AMAZONE SEQUENCING
**Current Status:** AMAZONE expanded 2026; added 100+ jobs  
**Risk:** Don't over-rely on single employer; need pipeline of 2–3 additional major employers  
**Action Required:** Close 2nd FDI by 2027; 3rd by 2028  
**Timeline:** Have 5+ qualified FDI prospects in pipeline by Q4 2026

### 4. LAKE ACTIVATION EXECUTION
**Current Status:** #1 Kansas state park (1M+ visitors); only 26-venue plan; UNDERDEVELOPED  
**Risk:** Miss 3–5 year window; competitors develop tourism assets; El Dorado loses quality-of-life draw  
**Action Required:** Phase 1 (3–4 anchor tenants: waterfront restaurant, boutique hotel, water sports) open by end of 2026  
**Timeline:** Day 1 site selection Q3 2026; grand opening Q4 2026

### 5. DATA CENTER RECRUITMENT WINDOW
**Current Status:** BEAD broadband 2026–2027; broadband parity by 2028  
**Risk:** Miss 24-month window; competitive advantage erodes; never attract data center  
**Action Required:** Close feasibility study Q4 2026; secure LOI Q2 2027; start construction by 2028  
**Timeline:** Aggressive outreach to Meta, Google, Amazon, Anthropic, Microsoft 2026–2027
