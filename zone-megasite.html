1,131-Acre Advanced Manufacturing Megasite

Prime industrial location for major FDI: data centers, battery manufacturing, precision ag tech, aerospace supply.

Allowed Uses

  • Data Centers: 20–50 MW facilities with water cooling
  • Manufacturing: Battery production, thermal systems, precision parts, biotech
  • Assembly: Aerospace components, ag equipment, composite fabrication
  • Cold Chain: Logistics, distribution, warehousing
  • R&D: Tech parks, biotech labs, research facilities

Not Allowed

  • Heavy polluting industries (refining, chemical)
  • Hazardous waste
  • Outdoor storage/junkyards

Infrastructure & Incentives

Site Features

  • Flat, shovel-ready land (minimal prep)
  • Direct I-35 on-ramps (71, 76)
  • Rail access (BNSF Newton, 30 mi)
  • Water infrastructure (23M gal/day)
  • Power available (Westar)
  • FTZ #28 enrollment

Typical FDI Package

  • EDA grant: $1–2M
  • KDOT/KDHE: $500K–$1M
  • Tax abatement: 8–10 years, 50–70%
  • Kansas STAR Bonds: 6.5% TIF rebate
  • Workforce training: $200K–$500K
  • Water rate discount: $1.25–$1.50/1K gal

Development Strategy

The megasite is designed for 2–4 major anchor tenants (data centers or manufacturing facilities of 200–500+ jobs each). Infrastructure investments ($5–10M) are front-loaded to support multiple projects simultaneously.

  • Year 1: Infrastructure design, utility upgrades, road improvements
  • Year 2: First facility groundbreaking (500+ jobs)
  • Year 3: Second facility begins (300+ jobs)
  • Years 4–5: Expansion of existing facilities or third project
