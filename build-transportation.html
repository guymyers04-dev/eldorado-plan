Infrastructure Roads & Corridors

El Dorado Road Network

Vision 2040 prioritizes strategic road improvements that support economic development, improve safety, and enhance connectivity between districts and regional centers.

US-77 Business Loop Enhancement

Widen and improve US-77 corridor through El Dorado (business loop). Add turn lanes, synchronize traffic signals, and improve signage for industrial traffic and regional logistics.

Est. cost: $12–15M · Timeline: 2026–2028

Central Avenue Corridor (Downtown)

Resurface Central Ave with enhanced pedestrian crossings, on-street parking, and street trees. Support downtown revitalization with better traffic flow and walkability.

Est. cost: $4–6M · Timeline: 2025–2026

North Gateway Access Roads

Build interior roads connecting North Gateway District to downtown, El Dorado Lake, and US-77. Enable walkable connectivity and support mixed-use development.

Est. cost: $8–10M · Timeline: 2025–2030

Lake Access Road Improvements

Improve roads leading to El Dorado Lake (scenic drives). Upgrade to lake access points, parking areas, and recreation facilities for visitor traffic.

Est. cost: $3–5M · Timeline: 2026–2029
Infrastructure Active Transportation

Trails, Biking & Pedestrian Network

Vision 2040 envisions a connected trail system linking downtown, El Dorado Lake, neighborhoods, and regional trail networks. 28 miles of paved bike/ped trails by 2040.

Trail System Components

Downtown Loop (3.2 miles)

Paved trail connecting downtown, public library, high school, and neighborhoods. Target completion: 2025.

Lakeshore Trail (6 miles)

Circumnavigation trail around El Dorado Lake with viewing platforms, rest areas, and wildlife habitat. Target completion: 2027.

Walnut River Greenway (8 miles)

Along Walnut River from city limits north to Lake, connecting neighborhoods to regional trail network. Target completion: 2030.

Regional Connectors (10+ miles)

Links to county trails, scenic loops, and neighboring communities. Ongoing expansion through 2040.

Infrastructure Features

  • Paved Surfaces
    10–12 ft wide asphalt for bikes and pedestrians
  • Shade Structures
    Pavilions, arbors, and shade trees every 0.5 mi
  • Water Fountains & Restrooms
    Water fountains and portable restrooms on longer trails
  • Wayfinding & Safety
    Clear signage, mile markers, and LED lighting for evening use
Expected Impact:

Daily users: 200 (year 1) → 1,000+ (year 5). Tourism boost from regional trail seekers. Health benefits from active recreation. Property values increase near trails.

Transit & Regional Links

🚌 Public Transit (Demand Response)

Expand El Dorado transit service (Apus Trans) with increased routes to Wichita, daily service for commuters and students, and connections to regional bus lines.

Timeline: 2026–2028

🚴 Bike Sharing Program

Launch bike-share network with 50–100 bikes at downtown, lake, and North Gateway hubs. Support casual users and tourists exploring the city.

Timeline: 2027–2028

✈️ Airport Infrastructure

Upgrade El Dorado Regional Airport (EQA) with ILS approach, expanded hangars, and FBO services. Support regional business aviation and emergency services.

Timeline: 2026–2030

🌐 Regional Highway Advocacy

Support Kansas DOT improvements to US-77 and K-254. Advocate for corridor as strategic freight route and tourism destination alternative to I-35.

Ongoing advocacy
