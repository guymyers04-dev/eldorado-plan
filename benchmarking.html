Competitive Benchmarking Analysis

El Dorado vs. five regional competitors: Salina, McPherson, Emporia, Dodge City, Ponca City

Executive Summary

Despite being the smallest population (12,500 vs. 13K–47K), El Dorado has the strongest unique structural advantages. El Dorado's water rights (23M gal/day), I-35 direct access, FDI proof-of-concept (AMAZONE), and state park asset position it as the leading candidate for advanced manufacturing, data centers, and aerospace supply in the region.

El Dorado's Top Advantages
  • 23M gal/day owned water rights (ONLY city in region)
  • I-35 direct access (Exits 71/76)
  • AMAZONE FDI proof-of-concept (operational 2025)
  • #1 Kansas State Park (1M+ annual visitors)
  • FTZ #28 enrollment (active)
Competitive Challenges
  • Smallest labor force (5,200 vs. 5K–19K)
  • GDP per capita gap ($47.5K vs. $56–69K)
  • Home price/income ratio elevated (3.46x)
  • Broadband parity by 2027 (BEAD funding)
  • Youth retention 38% (vs. 55% Salina)

Strategic Implications

  • Labor Force Growth: El Dorado's tight labor pool requires housing + workforce development to support 1,000+ jobs from future FDI
  • Youth Retention Crisis: Only 38% of young adults stay; root causes = limited higher ed (Butler CC only), fewer professional jobs, Wichita gravity. Mitigation: accelerate lake tourism + downtown activation
  • Skills Gap: El Dorado (8.2%) is below optimal for advanced manufacturing; AMAZONE expansion will raise this to 12–15% by 2028
