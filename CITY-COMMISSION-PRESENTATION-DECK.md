## SLIDE 18: Q&A / DISCUSSION

**Key Talking Points for Questions**

**"Why $510K? Is that too much?"**
> Expected ROI is $500M–$2B over 5 years. That's 100:1–400:1 return. For context, every grant dollar typically leverages $3–$5 in private investment. This is standard economic development investment.

**"How do we know data center recruitment will work?"**
> We have three advantages: (1) unique water rights (23M gal/day), (2) Kansas SB 98 tax exemption, (3) BEAD broadband. Hyperscale operators are actively seeking Midwest sites. Our broker will connect us to qualified prospects. If data center doesn't close, we still have 2–3 FDI facilities as alternative.

**"What if we can't hire good staff?"**
> We're recruiting nationally; these are highly sought positions. $120K for Data Center Director is competitive for rural Kansas. Roles report to EDA (not city), so we can recruit experienced development professionals.

**"How does this benefit residents?"**
> 1,600 new jobs + 5,100 new residents by 2040. That's a thriving, growing city. Downtown revitalized, lake activated, schools growing, workforce training programs. El Dorado becomes a destination, not a place people leave.

**"What's our competitive advantage vs. Salina or Wichita?"**
> Water rights (unique in Kansas). I-35 direct access (Salina is off-corridor). AMAZONE proof-of-concept (recent FDI win). Lake tourism asset (zero competitors have this). We're racing against time, not just geography.

**"What if this doesn't work?"**
> Worst case: We spent $510K, hired good people who generate ideas and opportunities, built relationships with data center operators and international investors. That's not wasted—that's foundation-building. Even partial success (1–2 FDI facilities instead of 3, or lake activation without data center) still achieves significant growth.
