## QUICK REFERENCE: KEY FINDINGS

### The Headline
**El Dorado faces a critical 4-year opportunity window (2026-2030).** Converging regional tailwinds — manufacturing reshoring, housing shortage, broadband deployment, Wichita MSA spillover — create conditions for 3.2% annual population growth (optimistic scenario) vs. 1.8% baseline. Execution on water infrastructure, broadband-ready industrial parks, and housing acceleration in H2 2026-H1 2027 determines outcome.

### Five Big Tailwinds

1. **Manufacturing Reshoring ($1.7T national; Panasonic precedent in Kansas)**  
   → El Dorado's industrial water capacity = competitive moat for water-intensive industries

2. **Historic Housing Shortage (4,000 homes/year needed; 55K unit deficit)**  
   → El Dorado $50K cheaper than metro average = powerful millennial magnet

3. **Broadband Deployment ($166.6M BEAD; construction H2 2026)**  
   → Remote work unlocked = 150-250 household recruitment opportunity

4. **Wichita MSA Expansion (663K→750K by 2040; 17.1% growth)**  
   → El Dorado at optimal 30-min commute distance = satellite growth capture

5. **Skills Gap + Education Partnership (Butler CC #2 in Kansas; workforce training)**  
   → Ag-tech, building trades, healthcare training hub positioning

### Three Critical Decisions (H2 2026)

1. **Water Infrastructure:** Accelerate B.A.S.E. grant deployment; target 2-3 water-using industrial recruits by 2030
2. **Housing Development:** Launch 50-75 unit development program; partner Habitat/land trusts for affordability
3. **Industrial Park:** Permit/plan broadband-ready acreage; target 100-150 acres ready by 2028

### Financial Impact

**Conservative Case (1.8% growth):**  
- Population +1,400 by 2030  
- Tax base +12.5% (+$3.6M cumulative revenue capacity)

**Optimistic Case (3.2% growth, full VIS 2040 execution):**  
- Population +3,000 by 2030  
- Tax base +45.8% (+$13.2M cumulative revenue capacity)  
- Commercial investment $180-250M

## LIMITATIONS & CAVEATS

1. **2030 Projections Are Scenarios, Not Predictions**
   - Conservative case assumes baseline Vision 2040 execution
   - Optimistic case assumes aggressive, coordinated implementation
   - Actual outcome will depend on external factors & city decisions

2. **Macro Uncertainty (2028-2030)**
   - Federal policy changes (tariffs, broadband funding, CHIPS Act)
   - Tech sector cycle (data center investment may pause 2027-2028)
   - Recession risk (not factored into baseline scenarios)
   - Climate impacts (drought, flooding) could affect agriculture/water supply

3. **Competitive Dynamics**
   - Other Kansas communities may execute more aggressively
   - Wichita metro core may reinvest to retain suburban growth
   - Adjacent counties could capture portion of spillover growth

4. **Vision 2040 Assumption**
   - Optimistic scenario assumes full execution of Vision 2040 priorities
   - If implementation delays occur, outcomes trend toward conservative case
   - City has limited control over regional economic factors (Panasonic, tariffs, etc.)
