Initiative Overview

What Is It?

Develop a regional food processing and specialty foods manufacturing cluster in the industrial mega-site. Target: 1-2 anchor facilities (150K-300K sf) plus supporting processors and value-added food businesses. Leverage regional agricultural supply chain.

Why It Matters

Food processing creates high-wage jobs (avg. $45K-52K + benefits), utilizes local agricultural inputs, and builds competitive regional advantage. El Dorado's location on US-77 and proximity to ranch/grain producers make it ideal for specialty meat processing, grain milling, and value-added food manufacturing.

Facility Opportunities

Anchor Facility #1: Specialty Meat Processing

  • Scale: 150K-200K sf
  • Focus: Beef, pork, specialty products
  • Investment: $20-30M
  • Employment: 250-350 direct jobs
  • Avg. Wage: $45K-52K + benefits
  • Annual Payroll: $12-15M

Anchor Facility #2: Grain Milling & Value-Added

  • Scale: 100K-150K sf
  • Focus: Flour milling, specialty grains, organic products
  • Investment: $15-25M
  • Employment: 100-150 direct jobs
  • Avg. Wage: $42K-48K + benefits
  • Annual Payroll: $5-7M

Supply Chain Advantage

El Dorado's location provides strategic advantages for food processing:

Regional Supply

  • Butler County ranchers (beef/pork)
  • Surrounding wheat + grain producers
  • Specialty crop farmers (organic)
  • Average transport cost: $2-3/unit (vs. $8-10 from Wichita)

Distribution Advantages

  • Direct access to US-77 / I-35
  • Rail access (BNSF) for bulk shipment
  • 4-hour radius to 50+ food distributors
  • Lower transportation costs = higher margins

Recruitment Strategy

Target Companies

  • Specialty Meat Processors: Companies with 50-300 employee operations seeking expansion or relocation
  • Organic/Natural Foods: Growing segment seeking regional production capacity
  • Value-Added Food Manufacturers: Prepared meals, specialty condiments, artisan products
  • Contract Packers: Regional co-packers serving natural/organic brands

Incentive Package

Land Costs: $2-3K/acre (vs. Wichita $6-8K)
Tax Abatement: 10-year phase-out (25% → 0%)
Training Grants: $200K-500K per facility
Infrastructure Credits: 50% utility connection costs
