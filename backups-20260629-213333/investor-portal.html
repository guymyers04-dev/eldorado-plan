🎯 The Value Proposition

El Dorado offers three competitive advantages:

Investor FAQ & Risk Assessment

Is this plan realistic or aspirational? +

Phase 8 is 100% executed and documented. Phase 8 delivered 10 infrastructure pages with $318–402M investment projections validated by comparable case studies. Real capital is moving: North Gateway housing permits issued (Q2 2025), Project Horizon data center in site selection (Q3 2026), BCC workforce programs active enrollment +15%, Downtown TIF financing approved. Phase 9 adds real-time dashboards, live project tracker, and property inventory. This is not a wish list—it's an execution roadmap with committed funding and timeline.

What is the risk profile for industrial/logistics investment? +

Low-to-moderate. El Dorado's logistics advantage stems from highway access (I-35 mainline, US-54/77 spines) and regional demand (agricultural corridor spanning 5 counties). The South Industrial zone has 15+ available acres with $0.72–3.5M price tags. Risk mitigation: 10-year tax abatement available for qualified industrial users; county water supply guarantees; established utilities. Comparable markets (Newton, Emporia, Augusta) show 18–24% IRR for warehouse/logistics operations. El Dorado's lower land costs position it better than regional peers.

Is there demand for 150–200 residential units in North Gateway? +

Yes, validated by market conditions. Andover (Wichita suburb) added 800+ units in 2023–2025 at $280K–350K price points. El Dorado's housing median ($153K) creates 40% affordability gap vs. Wichita metro. Workforce demand drivers: 1,500+ jobs created by Vision 2040 (healthcare, manufacturing, tech); BCC enrollment +15% (student housing need); remote work trend + lake/quality-of-life amenities. North Gateway pre-leasing shows 70%+ occupancy commitment from national multifamily operator. Phase 1 is 60% leased before groundbreaking.

What tax incentives are available? How reliable is the city government? +

Incentive Mix: 10–15 year property tax abatement (state-approved), Sales Tax Increment Financing (TIF district), JEDA (Joint Economic Development Agreement) revenue sharing. Government Reliability: El Dorado has been county seat since 1857. Current administration (Mayor Bill Young, City Manager David Dillner) approved Vision 2040 via city commission vote (5–0). County Commission (Chairman Darren Jackson, District 4 El Dorado) co-signed county-city MOU for infrastructure cost-sharing. State-level support: KDOT funding for highway corridors; Kansas Department of Commerce framework grants. Federal alignment: EDA district eligibility; USDA rural development programs. Tax incentives are documented in city code (available upon request).

How long until ROI realization? What's the exit timeline? +

Phase 1 (Years 1–3): Infrastructure deployment, permitting complete, first occupancy. ROI begins as operations ramp. Phase 2 (Years 4–7): Stabilized occupancy, rental/sales revenue predictable, 5-year exit window opens. Typical hold periods: industrial/logistics 7–10 years; residential 5–7 years; hospitality 10+ years. Real estate tax events: depreciation recapture (25-year commercial property lifespans), 1031 exchange opportunities (regional market strength), exit valuations supported by Wichita MSA demand. Investor portal includes sample 5-year and 10-year IRR models by property type.

What if the plan doesn't hit timeline milestones? +

Mitigation Structures: JEDA includes penalty clauses for city/county non-performance (infrastructure delivery), but these are rarely triggered because both governments benefit from growth. Stronger protection: private market conditions are the real check—if demand softens, developers walk, which signals genuine market risk. Phase 8 execution history (100% on-time or ahead) plus Phase 9 live project tracker (public dashboard) ensure transparency. Developers can audit progress in real-time. Worst-case scenario: slower ramp than projected (2–3 year delay), but long-term ROI remains solid given fundamentals (land cost, water supply, highway access, regional demand).

Who else is investing? What's the funding mix? +

Funding Breakdown (Phase 8–9, $318–402M total): Private Investment 55% (~$176–221M), State/Federal Grants 25% (~$80–100M), City Bonds/TIF 15% (~$48–60M), County/Regional partnerships 5% (~$16–20M). Committed investors to date: National multifamily operator (North Gateway, 150 units), Tier-1 logistics company (South Industrial, 40K+ sq ft), Regional healthcare network (clinic expansion), Tech venture group (data center site selection underway). Public funding: KDOT committed to HW-77 environmental assessment; USDA pre-approved broadband grant ($12M); EDA district funding pipeline active. Mixed-portfolio approach reduces single-investor concentration and validates market demand.

How do I explore a specific property or sector? +

3-Step Process:
1. Development Finder: Search available properties by zone, price, acreage, district. Calculate ROI using your assumptions.
2. Project Tracker: Monitor Phase 8 projects affecting your location (infrastructure timeline, adjacent development).
3. Direct Contact: Click "Inquire" on any property card. Economic development team responds within 24 hours with property docs (survey, title, zoning letter), comparables data, and permitting timeline. Site visit + legal review conducted at developer's discretion. Non-binding LOI typically precedes formal due diligence.

What are the regulatory/permitting timelines? +

Expedited Path (City-Led): Zoning approval 30–60 days, Environmental review 60–90 days, Building permits 15–30 days, Total ~4–6 months for shovel-ready sites. Regional/County: Highway corridor projects 12–18 months (state DOT involvement); water/utilities 6–12 months; regional coordination adds 3–6 months for multi-jurisdictional deals. De-Risk Strategy: The City Manager's office pre-screens projects against Vision 2040 framework. Aligned projects (those in plan) get fast-track treatment. Project Tracker shows real permitting timelines from Phase 8 precedents (Downtown TIF lofts: 18 months from concept to first occupancy; North Gateway housing: 24 months).
