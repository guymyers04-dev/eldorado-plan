Participatory Budgeting Program

Finance
Quick Win
< $50K
< 6 months

Initiative Overview

What Is It?

Allocate $250K of city discretionary funds annually for residents to directly vote on spending priorities. Residents submit project ideas (street improvements, parks, community programs), city staff vets feasibility, residents vote online & in-person on final projects. First-of-its-kind in Kansas to democratize public spending.

Why El Dorado?

Participatory budgeting increases civic engagement (typically 15–25% voter participation), rebuilds trust in government, and ensures public dollars reflect community values. El Dorado can become a model for small-town democracy in rural Kansas.

Implementation Plan

1
Design & Community Building
Establish Participatory Budgeting Steering Committee (city council + residents). Define budget pool ($250K), project eligibility criteria, voting rules. Launch awareness campaign. Create online nomination platform (Mindmixer or custom portal). Months: 1–2.
2
Ideation & Vetting
Residents submit project proposals (via online + paper forms). City staff + committee vet for feasibility, cost, impact. Shortlist 20–30 projects. Hold community meetings to explain finalists. Months: 2–3.
3
Voting & Implementation
Conduct 2-week online + in-person voting. Announce winners. City commits to implementing projects within 12 months. Annual cycle repeats. Track outcomes & resident satisfaction.
