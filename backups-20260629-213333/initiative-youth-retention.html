Initiative Overview

What Is It?

Create a "Future Makers" fellowship program pairing high school seniors (ages 16–18) with mentors in tech, entrepreneurship, and creative fields. Participants get $500/month stipends, attend 3-month cohorts, and gain job-ready skills. Goal: retain 75% of participants post-graduation.

Why El Dorado?

40% of El Dorado high school graduates leave town after graduation. Youth lack local role models and career pathways. A structured mentorship program with stipends + job placement creates reasons to stay and launch careers locally.

Implementation Plan

1
Mentor Network & Curriculum Design
Recruit 8–10 local mentors (tech entrepreneurs, creatives, business leaders). Develop 3-month curriculum covering startup basics, personal branding, freelance opportunities. Create mentorship matching system. Partner with El Dorado High School. Months: 1–2.
2
Launch Cohort 1
Recruit 15 high school seniors. Distribute stipends ($500/month × 3 = $1,500 per participant). Hold weekly 2-hour mentorship sessions + group events. Months: 2–4.
3
Job Placement & Alumni Network
Connect graduates to local job opportunities, apprenticeships, or startup incubators. Track post-graduation outcomes (employment, education, retention). Launch alumni network for ongoing support. Scale to 2 cohorts/year.

Budget Breakdown
