Risk Assessment & Mitigation

Economic Recession

Mitigation: Healthcare & education jobs are recession-resistant. Maintain 20–25% municipal reserves. Prioritize CDBG/HUD funding (countercyclical programs). Delay mega-site recruitment in downturns; focus on internal capacity building. Federal grants actually increase during recessions.

Grant Funding Uncertainty

Mitigation: Apply for EDA, USDA, DOT, HUD, EPA grants across portfolio (don't rely on single source). Municipal bond authorization provides backup ($150M available). State tax credits (LIHTC, HTC) less volatile than federal grants. Diversify funding sources across 5+ programs.

Business Recruitment Delays

Mitigation: Build mega-site infrastructure now (water, sewer, power, roads) to reduce risk. FDI recruitment typically 12–24 months from contact to LOI. Start now; expect results in 2027–2029. AMAZONE success validates strategy; replicate model with JETRO, GTAI, KOTRA.

Population Stagnation

Mitigation: Housing affordability is key. LIHTC, RHID, owner-occupied rehab make housing accessible to working families. Remote worker attraction via broadband (BEAD). Workforce training pipeline (Butler CC apprenticeships). Young family incentives (childcare, schools, parks).

Community Opposition

Mitigation: (See Community Engagement Strategy page.) Transparent communication, early engagement, visible quick wins, addressing specific concerns, local hiring preferences, community benefit agreements. Most opposition is fear of change; education + trust-building resolves this.

Data Center Decision Impact

Mitigation: Data center project ($500M+) would accelerate entire Vision 2040 timeline by 3–5 years. Current status: zoning ordinance approved (June 2026), no project proposed. Mayor Bill Young: "no project proposed." City has franchise fee revenue ($2.5M+/yr) if project proceeds. Keep open; don't depend on it. Water Authority de-risks other water users.
