Southeast El Dorado & Prospect Viability

Long-Term Growth & Strategic Choices

Strategic analysis of southeast expansion: Annex and develop, allow county development, or create satellite node? Feasibility and 7–10 year roadmap.

The Question: Expand South or Stay Consolidated?

Geographic Context

Lands southeast of El Dorado city boundary (Prospect vicinity) present a strategic decision point:

  • Current status: Unincorporated Butler County land
  • Development pressure: Moderate (future potential if infrastructure improves)
  • Competing interests: City growth aspirations vs. county agricultural preservation
  • Timeline: Not urgent now, but decision required within 7–10 years to avoid ad-hoc development

📊 Area Overview

  • Geographic scope: ~500–800 acres southeast of city limits
  • Current land use: Agricultural (pasture, crops)
  • Prospect location: ~3–5 miles southeast of downtown
  • Development potential: Industrial park, residential cluster, commercial node

Three Strategic Options

  • Option A: Annex & Develop — Extend city limits; plan mixed-use satellite node
  • Option B: County Development — Allow county to manage growth; City maintains compact footprint
  • Option C: Hybrid Model — Selective annexation for key projects; county manages remainder

Feasibility Analysis: Costs, Benefits, Risks

Option A: Annex & Develop

Scenario: City annexes 500–800 acres; develops mixed-use satellite node (residential, commercial, industrial)

  • Benefits: Tax base expansion, growth control, planned development, mixed-use integration
  • Costs: $15–25M infrastructure (utilities, roads); annexation negotiations; governance complexity
  • Timeline: 10–15 years for full development
  • Verdict: High investment; viable only if growth demand strong

Option B: County Development

Scenario: County allows rural/low-density development; City remains compact

  • Benefits: Lower City costs; agricultural preservation; no annexation complexity
  • Risks: Unplanned development; loss of tax base; infrastructure fragmentation; sprawl
  • Verdict: Low City investment, but cedes growth opportunity and control

Option C: Hybrid (Strategic Annexation)

Scenario: Annex key 100–150 acre parcels for anchor projects (industrial park, office park); county manages remainder

  • Benefits: Controlled growth; modest City investment; mixed-use nodes at strategic locations
  • Costs: $5–10M infrastructure (targeted investment)
  • Verdict: Balanced approach; recommended for most scenarios

💡 Recommendation: Hybrid Model with 10-Year Window

Decision point: Year 7–10 — As north/east/south development matures, assess demand for southeast expansion. If growth demand warrants, pursue selective annexation of key parcels (industrial park, commercial). If not, allow county to manage low-density rural development.

15-Year Scenarios: What Development Could Look Like

If Annexed (Option A/C): Phased Development

  • Years 1–7: City focuses on north, east, downtown growth (primary areas)
  • Years 8–10: Assess demand; if positive, annex 100–150 key acres near Prospect
  • Years 10–15: Develop satellite node: small industrial park (50–80 acres), office/retail cluster (20–30 acres), residential (40–60 acres)
  • Expected outcomes: 200–300 new jobs, $15–20M tax base growth, satellite community feel

If County-Led (Option B): Low-Density Rural

  • Years 1–15: County allows rural residential clustering; limited commercial/industrial
  • Pattern: 10–20 acre lots, rural home subdivisions, agricultural focus
  • Expected outcomes: 50–100 new rural residents, minimal tax base growth, limited City control

Decision Framework: When to Decide

Key Decision Triggers

  • Growth demand: If north/east development shows strong demand (pace faster than projected), southeast development becomes viable
  • Infrastructure readiness: If utilities can extend to southeast economically, annexation becomes feasible
  • Market interest: If industrial/commercial developers express interest in Prospect area, City should have annexation plan ready
  • County coordination: If county signals interest in rural preservation, City may prefer hybrid model

🎯 Recommended Action (Years 1–3)

  1. City Commission adopts "Southeast Growth Option" analysis document
  2. Establish ongoing dialogue with Butler County on land use expectations
  3. Monitor development demand in north/east areas; track growth velocity
  4. Year 7–10: Reassess conditions; decide between Options A/B/C
