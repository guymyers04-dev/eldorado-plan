Indirect Economic Impact (Tax Revenue & Spending Multiplier)

USD 490 employees spend 70–80% of take-home pay locally (housing, food, retail, services). This creates a multiplier effect:

Impact Type Amount Notes
Employee Spending (Local) $13–17M/yr 70–80% of $18–22M payroll spent locally
Property Tax Revenue (from payroll) $2–3M/yr Local property taxes on USD 490 employees' homes
Sales Tax Revenue (indirect) $800K–$1.2M/yr Sales tax on employee local spending
Total Tax Revenue Generated $2.8–4.2M/yr Local property + sales tax

Multiplier Ratio: For every $1 of USD 490 payroll, El Dorado generates $0.15–$0.20 in tax revenue

Induced Economic Impact (Supplier & Retail Multiplier)

When USD 490 spends $15–18M on operations, supplies, and contractors, those vendors hire locally and spend locally, creating secondary income:

  • Induced Income (10–15% of direct spending): $1.5–$2.7M annually
  • Support Jobs Created: 30–50 FTE (custodians, contractors, retail workers spending USD 490 supply dollars)

Total Annual Economic Impact: $35–45M (direct + indirect + induced)

USD 490's Role in Vision 2040

1. Workforce Development Pipeline

USD 490 → Butler CC → FDI employers. K-12 education quality directly impacts workforce readiness for precision ag, battery tech, aerospace supply, and biotech jobs. Improving USD 490's math proficiency (currently 24% vs. 31% state avg) means more students prepared for STEM careers.

2. Youth Retention Engine

Young families stay in El Dorado if schools are good. Investment in USD 490 → improved schools → better youth retention (currently 38%) → more talent available for FDI jobs.

3. Workforce Housing Demand

Families choose where to live partly based on school quality. North El Dorado housing development will attract younger families if USD 490 schools improve. Housing + good schools = dual magnet for families.

4. Community Stability & Cohesion

Thriving schools = vibrant community = attraction for relocated employees from major companies. Executives relocating to El Dorado care deeply about school quality.

USD 490 Improvement Roadmap (Tied to Vision 2040)

Years 1–2: Foundation

  • Math/reading proficiency programs (+10% improvement target)
  • Butler CC dual enrollment expansion
  • Career academy partnerships (precision ag, manufacturing)
  • Investment: $2–3M (grant funding + school budget)

Years 3–5: Acceleration

  • Graduation rate target: 85%+ (from current 79%)
  • STEM curriculum redesign (tied to FDI needs)
  • College/career readiness programs
  • Investment: $5–8M cumulative

Key Message: USD 490 Is Not a Cost; It's an Investment

City/County mindset shift: Don't think of USD 490 budget as a "spending line item." Think of it as a $35–45M annual economic engine that:

  • Directly employs 250–300 people
  • Generates $2.8–4.2M in tax revenue annually
  • Creates $13–17M in local employee spending
  • Supports 30–50 additional jobs in support industries
  • Drives workforce readiness for FDI jobs (1,000+ positions)
  • Attracts young families and professionals

Every dollar invested in USD 490 quality improvement pays dividends in workforce readiness, youth retention, and economic competitiveness.
