## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

El Dorado Vision 2040 is a comprehensive 14-year economic transformation strategy that leverages the city's unique competitive advantages—located 50 minutes north of Wichita, positioned on I-35 with rail access, blessed with abundant freshwater, and home to the 1,131-acre mega-site—to create a diversified, resilient economy.

**Three Strategic Phases:**

1. **Phase 1 (2026–2030): Foundation Building** — Attract 1–2 hyperscale data centers, 2–3 FDI facilities (German/Japanese manufacturers), activate lake amenities (hotel, RV park, water sports), secure 200+ workforce housing units
2. **Phase 2 (2028–2034): Consolidation & Scaling** — Scale successful sectors, build supporting infrastructure, achieve 15,000 population, establish international hub status
3. **Phase 3 (2034–2040): Maturation** — Diversified economy with 20,000+ population, $500M+ annual economic activity, thriving downtown, world-class quality of life

**The Strategic Imperative: Why Now?**

El Dorado faces a **24–36 month window** (July 2026 – December 2028) to establish itself in the hyperscale data center market before the wave of AI infrastructure investment peaks and larger metros consolidate supply chain dominance. Simultaneously, the city has unprecedented momentum: AMAZONE closed Dec 2025 (proof of FDI recruitment capability), water rights secured, SB 98 tax incentives approved, BEAD broadband infrastructure funded, and $100M+ in federal grants identified.

**Seven Research Domains Unified:**

This strategy integrates findings from:
1. **Competitive Benchmarking** (18 comparable metros)
2. **Industry Growth Projections** (data centers, FDI, hospitality, housing, lake activation)
3. **Real Estate & Infrastructure Atlas** (1,131-acre site, downtown potential, 300-acre lake)
4. **Regional Economic Trends** (Wichita metro growth, Kansas incentives, supply chain dynamics)
5. **Risk Management** (32 material risks, 4 critical path items, 6 contingency playbooks)
6. **Partnership Strategy** (140+ organizations across 12 categories)
7. **Funding Strategy** (100+ programs, $500M+ capital stack)
