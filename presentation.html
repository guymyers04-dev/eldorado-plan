El Dorado, Kansas

Vision 2040 — Executive Presentation

A comprehensive 15-year blueprint for regional leadership, economic growth, and 21st-century opportunity. Investment roadmap: $900M+ | Jobs: 1,600+ | Growth: +41%

Presented June 2026 | Butler County, Kansas

Why Now? The Convergence of Opportunity

  • I-35 Interstate Node — Direct exits at 71/76. Only city in region with turnpike access. Undermarketed advantage.
  • Water Abundance — Unique city-owned 23M gal/day water rights. 8.5B additional capacity for industry. Only Kansas city with USACE contract.
  • Regional Hub — 29 mi from Wichita MSA (650K+). Gateway to Flint Hills, agricultural heartland. Multiple community colleges within 50 mi.
  • Proven FDI Win — AMAZONE (German ag machinery) opened Dec. 2025. Validates mega-site and international recruitment strategy.
  • Federal & State Support — SS4A safety grant, BEAD broadband, SB 98 data center exemption, KDOT transportation funding alignment.
  • Anchor Assets — HF Sinclair (135K bbl/day refinery), Butler CC (#2 in state), SBA Hospital (74-bed). Foundation for health/education economies.
These conditions are rare. They won't persist forever. The window is now.

The 1,131-Acre Mega-Site

Certified Site — Ready for Enterprise

  • Rail: BNSF main line (46 trains/day), UP industrial lead, Savage transload terminal (built 2011)
  • Highway: I-35 Exits 71/76, K-254 (K-State designated ag corridor)
  • Water: 10 MGD dedicated industrial supply, 8.5B gal/yr additional capacity
  • Power: Evergy, three-phase power, 13.80¢/kWh (below national avg)
  • Labor: Butler CC workforce pipeline, rural workforce (low cost)
  • FTZ: FTZ #28 active — customs duty savings on imports

2026 Win: AMAZONE (Germany)

Opened December 2, 2025
German agricultural machinery company. 6.5 acres, 30,000 sq ft, 30+ North American employees. Site cited: "Heart of most important agricultural region in US" + I-35 access.

Proof of Concept: Mega-site positioning works. FDI recruitment strategy validated. Replicate model for Japan, Germany, South Korea targets.

Est. By 2040: 500–800 jobs created on mega-site. $50M+ in private investment.

Transportation Safety & Connectivity

Two federal studies guide $80M+ in transportation improvements through 2040.

SS4A PROGRAM (USDOT)

Safe Streets for All — 8 Priority Projects

Funded Grant: $10M+ for safety improvements in high-crash corridors. Near-term (2026–2028).

  • Central Avenue (K-254): Boyer Rd to Haverhill Rd. Lane narrowing, pedestrian crossings, signal upgrades. $851K–$1.7M per segment.
  • Main Street (US-77): Locust Ave to McCollum Rd. Downtown gateway corridor, nighttime impaired driving crashes, VRU safety.
  • Intersections: N Main & 6th (signal), Main & McCollum (roundabout, $1.7M), 6th & Haverhill, Haverhill & Towanda.

Risk Mitigation & Adaptive Strategy

Economic Recession

Mitigation: Diversify employment (healthcare/education recession-proof), maintain 20–25% municipal reserves, prioritize CDBG/HUD funding (countercyclical).

Grant Funding Uncertainty

Mitigation: Municipal bond authorization for backup, federal funding diversification (EDA, USDA, DOT, HUD, EPA), state incentive layering (STAR Bonds, tax credits).

Business Relocation Risk

Mitigation: HF Sinclair incentive packages, mega-site competitive positioning (AMAZONE success), business retention program (small business focus).

Population Stagnation

Mitigation: Housing affordability (LIHTC, owner-occupied rehab), remote worker attraction (broadband, co-working), workforce training pipeline (Butler CC).
