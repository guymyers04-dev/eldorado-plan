Community Engagement Strategy

Messaging frameworks, objection responses, communication tactics, and stakeholder persuasion strategies to build broad community support for Vision 2040.

Core Messaging Framework

Use these core messages consistently across all communications. Tailor tone/depth by audience, but keep message consistent.

🎯 THE ELEVATOR PITCH (30 seconds)

"El Dorado is positioned for significant growth. We have a unique combination of assets — I-35 access, abundant water, a thriving state park, and anchor institutions like Butler Community College. Vision 2040 is a roadmap to leverage these assets into 1,600+ jobs, 5,000+ new residents, and $900 million in investment. It's not pie-in-the-sky — AMAZONE, a German ag-machinery company, chose El Dorado and opened December 2025. This is achievable, and we're ready."

📊 THE FACTS (2-3 minutes)

Current Reality: 12,500 residents, $59K median income (gap: $22K below county average). Population stable but not growing. Lake = #1 state park but zero dining. Downtown has potential but aging buildings.

The Opportunity: I-35 direct access, 23M gal/day unique water rights, Wichita MSA satellite position, HF Sinclair refinery anchor, Butler CC workforce pipeline. These are rare, durable assets.

The Vision: By 2040: 17,600 residents (+41%), $75K+ median income, thriving downtown, 26 lake venues (dining, water sports, lodging), health economy jobs (surgical center, behavioral health), regional water authority. Funded by federal grants ($200M+), state programs ($100M+), private investment ($600M+).

Year 1 Wins: Regional Water Authority formed, SS4A safety projects start, BEAD broadband designed, grant applications submitted. Visible progress by end of 2026.

❤️ THE HEART MESSAGE (Emotional Connect)

"This is about creating opportunity for our kids and grandkids. Opportunity to stay in El Dorado without moving to Wichita. Opportunity to have good-paying jobs in healthcare, education, manufacturing. Opportunity to keep our community vibrant, not slowly decline. Vision 2040 isn't about changing who we are — it's about building on what we already are: hardworking people in a good place. We're just giving ourselves permission to grow."

🚀 THE PROOF POINT

"AMAZONE opened December 2, 2025. A German agricultural machinery company chose El Dorado over other US locations. They said: 'Heart of the most important agricultural region in the US' and 'I-35 access.' That's our story. Vision 2040 makes it easier to attract more companies like AMAZONE."

Audience-Specific Talking Points

📚 Talking to Educators (USD 490, USD 375, Butler CC)

Lead: "Vision 2040 makes education the economic engine."

• Butler CC expansion: more students, more programs, higher enrollment = more state funding

• K-12 enrollment growth: 1-3% annual population growth means more students, justifies new facilities

• Workforce pipeline: manufacturing, healthcare, tech jobs need trained workers — apprenticeships, training programs fund directly from businesses

• Teacher recruitment: growth = ability to hire, competitive salaries, new buildings

🏥 Talking to Healthcare Leaders (SBA Hospital)

Lead: "Vision 2040 solves your biggest challenge: access."

• Market gap: 1 hospital per 70,000+ residents (severe shortage). SBA can expand.

• Population growth: +41% = +5,100 residents = larger patient base, supports new services

• Surgical center, behavioral health, telehealth, senior care: $80M+ projects employ 425+ healthcare workers

• NHSC physician recruitment: rural health loan forgiveness program for providers who commit

• Supply chain: regional hospitals increasingly outsource services to competent rural centers

💼 Talking to Business Community (El Dorado Inc., Chamber)

Lead: "Vision 2040 = Revenue growth for existing businesses + New market opportunities."

• Workforce expansion: 1,600+ jobs = more customers with disposable income

• Mega-site: 500–800 jobs on mega-site attract ancillary services (food, retail, logistics)

• Lake activation: 26 venues = dining, retail, hospitality opportunities

• Real estate: residential boom creates development opportunities, property value appreciation

• Tax base growth: +150% = can fund infrastructure, reduce business tax burden long-term

🏠 Talking to Residents (Neighborhood Associations)

Lead: "Vision 2040 makes El Dorado a better place to live, not just bigger."

• Safety improvements: $10M SS4A grant = new street lighting, pedestrian crossings, traffic calming

• Lake activation: 26 new venues = more family activities, special events, recreational opportunities

• Downtown vitality: arts district, performing arts center, historic district restoration

• Schools: enrollment growth supports new buildings, updated facilities, teacher hiring

• Property values: cities with clear growth plans see 3-5% annual property appreciation

💰 Talking to Investors & Lenders (Banks, Pension Funds)

Lead: "Vision 2040 de-risks investment. Clear roadmap, federal backing, diversified revenue."

• Federal grant co-signing: EDA, USDA, HUD grants indicate federal confidence

• Mega-site proof: AMAZONE LOI validates FDI recruitment strategy

• Debt service: strong tax base growth supports municipal bonds

• EB-5 visa program: immigrant investor capital for senior care, hotel, entertainment projects

• STAR Bonds: sales tax increment district = self-funding mechanism for mega-site infrastructure

🤔 Talking to Skeptics ("I'm not convinced")

Lead: "Here's why smart people believe in this. Here's what we're tracking."

• Historical precedent: Wichita grew from 40K (1980) to 390K (today) by attracting aerospace. We're replicating that model at smaller scale.

• AMAZONE is real: Not hypothetical. German company, real job creation, real investment.

• Federal backing: $10M SS4A grant already awarded. EDA, USDA, HUD programs competitive but proven.

• Transparency: Quarterly scorecards. Track everything: population, jobs, investment, projects. Report to public monthly.

• Reversibility: If plan stalls, we didn't "waste" money — infrastructure (roads, broadband, water) benefits everyone regardless.

Common Objections & Responses

These are real concerns. Address them directly, honestly, with data.

"This is too ambitious. Who do you think we are?"

Response (Measured, Confident):

"El Dorado has done ambitious things before. We've built a state park that gets 1M+ visitors annually. We attracted and sustained HF Sinclair (135K bbl/day refinery). We're home to the #2 community college in Kansas. The assets are here.

But you're right to ask. That's why we're phasing this over 15 years, not 5. Year 1 is about laying foundation (water authority, grant applications, broadband design). We're not trying to do everything at once. And we're measuring progress quarterly — if something isn't working, we adjust."

"Where's the money coming from? Are we going to tax ourselves to death?"

Response (Data-Driven):

"Federal/state grants cover 30-40% ($300M+). That's not El Dorado money — it's federal/state investment in rural communities. Private investment covers 60-65% ($600M+). That's developers, businesses, investors betting on El Dorado's future. They put their own capital at risk.

Municipal bonds, if needed, are paid back by tax base growth. A growing city generates more revenue per capita than a stagnant one. Wichita didn't lower taxes when it grew — it grew its tax base so services improved without rate increases.

We're tracking mill levy closely. No major tax increases planned for Phase 1 (2026–2028). Any increases come only if commission votes for bonds (public vote required)."

"We'll lose our small-town character if we grow. I don't want El Dorado to become Wichita."

Response (Emotional + Practical):

"Fair concern. That's not what we're aiming for. Vision 2040 targets 17,600 residents by 2040. That's +5,100 people. We'll still be a small town — just a healthy one.

Small-town character comes from community connection, downtown vitality, and local pride — not population size. A thriving downtown (Vision 2040), active lake (26 venues), strong schools (enrollment growth), and job opportunities actually strengthen small-town identity.

Compare: Smaller Kansas towns without growth plans (8K–10K pop) feel declining. Hollowed-out downtowns, aging population, young people leaving. Vision 2040 is about preventing that. Not becoming Wichita. Becoming a healthy, vital small city."

"I don't trust the mega-site will actually get tenants. This could be a white elephant."

Response (Evidence-Based):

"AMAZONE is already there. Opened December 2025. German company, real jobs, real investment. That's proof of concept for mega-site strategy.

But you're right to be cautious. That's why Phase 1 is foundational: Water Authority (de-risks industrial recruitment), Broadband (enables tech jobs), Transportation (reduces friction for site access). We're not betting everything on mega-site occupancy.

Worst case: Mega-site infrastructure (roads, water, power) benefits El Dorado regardless. Healthcare economy, residential growth, downtown activation, lake projects all work independently of mega-site tenants."

"Why should I believe this will actually happen? City planners always promise big things."

Response (Accountability-Focused):

"You shouldn't take our word for it. That's why we're building quarterly scorecards and public reporting.

Track: How many grant applications submitted? How many awarded? How many jobs created? Population trend? Tax base growth? Mega-site occupancy? Lake venue openings? Housing units built?

Every quarter, public report. If we're not hitting milestones, community will know. Leadership will be accountable. If the plan isn't working, we adjust.

And this isn't just city staff talking. Chamber, El Dorado Inc., Butler CC, SBA Hospital — all staking their reputations on this. That's skin in the game."

"What about environmental impact? Will growth destroy the lake and wildlife?"

Response (Balanced):

"Environmental stewardship is built into Vision 2040. Lake activation is about more public access and amenities, not development that harms ecosystem. El Dorado State Park remains public recreation area (protected by USACE).

EPA smart growth programs (available to us free) specifically promote walkability, green infrastructure, habitat preservation. Downtown infill and residential expansion reduces sprawl pressure.

Walnut River Watershed Collaborative (new regional group) coordinates environmental protection. We're growing smart, not exploiting."

"This benefits businesses and developers more than regular people. What about affordability?"

Response (Equity-Focused):

"Fair critique. Growth can price people out if not managed. That's why Vision 2040 includes specific affordability programs:

• LIHTC 9% rural housing (low-income): 80–100 workforce rental units at 60–80% AMI

• Manufactured home community expansion: Most affordable housing option, critical for working families

• Homeownership ladder: Down-payment assistance, rehab revolving loan, owner-occupied tax abatement

• Local hiring preference: Phase 1 projects require local worker hiring targets (30%+)

Growth that leaves existing residents behind is a failure. Our metrics include affordability, income distribution, wealth-building for existing community."

Communication Tactics & Timeline

1. Launch & Announcement Phase (January–March 2026)

Goal: Introduce Vision 2040, build initial excitement, establish plan credibility

• Press conference: Mayor, City Manager, Chamber president, El Dorado Inc., major employers

• Town hall: Open community meeting. Present plan overview, Q&A, address concerns

• Website launch: eldorado-vision2040.com with interactive plan, briefs, stakeholder directory

• Media coverage: Op-eds in local/regional papers, radio interviews, social media campaign

• Chamber event: Formal dinner presentation to business leaders

Tactics: Earned media (press releases), owned media (website), paid media (limited), earned community (events)

2. Education & Engagement Phase (April–December 2026)

Goal: Deep community understanding, address objections, build grassroots support

• Monthly town halls: Rotating focus (economy, housing, transportation, lake, downtown)

• Stakeholder briefings: Regular updates to key groups (Chamber, schools, churches, civic organizations)

• Social media: Weekly updates on grant applications, project progress, quick wins

• Email newsletter: Bi-weekly Vision 2040 newsletter to 2,000+ subscribers

• Video series: 2–3 minute explainers on mega-site, water authority, health economy, housing

• Community advisory board: Public meetings, feedback loop, resident voice

Tactics: Direct engagement (town halls, briefings), digital (social, email, video), community partnerships

3. Early Wins & Accountability Phase (January 2027–2028)

Goal: Demonstrate progress, build confidence, momentum for Phase 2

• Quarterly scorecards: Public dashboard showing metrics (jobs, population, investment, projects)

• Grant award celebrations: High-visibility events when EDA, USDA, HUD grants awarded

• Project groundbreakings: SS4A projects, broadband, childcare campus, housing development

• Business spotlights: Monthly features on new businesses, job creators, AMAZONE success story updates

• Annual report: Community report card, progress against 2040 vision, adjustments for Phase 2

Tactics: Celebrations (events, press events), transparency (dashboards, public data), storytelling (business spotlights, resident interviews)

4. Momentum & Growth Phase (2029–2034)

Goal: Sustain support through major construction, visible transformation, economic results

• Annual Vision 2040 Summit: Multi-day regional event featuring speakers, networking, project showcases

• Resident success stories: Feature people who got jobs, started businesses, moved to El Dorado because of growth

• Peer city comparisons: Compare El Dorado trajectory to similar-sized Kansas cities (Newton, Hays, Salina) to show impact

• Educational institution partnerships: Butler CC marketing campaigns highlighting growth, job training, career pathways

• Media partnerships: Feature El Dorado in Kansas business publications, national rural development stories

Tactics: Celebratory events, earned media (national stories), peer benchmarking, success metrics visibility

Why Different Stakeholder Groups Should Support Vision 2040

🏢 Business Owners

Workforce expansion (1,600+ jobs) = more customers. Lake activation = new markets. Tax base growth = infrastructure investment without rate increases. AMAZONE precedent = competitive advantage in region.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Families with Children

School enrollment growth = new facilities. Childcare expansion = access (currently desert). Job opportunities = kids can stay in hometown. Parks/lake = recreation. Property values = wealth building.

🏥 Healthcare Workers

SBA Hospital expansion = career advancement, higher pay, new specialties. Healthcare shortage = job security + wage growth. Workforce training pipeline = recruitment advantage for nursing, therapy roles.

🎓 Educators

Student enrollment growth = job security, new hires. Better facilities = more competitive compensation. Butler CC growth = regional draw, state funding. Workforce training = partnerships with businesses.

🌱 Young Adults

Jobs available locally (don't need to move to Wichita). Housing affordability (LIHTC, RHID programs). Entertainment/culture (lake, downtown, arts). Education pathways (Butler CC, apprenticeships).

👴 Seniors / Retirees

Senior care campus = specialized housing. Healthcare expansion = more providers. Cultural/recreational (lake venues, downtown). Property value appreciation = wealth preservation.

🏠 Homeowners

Property value appreciation (cities with growth plans see 3-5% annual growth). Infrastructure investment (roads, water, broadband). Community safety (SS4A program). School quality (enrollment growth).

🛠️ Construction / Trades

Building boom (infrastructure, residential, commercial projects = 15+ years of steady work). Higher wages (labor shortage = better pay). Job security (diversified projects, not cyclical).

Crisis Communication & Rapid Response

Sometimes criticism or setbacks emerge. Have rapid response playbook ready.

If Grant Application Rejected

Message: "Competitive process. We're reapplying. Multiple programs, multiple shots."

• Emphasize portfolio approach (not dependent on single grant)

• Highlight alternative funding (state programs, private investment, bonds)

• Show progress on other fronts (AMAZONE, BEAD, broadband)

• Don't catastrophize; it's normal in federal grant process

If Mega-Site Deal Stalls

Message: "FDI recruitment is long-term play. AMAZONE success validates strategy. Patient capital and good infrastructure win."

• Emphasize foundational work (water authority, broadband, transportation) benefits El Dorado regardless

• Continue active FDI pipeline (multiple companies in conversation stage)

• Highlight AMAZONE as proof (one deal is enough to validate model)

• Other projects (housing, health, lake, downtown) provide growth even without mega-site tenants

If Negative Media Coverage

Message: "Transparent about progress. Accountable for results. Here's what we've delivered so far."

• Provide data (scorecards, metrics) to counter speculation

• Acknowledge legitimate concerns; explain how plan addresses them

• Feature success stories (AMAZONE, grant awards, groundbreakings)

• Engage community (town halls, Q&A, transparency) to build trust

If Population Growth Slower Than Target

Message: "Long-term play. Quality over quantity. Attracting right workforce, building sustainable growth."

• Emphasize 15-year timeline (not expected to hit 2040 targets by 2030)

• Highlight progress (jobs created, projects completed, investment attracted)

• Focus on employment growth (more important than raw population)

• Compare to peer cities (El Dorado still outperforming similar-sized Kansas towns)

Communication Effectiveness Metrics

Reach

  • # Email newsletter subscribers (target: 3,000+)
  • # Social media followers (target: 2,000+ combined)
  • # Town hall attendees (target: 100+ per event)
  • # Website visits/month (target: 5,000+)

Sentiment

  • % Favorable opinion (annual survey)
  • % "Understand plan" (baseline: 30%, target: 70%)
  • % "Support growth" (baseline: 40%, target: 65%)
  • # Social media comments (engagement proxy)

Action

  • % Commission votes in favor of Vision 2040 initiatives (target: 90%+)
  • # Businesses expressing support (target: 100+)
  • # Residents volunteering for committees (target: 50+)
  • # Press mentions/articles (target: 20+/year)
