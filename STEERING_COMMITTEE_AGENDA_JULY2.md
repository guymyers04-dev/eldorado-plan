### 2. FINANCIAL OPPORTUNITY OVERVIEW (2:10 - 2:25, 15 min)
**Speaker:** Finance Director

**Presentation (use slides):**

**Slide 1: The Leakage**
- Butler County population: 70,000
- Annual healthcare spending: ~$120 million
- Exits to Wichita: 40% = $48 million/year
- SBA Hospital capacity: 74 beds (1.06 per 1,000 pop vs. 2.8 national avg)
- Missing: 1 surgical center, 24 behavioral health beds, 8 dentists, 12 primary care physicians

**Slide 2: Phase 1A Financial Model**
- Investment: $55 million
  - Surgical center: $25M (12,000 sq ft ASC + specialty clinics)
  - Behavioral health wing: $12M (12-bed pilot, expandable to 24)
  - Telehealth infrastructure: $3M
  - Dental clinic: $4M
  - Planning/permits/contingency: $11M

- Funding sources:
  - USDA Community Facilities Loan: $18-20M (2.75-4.25%, 35-year term)
  - SBA Hospital commitment: $13M (from operations + reserves)
  - City bonds/local matching: $15-20M (City Council vote needed)
  - Other federal grants: $2-5M (HRSA, SAMHSA, HUD staggered)

**Slide 3: Return on Investment**
- 5-year horizon:
  - 130 new jobs (Phase 1A) = $3.9M payroll
  - $120M annual healthcare leakage reduced to $60M (50% capture)
  - $60M additional annual spending = $4.2M additional tax revenue (7%)
  - Payoff period: 13 years (conservative)
  - Break-even with state/federal grants: 8 years

- 10-year horizon (full plan):
  - 660+ healthcare jobs
  - $200M+ annual spending retained
  - $14M+ annual tax revenue gain
  - Population growth: 12,700 → 18,200 (+43%)

**Key Talking Points:**
- "This is not charity. This is self-funding regional development. USDA loans are designed exactly for this."
- "The biggest risk is doing nothing — we bleed $120M/year to Wichita indefinitely."
- "Federal leverage is 53-63% of total cost. City/hospital bears only 37-42%."

### 3. PHASE 1A STRATEGIC OVERVIEW (2:25 - 2:40, 15 min)
**Speakers:** SBA Hospital CEO + USD 490 Superintendent

**SBA Hospital CEO:**

"Phase 1A has four components, each filling a different gap:

**Component 1: Surgical Center (ASC)**
- Current state: Orthopedic, general surgery, cardiac cases go to Wichita (37-mile drive)
- Solution: 8,000 sq ft ambulatory surgical center attached to SBA Hospital
- Procedures: Same-day surgeries (orthopedics, cardiology, general surgery, GYN)
- Volume: Target 12-15 procedures/day, break-even at 8
- Financial: ASCs run at 40-60% lower cost than inpatient settings
- Recruitment: Partner with Via Christi physician groups (Wichita) for specialty coverage
- Timeline: Design selected this month, construction begins Oct 1, first surgery Q3 2026

**Component 2: Behavioral Health Wing**
- Current state: ZERO psychiatric beds in all of Butler County; mental health cases go to Wichita ER
- Kansas ranks 47th in mental health access; rural overdose rates 35% above state average
- Solution: 12-bed pilot behavioral health + addiction treatment wing (expandable to 24)
- Scope: Inpatient psychiatric + substance abuse treatment + dual-diagnosis care
- Funding: 70% grant-funded through SAMHSA (State Opioid Response) — limited financial risk
- Staffing: Psychiatrist + psychiatric nurses + counselors (recruitment starts August)
- Timeline: Design specs finalized by Aug, permits by Sept, construction begins Oct 1

**Component 3: Specialty Recruitment**
- Target specialties: General surgery, orthopedics, psychiatry, internal medicine
- Compensation package: $80K-$120K (NHSC loan repayment $50K + Kansas SHARP $35K + SBA match $20K)
- Target pool: KU Medical Center + OU Health residencies + Wichita physicians willing to relocate
- Timeline: Recruitment begins July, 3-4 physicians committed by Sept 15

**Timeline Summary:**
- July: Architect selected, physician recruitment begins
- Aug: Design finalized, permits submitted, physician offers made
- Sept: Board approvals, construction documents final, physician commitments
- Oct 1: Groundbreaking ceremony

Board vote needed by July 25 on Phase 1A commitment ($13M local capital)."

**USD 490 Superintendent:**

"The workforce pipeline is critical. Phase 1A creates 130 jobs, but we can't fill them with imported talent. We're building a local supply chain:

**K-12 Health Pathway:**
- Launch: September 2026 (grades 9-12)
- Year 1 (Grades 9-10): Anatomy, physiology, health career exploration
- Year 2 (Grade 11): Medical assistant certification (clinically-relevant skills)
- Year 3 (Grade 12): 200-hour internship at SBA Hospital + dual enrollment at Butler CC
- Outcomes: 15-20 graduates/year ready for BCC health programs or entry-level healthcare jobs
- Budget: $50K/year (funded by grants + SBA Hospital partnership)

**Butler Community College Expansion:**
- Current enrollment: 300 health sciences students
- Target by 2027: 400+ students (+33% growth)
- New programs: Respiratory Therapy, Paramedic, Mental Health Technician, Dental Hygiene
- Instructor hiring: 6-8 FTE (begins July, starts teaching Sept 2026)
- Clinical partnerships: SBA Hospital practicum sites, UMKC dental teaching clinic

**Closed Loop Pipeline:**
USD 490 (grades 9-12) → Butler CC (2-year programs) → SBA Hospital + health employers (local jobs)

This isn't theoretical. We're creating a talent supply chain that feeds local demand."
