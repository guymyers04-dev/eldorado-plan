## KEY INSIGHTS FOR DECISION-MAKERS

### Why Niche Industries Matter
1. **Data centers saturate**: By 2028, every city with water + I-35 access will be recruiting data centers. El Dorado's window closes.
2. **Niche sectors are wide open**: Precision ag tech, battery supply chain, biotech — these are emerging networks. El Dorado can be in the first wave, not the hundredth.
3. **Network effects are real**: AMAZONE (2025 FDI win) validates ag-tech manufacturing. This triggers follow-on companies. Delay by 1 year = miss the network.
4. **Incentive ROI is exceptional**: $1.2M EDA grant + $4M tax abatement = $5.2M city cost. One Precision Ag facility = 50 jobs × $60K = $3M annual payroll, ~$300K/yr city tax revenue. Payback in ~17 years from one facility alone; subsequent facilities accelerate ROI.

### Why This Plan Is Different
- **Specific targets, not generic sectors.** Not "we want manufacturing" but "we want Climate FieldView, Raven Industries, Gro Intelligence" (decision-makers identified, timelines clear).
- **Execution detail.** Not "recruit companies" but "Month 1 research, Month 2 warm intros from KDC, Month 3 site visits, Month 4 LOI negotiation, Month 8 signing, Month 12 groundbreaking."
- **Contingency planning.** If Market A fails, Markets B–E provide upside optionality.
- **Stakeholder alignment.** K-State partnership (academic), SCKEDD (financing), WAMPO (infrastructure), KDC (state support) — all known players, existing relationships, clear value exchange.
