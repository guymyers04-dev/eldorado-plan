## CRITICAL DEPENDENCIES & RISKS

### Critical Success Factors

1. **Staffing Continuity:** Niche Industries Development Officer must stay in role for 2+ years (consistency builds relationships)
2. **State Partnership:** KDC "Framework for Growth" designation must be approved (gates SelectUSA access + warm intros)
3. **Warm Introductions:** SCKEDD, KDC, and Kansas Commerce must actively facilitate decision-maker meetings (cold outreach = 5% conversion; warm intros = 25%+)
4. **Incentive Flexibility:** City Commission must allow customized packages per company (one-size-fits-all fails)
5. **Timeline Discipline:** Stick to Q1→Q2→Q3→Q4 roadmap (delays compound; Q2 outreach miss → Q3 site visits → Q4 LOI = 6-month slip)

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|-----------|
| **Market window closes (data center saturation by 2028)** | Medium | High | Pre-existing LOIs with 2–3 niche facilities provide diversification |
| **First recruit delays negotiation (6+ months)** | Medium | Medium | Maintain steady outreach to 2nd/3rd prospects; use any LOI as proof-of-concept |
| **Agricultural sector slowdown** | Low | Medium | Biotech, aerospace, logistics provide upside if precision ag stalls |
| **Staffing turnover** | Low | High | Document all relationships in CRM; ensure backup owner for each company |
| **Incentive budget exhausted early** | Low | Medium | Pre-prioritize companies (Tier-1 gets first $6–8M; Tier-2 gets remainder) |
| **Regulatory/permitting delays** | Low | Medium | Coordinate with City/County early; ID permitting blockers before LOI signing |
